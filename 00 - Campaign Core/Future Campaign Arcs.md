# Campaign Arcs — Grand Edition

## Overview
This is the complete campaign structure, merging the original Night Train to Sharn arc plans with the Waterdeep: Dragon Heist / Sharn Schema Scandal conversion. Sessions 1-10 form the "Dragon Heist" core. Sessions 9-10 are the Siege of the Dragon's Rest. Session 11+ covers Duur'Sharaat and the expansion into the original epic arcs.

---

## Arc 1 — The Firefly Incident (Session 1)
**Status: COMPLETE.** The party survived the train heist and now carries the Siberys shard — a fragment of the Draconic Prophecy crystallized in dragonshard, warm and humming and impossibly valuable. They arrive in Sharn as nobodies with a kingdom-changing secret.

---

## Arc 2 — Sharn Noir: The Dragon's Rest (Sessions 2-3)
The party settles into Sharn and receives **The Dragon's Rest** — a bridge pub spanning two towers, gifted as compensation (or guilt money) after the Firefly incident.

### Key Events
- Getting the deed from Lady Elira ir'Orien or as a settlement from House Orien's legal fixers
- Discovering the pub is a fixer-upper with its own history (and possibly a ghost, a hidden room, or a rival)
- Meeting the neighbors: a shifter community, a Boromar-operated gambling den, a House Ghallanda rival pub
- **Emmek d'Ghallanda** — a rival restaurateur who wants the Dragon's Rest shut down
- The first faction emissaries arrive: an Aurum agent with a "generous offer," a Deneith marshal offering protection (surveillance), a Professor Nymm sighting
- Rebuilding the pub: hiring staff, dealing with the Sharn Watch, the guilds, and the crime syndicates who expect their cut

### DM Notes
- Let the pub feel like *home* — the players should care about it before the world tries to take it away
- Every NPC the party hires could be a spy for a different faction
- The pub's bridge location means there are two entrances (one in each tower), making it a nightmare to defend and a dream to escape from

## Arc 3 — The Factions Close In (Sessions 4-5)
The explosion. Someone has decided to stop being subtle.

### The Inciting Incident
A fireball (or arcane explosion, or alchemical blast) tears through a Sharn street, killing civilians and sending a message: the race for the shard has gone public. A construct or operative carrying information about Duur'Sharaat is killed in the blast. The party investigates because:

1. It happened near the Dragon's Rest
2. One of the victims was carrying a note with the party's names
3. The Sharn Watch is asking questions — and the Sharn Inquisitive is printing answers
4. All 8+ factions suddenly accelerate their plans

### Key Events
- The crime scene investigation (see Session 4)
- Meeting the faction patrons formally — each offers a different deal
- The party must choose who to trust (and who to use)
- The Nimblewright / construct investigation leads to the seasonal villain chain
- The shard begins reacting more strongly — visions, whispers, Prophecy fragments

## Arc 4 — The Hunt: Seasonal Villain Chain (Sessions 6-8)
One syndicate rises as the main antagonist. The party chases the artifact trail through Sharn.

### Choose Your Villain
Pick ONE syndicate as the primary antagonist. The others remain active but secondary.

| Villain | Leader | Their Plan for the Schema | Finale Location |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Boromar Clan** | The Rake (halfling crime lord, Boromar leadership) | Sell it to the highest bidder — Karrnath, Droaam, or a rogue House | King of Fire nightclub (Firelight) |
| **Daask** | Niho Koi (oni mage, Droaam's agent) | Arm Droaam's monster legions for continental conquest | The Storm Spire construct beneath the Cogs |
| **House Tarkanan** | Oleander Nightbloom (drow aberrant-mark assassin) | Use the schema to destroy the dragonmarked houses — every last one | Nightshade tavern, Shae Lias |
| **The Tyrants** | The Faceless Council (doppelganger/changeling leaders) | Auction the schema to all parties simultaneously — profit from the chaos | Diamond Theater (Lower Menthis) |

### Seasonal Encounter Chains
Each season provides a different flavor and encounter path. The conversion guide provides detailed chains:

- **Spring (Boromar):** Heavy rain, Bazaar chase, Hrabbaz the enforcer, Sundry's pawnshop, skycoach battle, Cloud Dragon restaurant heist
- **Summer (Tyrants):** Heat wave, shifter informants, raging chimera, Daca's Watch, Dolessen the Smoking Flute, Diamond Theater infiltration
- **Autumn (House Tarkanan):** Cool winds, Oleander's poisoned trail, Shae Lias underdark connections, the Broken Mirror, Gorgon's Run
- **Winter (Daask):** Heavy snow, Cogs descent, warforged prisoner, Pool of Onatar's Tears, House Jorasco chase, skycoach battle, Aurora Gallery auction

### The Encounter Chain Structure
Regardless of season, the party follows the same structure:
1. **The Lead** — Find the person who last handled the artifact
2. **The Chase** — Track the artifact through Sharn's wards
3. **The Twist** — Another faction intercepts; the trail goes sideways
4. **The Setpiece** — A major combat or heist in a unique Sharn location
5. **The Revelation** — The artifact (when attuned) reveals Duur'Sharaat's entrance

## Arc 5 — The Siege of the Dragon's Rest (Sessions 9-10)
The party has both the Key and the shard. Every faction in Sharn knows it. The syndicates form a desperate, unstable coalition to take the Dragon's Rest by force. The party must hold their home, call in every favor earned across Sessions 2-8, and exploit the fault lines between factions who hate each other more than they want the prize. This is the penultimate battle of Act 1.

### The Coalition
All six major factions surround Bridge 47: Boromar Clan, Daask, House Tarkanan, The Tyrants, The Aurum, and House Deneith. They are not allies — they are rivals forced into cooperation by the party's possession of both artifacts. The coalition is held together by mutual fear and mutual suspicion. The party's goal is to find the cracks and split it apart.

### The Siege Structure
- **Phase 0 — Calling in Favors:** The party rallies NPC allies from throughout the campaign. Each ally provides a specific mechanical benefit.
- **Phase 1 — The Coalition Gathers:** Factions arrive, posture, and argue. The party's first opportunity to plant seeds of betrayal.
- **Phase 2 — The Assault:** 3-5 combat waves with lulls between them. Between waves, the party triggers fault lines to fracture the coalition.
- **Phase 3 — Fracture Lines:** The coalition collapses. Factions turn on each other in the street. The party watches their enemies destroy themselves.
- **Phase 4 — Victory and Descent:** The siege ends. The sealed Dhakaani door in the cellar opens. The party descends into Duur'Sharaat.

### Key Tensions to Exploit
- Boromar vs. Daask: Archrivals for Sharn's underworld
- Tarkanan vs. The Dragonmarked: Oleander despises Deneith and the Aurum
- The Tyrants vs. Everyone: Playing all sides, loyal to none
- The Aurum vs. The Syndicates: Old money looking down on street criminals
- Deneith vs. Its Own Orders: The marshals didn't sign up to fight alongside monsters

### Outcomes
- The party's faction standing permanently shifts based on who they fought, who they turned, and who they spared
- The Dragon's Rest is damaged but standing — a symbol of resistance in Sharn's underworld
- The party's reputation: "They held against six factions"
- The open Dhakaani door in the cellar becomes a permanent strategic asset

## Arc 5.5 — Duur'Sharaat: The Lost Workshop (Session 11+)
The vault. With the siege won and no external pressure, the party descends through the cellar door into the Dhakaani workshop buried beneath Old Sharn since the War of the Mark.

### The Descent
The shard opens the way. The party navigates:
- **The Approach:** A spiral stairway through ancient stone, flanked by Dhakaani constructs that recognize the Forge-Warden's presence in the shard
- **The Workshop:** A Dhakaani artifice-tomb — guardian constructs, a rune-locked vault, an echo chamber of the artificers' final moments, and the central forge
- **The Vault:** The Storm Spire Schema chamber. The schema embeds itself into the mind of whoever claims it

### No External Pressure
Unlike the original design, the party enters Duur'Sharaat on their own terms. The siege was the test. The dungeon is the reward. They earned the right to explore without a villain chasing them — though the workshop's own defenses are threat enough.

### Outcomes
- **If the party claims the schema:** Merrix d'Cannith pays 500,000 gp. The party is suddenly rich, famous, and *terrifyingly* important. Every faction now wants them dead or owned.
## Arc 6 — The Next War (Sessions 12-17)
With the schema (or without it), the party learns that Dragon Heist was the appetizer. The main course is continental.

### Key Revelations
- House Cannith has been building warforged that don't sleep, don't question, and don't stop
- Karrnath is reactivating undead legions — the Emerald Claw's Keeper Malachi is directing them
- The Twelve is consolidating power across all dragonmarked houses — Cipher's identity inches closer
- The Aurum is funding *both* sides of a coming conflict
- The Lord of Blades has been collecting schemas — and the party just led him to the biggest one

### Key Events
- A massacre blamed on "rogue warforged" — actually a Cannith test gone wrong
- The party is framed for a crime that makes them enemies of a dragonmarked house
- A summit of powers in Skyway where the shard (or schema) is demanded as tribute
- The choice: expose the conspiracy or use it for leverage

## Arc 7 — Ancient Secrets (Sessions 18-21)
The shard connects to something deeper than Dhakaani workshops. The party descends further than anyone has gone — into the Depths, then beyond.

### Key Revelations
- The Dhakaani were not destroyed by the daelkyr — they sealed *themselves* to prevent something worse
- The Storm Spire Schema is a *copy* of a far older design — something from the Age of Demons
- The shard is one of seven Keys that form a sentence in the Draconic Prophecy
- The Mourning was not an accident — it was a *word* in the Prophecy spoken prematurely
- The next word is coming — and the party now holds one of the keys to speak or silence it

### Key Events
- Expedition to the Cogs, then deeper — Khyber's Gate, the Depths
- Contact with the Keeper of the Cogs — who reveals they are a fallen dragon, bound to Sharn
- The Lord of Blades hunts the party directly, not through agents
- A conversation with an actual dragon of the Chamber — terrifying, enlightening, and binding

## Arc 8 — Prophecy (Sessions 22-25)
The endgame. The Draconic Prophecy is in motion, and the party stands at its pivot point.

### The Final Choice
- **Speak the next word:** The Prophecy advances. The Mourning's true cause is revealed. The peace shatters — but maybe that's what prevents a worse war.
- **Silence it:** The shard is destroyed, the schema sealed, the Prophecy changed. The dragons will not forgive this — but Khorvaire gets another generation of peace.
- **Rewrite it:** The party discovers that the Prophecy is not fixed — it is a conversation. If they are bold enough, they can add their *own* word.
- **Become it:** The party learns they are not just reading the Prophecy — they *are* the Prophecy. Their choices have been part of it all along.

### Possible Endings
- **Heroes of Sharn:** The party claims the schema, uses the wealth to build something lasting, and becomes a power in the city
- **The New Aurum:** The party keeps the schema and the shard, becoming the most dangerous private faction in Khorvaire
- **The Dragons' Agents:** The party allies with the Chamber, becoming agents of draconic will
- **The Mourning's End:** The party uses the Storm Spire to undo the Mourning — or discovers it can't be undone, only replicated
- **A New War:** The party's choices ignite the Next War — and they must decide which side they're on
