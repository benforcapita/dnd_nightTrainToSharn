# Major Mysteries — Grand Edition

## 1. The Siberys Shard — Heart of Duur'Sharaat

**What the party knows:**
- A fragment of Siberys dragonshard was hidden inside a Dhakaani statue aboard The Firefly
- Raiders wanted it. Now the party has it
- It hums. It's warm. It reacts to dragonmarks and the city of Sharn

**What is true (to be revealed over the campaign):**
- The shard is not a key — it is a power source. The Dhakaani called it the *Heart of Duur'Sharaat*, the living energy core that powered their greatest workshop
- The actual Key to Duur'Sharaat is a separate artifact, hidden somewhere in the city of Sharn — its location encoded in Dhakaani ruins, old maps, and faction intelligence
- The Key cannot be activated without the shard. It is a lock mechanism; the shard is the current that turns it
- The shard contains fragments of the Draconic Prophecy — crystalline "words" that hum at the frequency of the Mourning
- The shard is alive, in a sense — it carries the consciousness-imprint of the Forge-Warden who powered it with their own life force
- The Key is one of a kind. Whoever holds the Key holds the door; whoever holds the shard decides whether it opens

**Revealed in:**
- Session 1: The party acquires the shard
- Session 2: First dream of the Forge-Warden
- Session 4: Shard reacts to the explosion; the party learns it is a power source, not a key
- Sessions 5-8: The race for the Key through Sharn — the party follows the trail while factions close in
- Session 8-9: The Key is found (by the party or a rival faction)
- Sessions 9-10: The Siege of the Dragon's Rest — all factions converge on the party's pub
- Session 11+: The shard powers the Key; Duur'Sharaat opens
- Arc 7-8: The true nature of the shard, the Key, and the Prophecy is revealed

## 2. Duur'Sharaat — The Lost Workshop

**What the party knows:**
- The shard shows visions of a Dhakaani workshop
- Something valuable is sealed beneath Sharn
- Every faction wants it

**What is true:**
- Duur'Sharaat contains the Storm Spire Schema — a blueprint for a device that can replicate or counter the Mourning
- It was sealed during the War of the Mark by the last Forge-Warden, who gave their life to power the shard and then hid the Key separately — ensuring no single person could ever open the vault alone
- Merrix d'Cannith will pay 500,000 gp for the schema
- The Daughters of Sora Kell (Daask) want it to arm Droaam
- The Boromar Clan wants it to sell to the highest bidder
- House Tarkanan wants it to destroy the dragonmarked houses
- The Tyrants want to auction it to everyone simultaneously
- The Lord of Blades wants to build a warforged army that never stops
- The Emerald Claw wants to resurrect Karrnath's undead legions
- Most factions are racing for the Key, unaware that the shard is required to use it. The party's possession of the shard is their hidden leverage — and their hidden danger

**Revealed in:**
- Sessions 4-8: The artifact trail leads toward the Key's location
- Session 8-9: The Key is acquired — and the factions realize the party has the power source
- Sessions 9-10: The Siege of the Dragon's Rest — all factions converge on the party's pub
- Session 11+: The dungeon itself — Duur'Sharaat, the lost workshop

## 3. Who Tipped Off Captain Ash?

**What the party knows:**
- Captain Ash had intelligence about The Firefly's secret cargo
- Someone told him about the statue and the shard
- That someone is still out there

**What is true (choose based on main villain):**
- **The Aurum:** Lord Vex tipped off Ash, hoping the chaos would let the Aurum acquire the shard during the confusion. Ash was a pawn.
- **The Twelve:** Cipher wanted the shard removed from House Orien control — a rival house's embarrassment is The Twelve's opportunity
- **House Tarkanan:** An operative learned about the transport and fed the information to Ash, hoping the Raiders would destroy the statue (and the shard with it)
- **Daask:** Niho Koi learned about the transport through Droaam intelligence and fed it to Ash, expecting the Raiders to do the dirty work before Daask swept in
- **A rogue Orien operative:** Someone inside House Orien is working against Lady Elira. They arranged the "leak" to damage her career.

**Revealed in:**
- Sessions 5-8: Investigation reveals who tipped Ash
- The revelation should tie into the main villain's plot

## 4. The Firefly Cover-Up

**What the party knows:**
- House Orien is lying about the Firefly incident
- The shard officially "never existed"
- Professor Nymm vanished

**What is true:**
- House Orien was transporting the statue as a favor to The Twelve
- The Twelve wanted the statue studied at Morgrave University — or somewhere less public
- Professor Nymm was The Twelve's agent (or the Aurum's, or working independently)
- Lady Elira may not have known what she was transporting — or she knew everything
- Nymm vanished because he knows too much — and someone is hunting him

**Revealed in:**
- Sessions 3-6: The party encounters people who know about the cover-up
- Sessions 7-8: Nymm resurfaces — as ally, victim, or villain
- Arc 6-7: The full truth about The Twelve's involvement

## 5. The Dhakaani Connection

**What the party knows:**
- The statue was Dhakaani
- The shard was hidden inside it
- The Dhakaani apparently sealed something beneath Sharn

**What is true:**
- The Dhakaani empire was not destroyed by the daelkyr — they sealed *themselves* to prevent something worse
- The Storm Spire Schema was created as a defensive weapon against daelkyr flesh-warping
- The Dhakaani artificers realized the schema could be inverted — used to *create* Mourning-like effects rather than counter them
- They sealed the schema rather than destroy it — because they believed future generations might need it
- The Forge-Warden who created the Key and the shard knew about the Draconic Prophecy
- They separated the Key from its power source deliberately — two halves of a weapon too dangerous for anyone to wield alone
- They were actively working to *change* the Prophecy — to add a word that would prevent the Mourning

**Revealed in:**
- Session 11+: Duur'Sharaat reveals the historical record
- Arc 7: The deeper truth about Dhakaani intentions
- Arc 8: The Prophecy connection becomes the campaign's final mystery

## 6. The Prophecy Fragment

**What the party knows:**
- The shard hums at a frequency associated with the Mourning
- Scholars whisper about Prophecy fragments connected to Cyre's destruction
- The shard may contain a crystallized memory of what caused the Mourning

**What is true:**
- The Mourning was not an accident — it was a *word* in the Draconic Prophecy spoken prematurely
- The shard contains multiple Prophecy fragments — echoes of words already spoken and hints of words yet to come
- The fragments are not a sequence to be assembled; they are a resonant chorus. When the shard powers the Key, the fragments will sing — and the Prophecy will advance one way or another
- The dragons of the Chamber have been tracking these fragments for centuries
- The Lord of Blades believes the sentence, when completed, will awaken all warforged into a single consciousness
- The Emerald Claw believes it will resurrect Karrnath's dead
- No one — not even the dragons — knows what the complete sentence actually means

**Revealed in:**
- Arc 7: The dragon of the Chamber explains the Prophecy
- Arc 8: The party learns the sentence — and must decide whether to speak it

## 7. Cipher's Identity

**What the party knows:**
- Cipher is an anonymous operative within The Twelve
- Cipher authorized the statue transport
- Cipher is watching the party

**What is true (choose one):**
- **Cipher is Professor Nymm.** The absent-minded professor is The Twelve's most dangerous operative. His "research" on the train was surveillance. His "disappearance" was extraction.
- **Cipher is Lady Elira ir'Orien.** The idealistic heir is playing a very long game. She arranged the Firefly incident to expose corruption within House Orien — and advance her own position.
- **Cipher is multiple people.** The Twelve uses "Cipher" as a title, not a name. Every Cipher the party meets is a different person — and none of them know who the others are.
- **Cipher is a warforged.** A construct designed by The Twelve for surveillance and manipulation. It has no identity because it was never given one.
- **Cipher is the shard itself.** The Forge-Warden's consciousness, speaking through the crystal, manipulating events to bring the Key and the shard together.

**Revealed in:**
- Arc 6-7: Cipher's identity is discovered
- The revelation should be a campaign-defining moment
