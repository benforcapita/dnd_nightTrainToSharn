# Scene 5 — Train Combat

## Scene Purpose
- Dynamic combat on a moving train
- [[Environmental Hazards]] keep the fight unpredictable
- Players can choose sides or play both ends against the middle
- Introduce the train as a character — alive, dangerous, fragile

## Battlefield: The Moving Train
The fight sprawls across multiple cars. Use the [[Environmental Hazards]] table at initiative count 20 each round.

## Combatants

### [[Captain Ash]]'s Raiders (4 remaining)
- **2 Train Raiders** (see [[Train Raider Statblocks]]) — engaging Deneith guards
- **1 Saboteur** — attempting to breach the cargo car seal
- **[[Captain Ash]]** — commanding from the junction between cars

### [[House Deneith]] Security (3 guards + [[Marshal Serik ir'Deneith|Serik] ir'Deneith|Serik]])
- **2 Deneith Guards** — standard soldiers, outmatched but disciplined
- **1 Elite Guard** — protecting Lady [[Lady Elira ir'Orien|Elira] ir'Orien|Elira]]'s cabin
- **[[Marshal Serik ir'Deneith|Serik] ir'Deneith|Serik]] ir'Deneith]]** — defending the cargo car with lethal intent

### Neutral/Civilians
- **[[Professor Nymm]]** — hiding under a table, taking frantic notes
- **Passengers** — cowering, screaming, some trying to flee to the observation car
- **The Players** — caught in the middle

## Tactical Options

### For the Players
| Approach | Risk | Reward |
|----------|------|--------|
| Help [[Captain Ash|Ash]] open the seal | Betray [[House Orien]]; gain [[Captain Ash|Ash]]'s respect | Learn what's in the crate |
| Help [[Marshal Serik ir'Deneith|Serik] ir'Deneith|Serik]] repel [[The Raiders]] | Gain House favor; become pawns | Safety, but no answers |
| Play both sides | Dangerous; could anger everyone | Maximum information |
| Protect civilians | Morally right; no material gain | XP; possible allies later |
| Investigate [[Lady Elira ir'Orien|Elira] ir'Orien|Elira]]'s cabin | Off the main fight; reveals secrets | Plot advancement |

## Key Moments

### Round 2-3: Lightning Surge
The conductor stones flare. Arcs of blue-white energy leap between metal surfaces.
- Anyone touching metal takes `1d6 lightning damage`
- The lights die completely — dim emergency arcane lamps only
- Combatants must make **DC 12 Dexterity saves** or drop metal weapons

### Round 4: The Roof Breach
A raider on the roof is struck by debris and crashes through the dining car ceiling. Now there's a hole in the roof — and wind screaming through.
- **New hazard:** Open Roof Gust (see [[Environmental Hazards]])
- Anyone within 10 feet of the hole risks being pulled out

### Round 5-6: [[Captain Ash|Ash]] vs. [[Marshal Serik ir'Deneith|Serik] ir'Deneith|Serik]]
If both are still standing, they clash at the cargo car junction.

## Environmental Hazard: Sudden Turn
At initiative count 20, the train takes a sharp curve:
- **DC 13 Dexterity save** or fall prone
- Prone creatures slide `1d4 x 5 feet` toward the outer wall
- Failed save by 5+ = fall and take `1d6 bludgeoning damage`

## Resolution Paths
- **If [[Captain Ash|Ash]] wins:** He opens the crate, sees [[The Statue]], is interrupted by players or surviving Deneith forces before he can extract it.
- **If [[Marshal Serik ir'Deneith|Serik] ir'Deneith|Serik]] wins:** He drives off [[The Raiders]] but the seal is already damaged — [[The Statue]]'s hidden compartment cracks open, revealing a faint golden glow.
- **If players mediate:** A tense standoff. Both sides want [[The Statue]]. Both sides distrust each other. The players become the deciding factor.
