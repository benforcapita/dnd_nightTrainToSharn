# Scene 4 — First View of the Pub

### Read Aloud
> *"The Dragon's Rest is exactly what you expected and nothing you hoped for.
> 
> The sign hangs by one chain. The windows are opaque with grime. A drunk snorer occupies the doorstep — he waves without opening his eyes as you step over him.
> 
> Inside: a long taproom that spans the bridge, windows on both sides, the drop visible between the floorboards. A hearth big enough to roast an owlbear. Tables scarred with knife-fight history. A bar that's seen better centuries.
> 
> The air smells of old beer, older regret, and just beneath it — stone polish. Someone was here recently. Cleaning.
> 
> A cold spot passes through the room. The temperature drops ten degrees. For a moment, you hear bells that aren't ringing.
> 
> Then it passes. The drunk on the doorstep yawns. 'She's got good bones,' he mutters. 'And a ghost. But the ghost don't bite.'"*

### Exploration
Let the party explore:
- **Taproom:** Dirty but solid. The hearth works. Someone left a half-full bottle of decent whiskey on the bar.
- **Kitchen:** A disaster. Rats, spoiled food, and what appears to be an abandoned alchemy setup. The previous owner was experimenting with something.
- **Upper rooms:** Four bedrooms, dusty but habitable. One has a bloodstain and a false wall.
- **Cellar:** Cold stone, empty ale barrels, a trapdoor to nothing, and a sealed Dhakaani door no one mentions.
- **Belfry:** Pigeons, rusted bells, bone fragments. A 360-degree view of Sharn. The cold spot is strongest here.

### The Ghost Encounter
At some point during exploration, **The Dragons Rest|Torvin Stonehands** manifests. Not as a threat — as a presence. He appears as:
- A cold hand on a shoulder
- A phantom bell tone that only one character hears
- A brief apparition: a dwarf in work clothes, staring at the broken bells with infinite sadness
- Words whispered in a language no one speaks, but everyone understands: *"Ring them again. Please."*
