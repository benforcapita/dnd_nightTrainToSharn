# Scene 5 — Exploring Bridge 47

## Scene Goal
The party tours their new home properly. They learn the bridge's quirks, meet the neighbors, discover the ghost, and understand why this place matters. This scene grounds the campaign — the pub should feel like theirs by the time it ends.

## The Walkthrough

### Ground Floor: The Taproom
After the initial shock of seeing the place, give the party time to poke around. Let them notice:

- **The bar itself** is dwarven stonework — older than the building around it. A DC 12 Intelligence (History) check reveals it's pre-Galifar construction, possibly Dhakaani foundation work repurposed.
- **The hearth chimney** rises straight through three floors. The stones are warm despite no fire. The warmth doesn't feel like fire. It feels like... presence.
- **South windows** look out over the bridge span and the drop to the lower wards. On a clear day you can see the Dagger River glinting below. Tonight, it's just darkness and distant lanterns.
- **North windows** face the Bazaar market square. Merchants are packing up for the evening. A halfling across the way is watching the pub a little too intently (Boromar lookout — doesn't approach yet).

### Upper Floor: The Bridge Rooms
Four rooms. Each tells a story:

| Room | Discovery | Significance |
|---|---|---|
| **Room 1 — The Officer's Room** | A Cyran military coat still hangs in the wardrobe. Medal ribbons. A letter that was never sent, addressed to someone in Metrol. It reads: *"I have found a place. It is not home. But it is safe. Come find me."* | The previous-previous owner was a Cyran officer who never reunited with their family. The pub has always been a refuge for the broken. |
| **Room 2 — The Bloodstain** | A dark stain on the floorboards that won't scrub out. A DC 14 Investigation reveals it's not blood — it's ink. The same ink used in House Orien manifests. Someone was writing something here. Someone else stopped them. | A minor mystery. House Orien records were being copied here. By whom? For whom? |
| **Room 3 — The False Wall** | The wall between Room 3 and the bridge maintenance crawlspace is thin plaster over a wooden frame. The crawlspace leads to a narrow walkway that runs the length of the bridge's interior. It smells of old stone, rat droppings, and... something else. Something arcane. | Smuggling route. Escape route. Ambush route. The party can use this — but so can anyone who knows about it. |
| **Room 4 — The Artist's Room** | Faded murals cover the walls: dragons in flight, Sharn's towers at dawn, a woman's face that repeats in every painting. The signature: "T.S." — Torvin Stonehands' daughter. She painted these 50 years ago. She disappeared the same night Torvin died. | Emotional anchor. Torvin's tragedy isn't just his death — it's the family he lost. |

### The Belfry
Climb the narrow spiral staircase to the turret:

**Read Aloud:**
> *"The belfry is a small stone room open to the sky. Three brass bells hang from iron beams — silent, rusted, jammed with what looks like old bone fragments caught between bell and clapper. Pigeons have claimed the rafters. Their cooing echoes off the stone.
>
> The view steals your breath. From here you can see the Bazaar sprawling west, Firelight glittering east, towers climbing into the clouds above and dropping into darkness below. Skycoaches drift between the spires like fish in a vertical sea.
>
> A dwarf-shaped impression is pressed into the stone ledge — as if someone fell onto it from a great height and the stone itself remembers."*

**Restoring the Bells:** Takes 4 hours/day for one week with tinker's tools. Once restored, the bells can be rung as an alarm — everyone on Bridge 47 and the surrounding spans will hear it.

### The Under-Bridge Cellar
Down the stone stairs into the bridge's belly:

**Read Aloud:**
> *"The cellar is colder than it should be. Stone arches support the bridge above you; below, only ancient masonry and 200 feet of empty air before the Depths. Ale barrels line one wall — most are empty, a few still sealed with wax that crumbles at a touch.
>
> A trapdoor in the floor opens to nothing — just the wind and the drop. Iron rungs set into the bridge's underside suggest a rope ladder was once used here. Emergency exit? Smuggling dock? Execution platform?
>
> And at the far end of the cellar, where the stonework changes from dwarven to something older, something deeper — a sealed door. No handle. No hinges. Just a smooth slab of dark stone marked with runes that predate human civilization."*

**The Sealed Door:** DC 18 Intelligence (Arcana or History) to recognize the runes as Dhakaani — Old Goblin, the language of the empire that ruled Khorvaire before humans existed. The runes roughly translate to: *"What is forged below stays below. The Warden watches."* The shard hums audibly when brought near the door. Do NOT open this door yet — it's a Season 5+ reveal. For now, it's pure atmosphere and a promise of things to come.

## The Neighbors

As the party explores the bridge and the pub's immediate surroundings, introduce the neighborhood. These are low-stakes encounters — conversations, not conflicts.

### Riddick the Launderer
**Location:** A stall built into the bridge's underside, accessible by a rickety staircase.

Riddick is a middle-aged human with permanently damp hands and an encyclopedic knowledge of the neighborhood's dirty laundry — literally and figuratively. He washes clothes for half the Bazaar, including Boromar enforcers who pay in silver and never ask for change.

**What He Offers:**
- Gossip: "That halfling across the way — Emmek? He's had his eye on this place since before the last owner drank himself to death. Watch your back."
- A regular laundry discount if the party sends customers his way
- "I find things in pockets. Sometimes interesting things. If you're looking for something, I might have seen it."

**Personality:** Cheerful, indiscreet, genuinely friendly. He's the closest thing to a welcome wagon. He also never forgets a face or a debt.

### The Shifter Community
**Location:** A cluster of apartments on the Firelight side of the bridge.

A small community of shifters lives in the tenement directly across from the Dragon's Rest's Firelight entrance. They're wary — shifters are second-class citizens in Sharn, and new neighbors usually mean trouble.

**Key Figures:**
- **Old Mera:** The community's grandmother-figure. She's missing three fingers on her left hand (a war injury she doesn't discuss). She greets the party with: *"New faces on the bridge. You the ones who own the pub now? Good. The last one was a drunk. Are you drunks?"*
- **Kel:** A young shifter (early 20s) who runs errands for the community. Quick, quiet, fiercely protective of his people. If the party shows any kindness to the shifters, Kel becomes a loyal ally — he'll watch the pub when they're away, run messages, and eventually warn them about Tyrant surveillance (because Leara is watching through him whether he knows it or not).
- **The Watcher:** A shifter named Vara who doesn't live in the tenement but visits frequently. She's Leara's agent. She'll be friendly, helpful, and will report everything the party says and does to the Tyrants. The party won't know this until Session 3.

**What They Offer:**
- If treated with respect: information about the neighborhood, warnings about Daask movements nearby, a network of eyes on the bridge
- If dismissed or threatened: cold silence, and Leara gets a very negative first report

### Watch Sergeant Kael
**Location:** The Bazaar watchpost, a 5-minute walk from the bridge.

The party meets Kael when he stops by the pub on his evening rounds. He's a heavyset human with tired eyes and a badge he polishes obsessively.

**Read Aloud:**
> *"A Watch sergeant leans in the doorway, one hand resting on his truncheon. He's not threatening — he looks more tired than dangerous. But his eyes are sharp, and they take in everything: the broken sign, the dusty bar, the four of you.
>
> 'New owners, then. About time. The last one stopped paying for his drinks, which I took as a personal insult.' He steps inside, uninvited but unhurried. 'Name's Kael. I walk this beat. Which means I'm the one who gets called when something goes wrong on Bridge 47. And something always goes wrong on Bridge 47.'"*

**What He Offers:**
- Protection (of a sort): He'll respond to calls from the Dragon's Rest faster than any other establishment on his beat — as long as the drinks are free
- Information: He knows which gangs are active nearby, which inspectors are bribable, and which crimes the Watch actually cares about
- A warning: "The bridge has two ends. Bazaar Watch on one side, Firelight Watch on the other. We don't talk to each other much. If you're smart, you'll keep it that way."

**Personality:** Corrupt but not evil. He genuinely wants to keep people safe — he just also wants to get paid for it. He's a recurring NPC who'll become more important as the campaign escalates.

## The Ghost: Torvin Stonehands

The party should encounter Torvin by the end of this exploration. Don't force it — let it be a natural moment of quiet.

**When It Happens:** When a character is alone (or nearly alone) in the taproom, or when the party settles in for the evening, or when someone touches the bell rope in the belfry.

**The Manifestation:**
- The temperature drops sharply
- A faint sound of bells — not the rusted bells in the belfry, but clear, resonant tones
- A translucent dwarf appears near the hearth, or by the bar, or standing at the south window staring out at the city
- He doesn't speak at first. He looks at the party with an expression that's equal parts hope and grief

**If Approached with Kindness:**
> *Torvin's voice sounds like stones grinding underwater. 'You came. Someone finally came.' He looks around the pub, at the dust, the neglect, the ruin. 'She was beautiful once. My daughter painted the rooms. My wife poured the first ale. We built this place when the bridge was still young.'*

**What Torvin Reveals:**
- He was the original owner, 50 years ago. A dwarf stonewright who retired from the Mror Holds and built the Dragon's Rest with his own hands.
- His daughter Tessa painted the murals in Room 4.
- One night, thieves broke in through the under-bridge trapdoor. Torvin confronted them. They threw him from the belfry.
- His wife took Tessa and fled. He doesn't know where they went. He's been waiting ever since.
- "The bells. I used to ring them every evening. The whole bridge would stop to listen. If you could make them sing again... I would be grateful."

**The Deal:** If the party restores the bells, Torvin becomes a **helpful spirit**. He can:
- Warn of intruders (the temperature drops when someone hostile enters)
- Once per session, manifest enough to shove an enemy (disadvantage on one attack roll, or a shove that pushes someone toward a window)
- Guide the party to things he's noticed in his decades of watching (hidden caches, forgotten doors, secrets only a ghost would know)
- On one critical occasion later in the campaign, do something dramatic — DM's choice, but it should feel earned

**If the Party Tries to Exorcise Him:** Torvin *begs* to stay. This is his home. The choice is a moral test — and if they go through with it, the pub feels colder forever.

## The Two-Ward Trick

At some point during the exploration, a character with decent passive Perception or Investigation notices something crucial:

> *"You realize it as you walk from the Bazaar taproom to the Firelight entrance: this pub sits exactly on the ward line. The Bazaar door opens into Middle Dura. The Firelight door opens into Lower Menthis. The bridge itself is the border. And the two Watch precincts on either side? They don't coordinate. They barely acknowledge each other exists."*

This is the pub's secret weapon — and its vulnerability. The party can:
- Slip out one door when trouble comes in the other
- Move cargo that "never entered" either ward
- Play the two Watch sergeants against each other
- But also: be attacked from either direction, and no Watch patrol can cover both sides at once

Let the players discover this themselves. It's more satisfying that way.

## Closing the Scene

By the end of the exploration, the party should feel like Bridge 47 is a real place with real history. They've met neighbors, discovered secrets, and — if they were kind — made a ghost into a friend. The pub isn't just a building anymore. It's theirs.

And now, having fallen in love with it, they're about to find out how much it's going to cost.
