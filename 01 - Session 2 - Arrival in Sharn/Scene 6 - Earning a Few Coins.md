# Scene 6 — Earning a Few Coins

## Scene Goal
Before the financial reality hits, give the party a chance to explore the bridge and immediate neighborhood through 1-2 small, local jobs. These should feel like natural neighborhood interactions — not the full-blown questing of Session 3. Payout is modest (30-50 gp); the real reward is deeper connection to Bridge 47 and its people.

## Mini-Quest: The Bridge Rats

**Source:** Riddick the launderer mentions it while the party chats with him. "There's something in the crawlspace under the bridge. Bigger than rats. Meaner than rats. It's been chewing through my laundry lines and — I swear by the Host — it *growled* at me last night."

**The Job:** Crawl through the bridge's interior maintenance walkway (accessed via the false wall in Room 3), find whatever's nesting there, and deal with it.

**The Walkway:**
> *"The maintenance corridor runs the length of Bridge 47 — a narrow stone passage barely wide enough for one person at a time. Dust chokes the air. Spiderwebs drape the ceiling like funeral veils. Every few yards, a grate in the floor offers a vertiginous view of the Depths, 200 feet below.
>
> Something scurries ahead of you. Something bigger than a spider."*

**The Threat:** A **bridge quasit** — a minor demon that slipped through a planar leak decades ago and has been living in the bridge's guts ever since. It's not powerful (CR 1, quasit stats), but it's clever and spiteful. It's been chewing through lines, stealing shiny objects, and leaving foul-smelling "gifts" in inconvenient places.

**Combat Note:** The quasit doesn't fight to the death. At half HP, it tries to flee through a grate. If cornered, it offers information: "The big door downstairs — the stone one — your shiny rock likes it! I've seen it glow! Let me go and I'll tell you more!" The quasit knows the sealed cellar door sometimes hums at midnight.

**Payout:** 
- 35 gp from Riddick (he's grateful, and he spreads the word about "the new pub owners who actually help people")
- A trinket the quasit stole: roll on the Trinkets table, or give them a **small obsidian figurine of a goblin** (Dhakaani craftsmanship — worth 25 gp to a collector, or it might react to the shard later)
- Riddick's loyalty: he'll now actively watch for trouble around the bridge and report to the party

**Faction Hook:** The quasit mentions seeing "a shifter who doesn't smell like the others" poking around the pub at odd hours. That's Vara, the Tyrant watcher.

## Mini-Quest: Kel's Errand

**Source:** Kel approaches the party during their bridge exploration (if they've been friendly to the shifters) or Old Mera asks as a test of character (if they haven't engaged yet).

**The Job:** "There's a package at a dead drop in Firelight. I was supposed to pick it up, but... some people are watching the route. People I don't want to see me. Could you grab it? It's just medicine for Old Mera's joints. Nothing illegal." (It is, in fact, just medicine. Kel is honest.)

**The Route:** From the Dragon's Rest Firelight entrance, through three side streets to a mailbox outside a derelict temple. The route goes past:
- A Daask gambling operation (two goblins playing dice in an alley — they'll hassle anyone who passes, but back off if the party looks dangerous)
- A Boromar lookout post (a halfling on a rooftop who watches but doesn't interfere)
- The derelict temple itself — which is the same temple from "The Bell Tower Haunting" side quest

**Complication:** When the party reaches the dead drop, they find a Boromar thug already there, trying to break into the mailbox. "This is Boromar business. Walk away." The party can:
- Intimidate/persuade (DC 12): the thug leaves, muttering about "shifter-loving outsiders"
- Fight: one thug (bandit stats) — quick, easy, but the Boromar remember
- Wait: the thug gives up after failing to open the lock, leaves, and the party retrieves the package

**Payout:**
- 30 gp from a grateful Old Mera
- Kel's loyalty: he becomes a permanent ally on the bridge
- The shifters warm to the party immediately
- Old Mera shares a piece of neighborhood wisdom: "That halfling across the way — Emmek — he's in debt to the Boromar. Desperate people do desperate things. Watch your kitchen."

**Faction Hook:** The Boromar thug was there on Emmek's tip — Emmek told the Boromar the shifters were moving contraband (they weren't). Emmek's campaign against the Dragon's Rest has already begun.

## DM Guidance: When to Run These

- Run **1 quest** in Session 2, not both. These are seasoning, not the main course.
- **The Bridge Rats** is better if the party engaged with Riddick and enjoys dungeon-crawling. It also connects directly to the sealed Dhakaani door.
- **Kel's Errand** is better if the party engaged with the shifters. It introduces faction dynamics and the Emmek subplot earlier.
- If the party doesn't seem interested in side work yet, skip straight to Scene 7. Session 3 has plenty of questing.

**Pacing Check:** After exploring the bridge (Scene 5) and one small job, the party should feel like Bridge 47 is their neighborhood and its people are their people. They should have 30-50 gp in pocket — enough to feel like they've started, not enough to solve their problems.
