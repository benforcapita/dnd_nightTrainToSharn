# Scene 8 — The Ledger Does Not Lie

## Scene Goal
The hammer falls. After a session of exploring, meeting neighbors, and starting to love this place, the party confronts the brutal math. This pub is theirs — but it's a ruin, and ruin is expensive. This scene should land like a punch to the gut: they've got 30 days and they're broke.

## Setup
The party has explored the bridge. They've met neighbors. They've maybe done a small job and earned a handful of coins. They've fallen in love with this place — the ghost, the view, the potential.

Now comes the reality check.

### Read Aloud — The Financial Reality
> *"You spread your possessions across the bar. The coins from your pockets. Whatever you managed to carry off the Firefly. The shard — unsellable, too hot, too dangerous, glowing faintly as if it knows you're thinking about it.
>
> Someone finds a ledger — the previous owner's, half-soaked in spilled whiskey, the last pages torn out. The numbers are clear enough. And they are not kind.
>
> The Sharn Inquisitive sits on the bar, still smelling of fresh ink. The front page screams about the Firefly. The back pages are quieter — job listings, bounties, 'discreet contractors wanted.' Someone has circled three postings in red ink: the dead owner, desperately trying to save this place before they gave up.
>
> Outside, Sharn hums with commerce. Skycoaches drift past. Merchant carts rattle over the bridge. Somewhere a noble is paying too much for a simple job. Somewhere a monster has a bounty on its head.
>
> You're broke in the richest city on the continent. And this pub — this beautiful, haunted, impossible pub — is about to be taken from you unless you find a way to pay."*

### The Numbers
The party needs gold — and soon:

| Expense                            | Amount         | Deadline                  |
| ---------------------------------- | -------------- | ------------------------- |
| Back taxes                         | 100 gp         | 30 days                   |
| Bakers' and Brewers' Guild license | 25 gp          | Before opening            |
| Innkeepers' Association membership | 50 gp          | Optional, but recommended |
| Bridge Maintenance "fee"           | 10 gp          | Monthly                   |
| Kitchen restoration                | 100 gp         | Before health inspection  |
| Room restoration                   | 200 gp         | Before renting            |
| Fix the bells                      | 50 gp + 1 week | Optional                  |
| **Total minimum needed**           | **~175 gp**    | Within 2-3 weeks          |
| **Full renovation dream**          | **~1,000 gp**  | Eventually                |

**Their current assets:** Whatever they carried off the Firefly (possibly ~50 gp from Deneith, raider gear to sell, and the shard they *cannot* sell openly) plus whatever they earned from a bridge mini-quest (~30-50 gp).

## How To Make Money In Sharn
The party has options. This scene introduces them:

**The Sharn Inquisitive Classifieds:** A copy of the morning paper is on the bar. The back pages are crammed with job listings, bounties, and "discreet contractors wanted." Someone — the dead owner — circled three in red ink. A contact name is scribbled in the margin.

**The Notice Board (Bazaar, Middle Dura):** The city maintains public notice boards in every major ward. Bazaar's is outside the Gold & Gear pawn shop. Postings include bounties, odd jobs, and "no questions asked" work.

**Word of Mouth:** The neighborhood already knows the pub has new owners. The shifters, Riddick, even Kael — they all know people who need things done. Small jobs come to those who are visible and willing.

**The Bridge Itself:** Riddick mentioned something about "the under-bridge passage connecting to old smuggling routes." There might be forgotten caches down there.

## The Bridge-Specific Money Angle

Since the party now knows the bridge, add these location-specific money hooks:

| Source | Opportunity | Reward |
|---|---|---|
| Riddick | "The old owner hid something before he died. Said it was 'insurance.' Never found it." | Hidden cache somewhere in the pub or bridge interior |
| Kael | "Bounty board at the watchpost. Nothing big. Enough for groceries." | Small bounties (20-30 gp) for local troublemakers |
| Old Mera | "The shifter community needs muscle sometimes. We pay what we can." | 20-40 gp for local protection work |
| The false wall (Room 3) | The crawlspace continues past the bridge — it connects to an old maintenance hub that might have salvageable materials | Scrap metal, old tools worth 15-25 gp |

## DM Guidance: Pacing the Money Bomb

This scene lands at the end of the session for a reason. The party should feel:
1. **Connected** — they love this place now
2. **Overwhelmed** — the numbers are big and the deadline is real
3. **Motivated** — not desperate yet, but the pressure is on

End the scene by transitioning to the Morning Visitors, who will make the pressure even more concrete. The tax collector's knock should feel like the universe piling on.

**Transition Line:**
> *"You go to bed with numbers swimming behind your eyes. One hundred and seventy-five galifars, minimum. Thirty days. You've got maybe eighty between you if you're lucky.
>
> Tomorrow, you'll figure it out. Tomorrow, you'll start earning.
>
> Tomorrow arrives with a knock at the door. And another. And another."*
