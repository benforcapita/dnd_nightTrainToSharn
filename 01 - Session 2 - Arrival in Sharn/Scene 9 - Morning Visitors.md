# Scene 9 — Morning Visitors (The Money Bomb)

## Scene Goal
The final scene of Session 2. On the heels of the financial reality sinking in, the visitors arrive — each one bringing a bill, a demand, or an offer with strings. This is the money bomb. The party should end this session feeling the pressure: they need to earn serious gold, and fast.

Play the visitors as a rapid sequence — knock, demand, knock, demand. Let the party barely breathe between them.

### Visitor 1: The Tax Collector
A bored-looking gnome with a City Council badge and a clipboard that has seen things.

> *"Back taxes on Bridge 47, Parcel 3 — The Dragon's Rest. One hundred galifars. You have thirty days. After that, the city repossesses and the property goes to auction. Emmek d'Ghallanda has already filed a notice of interest. Have a nice day."*

She doesn't wait for a response. She's already walking to the next delinquent property.

### Visitor 2: The Guild Inspector
A halfling woman with the Bakers' and Brewers' Guild crest pinned to her lapel. She sniffs the air disapprovingly.

> *"New ownership, then. I'm required to inform you that operating a food or beverage establishment within Sharn municipal limits requires a Guild license. Twenty-five galifars. Annually. Also: your kitchen failed its last inspection. You'll need a reinspection before you can serve food. The fee for that is fifteen galifars. The inspector — that's me — will be back in two weeks."*

She leaves a form. It's three pages long. The fine print mentions "additional compliance fees."

### Visitor 3: Emmek d'Ghallanda
The rival. He arrives with a bottle of wine (cheap) and a smile (fake).

> *"Welcome to the neighborhood! I run the Golden Hearth, just across the bridge. Lovely to have neighbors. I heard about the tax situation — tragic, really. The previous owner, well... between us, he owed money to some people who don't accept excuses. I do hope you'll have better luck."*

He looks around the taproom with poorly disguised hunger. This is his expansion. He wants it.

> *"If you ever find yourselves overwhelmed — and most new owners do — I'm always interested in acquiring adjacent properties. A generous offer. No pressure."*

All pressure.

### Visitor 4: The Bridge Maintenance Collector
A dwarf with more scar tissue than skin and a wrench the size of his forearm. He represents the Bridge Maintenance Collective — which is absolutely a Boromar front, though no one says it out loud.

> *"Bridge 47 is due for structural assessment. Ten galifars a month, payable to the Collective. Keeps the inspectors away. Keeps the bridge standing. Everyone on the span pays it. You want to be everyone, don't you?"*

He doesn't threaten. He doesn't need to. The implication is clear: pay the "fee" or the bridge develops "structural problems" that "only affect" the Dragon's Rest.

### Visitor 5 (Optional): The First Faction Emissary
Based on Session 1 choices, one faction makes early contact. This is a *taste* of what's coming in Session 3 — not the full pitch, just a hello:

- **Corrin Delmaco (Boromar):** A note delivered by a well-dressed halfling child. *"Heard about the train. Heard you handled yourselves. The Boromar Clan values capable people. Let's talk when you're settled. — C.D."*
- **Niho Koi (Daask):** No note. No emissary. But when the party looks out the south windows that night, a massive silhouette stands on the opposite bridge. Watching. It doesn't move. It doesn't blink. Then it's gone.
- **Leara (Tyrants):** Vara the shifter stops by with a basket of bread — "from the community." She's warm, friendly, and asks too many questions about where the party came from and what they're carrying.
- **Marshal Serik ir'Deneith:** A formal letter. *"House Deneith extends its protection to all legitimate businesses in Sharn. Given recent events, we recommend you register your establishment with our Security Directorate. A representative will visit next week."*

---

## Session End

The visitors leave. The party stands in their dusty taproom, a stack of bills on the bar, a rival across the bridge, a ghost in the belfry, and a shard that glows a little brighter every time someone looks at it too long.

The session should end with a clear, shared understanding: **we need money, and we need it together.**

### Closing Read Aloud
> *"The door closes behind the last visitor. The taproom is quiet again — quieter than it was before, because now the silence is full of numbers. One hundred galifars to the city. Twenty-five to the Guild. Fifteen for the inspector. Ten to the bridge. Fifty to fix the bells Torvin loves. A hundred to make the kitchen safe. Two hundred to make the rooms livable.
>
> And somewhere across the bridge, Emmek d'Ghallanda is already counting the days until your deadline.
>
> Outside, Sharn doesn't care. The city churns on — skycoaches drifting, merchants shouting, temples tolling the hour. It will swallow you if you let it.
>
> The shard pulses once in your pocket. Warm. Steady. Like a heartbeat.
>
> You are not alone. You have a pub. You have a ghost. You have each other.
>
> And tomorrow, you start earning."*
