# Session 2 — Ending

The party has:
- A pub they now know intimately — every creaky floorboard, every neighbor's face, every secret in the walls
- A ghost who might become a friend (Torvin, if they were kind)
- A bridge that's more than a bridge — two wards, two watch precincts, a smuggler's dream
- Neighbors who are starting to trust them (or starting to watch them)
- A stack of bills that landed like a hammer: taxes, guild fees, bridge "maintenance," inspection costs
- A rival (Emmek) who's already counting the days
- A shard (glowing warmer)
- The first whisper of faction attention
- A clear, shared problem: **they need ~175 gp in 30 days, and they have maybe half of it**

## The Emotional Arc
Session 2 should end with the party feeling two things simultaneously:
1. **Love for this place.** The bridge, the pub, Torvin, the shifters, the view from the belfry — it's their home now, and it's worth fighting for.
2. **Pressure.** The numbers are real. The deadline is real. The people circling them are real. They can't do this alone.

This is the engine that drives Session 3: the party comes together to earn the money, and in doing so, they attract every faction in Sharn.

## DM Reminders
- Let Session 2 breathe. The players just survived a train heist. They need to feel like they've landed somewhere real and worth caring about.
- The pub and bridge should feel like *theirs* by the end. Encourage them to name rooms, plan renovations, form opinions about neighbors.
- Every NPC they meet in this session returns. Riddick, Kael, Old Mera, Kel, Emmek — they're all recurring. Let the players form attachments.
- Torvin is a gift. If they restore his bells, he watches over them. If they ignore him, the pub feels colder. Either way, he's there.
- The shard's dream (Scene 7) is the first clue in the campaign-spanning mystery. Play it for awe, not exposition.
- The money bomb (Scenes 8-9) should feel overwhelming but not hopeless. The party should think: "We can do this. But we need to work together."

## Next Session — Session 3: The Factions Close In
The party needs gold. They hit the streets of Sharn to earn it — and every job they take, every contact they make, brings them deeper into the faction web. By the end of the week, they'll have the money (or most of it), but they'll also have a dozen new "friends" who want something in return. The factions parade through their pub, and the party must decide who to trust — before someone makes the choice for them.
