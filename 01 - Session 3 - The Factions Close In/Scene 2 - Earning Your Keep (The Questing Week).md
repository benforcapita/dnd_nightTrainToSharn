# Scene 2 — Earning Your Keep (The Questing Week)

### Setup
Between cleaning the taproom and dealing with guild officials, the party needs to earn gold. This week is the perfect time for 2-3 quests. The faction emissaries arrive throughout the week — sometimes during a job, sometimes after.

### Quest Integration: Faction Flavors
Quests taken during this week can tilt faction attitudes. Track who the party helps (or hurts):

| If the party... | Faction Effect |
|---|---|
| Takes a job in Daask territory, kills monsters | Daask notices — hostility or respect depending on approach |
| Helps shifters, shows compassion to outcasts | The Tyrants — Leara's Session 3 approach is warmer; Tyrants see potential allies |
| Does work for Boromar Clan contacts (legal or gray) | Corrin's offer includes a "loyalty discount" on the protection tax |
| Refuses shady work, sticks to honest jobs | Deneith and Orien view them as legitimate operators |
| Takes shady work, kills to get ahead | Daask and Boromar Clan see them as "professionals"; Deneith files a report |
| Explores Sharn|the Cogs or Dhakaani ruins | The Twelve takes interest; Cipher accelerates observation |
| Works near Morgrave University | Professor Nymm's contacts learn the party is active; a letter may arrive |
| Loots a Cannith facility or interferes with Cannith property | Merrix d'Cannith hears their names; future patron or problem |

### Quest Hubs by Ward
The party can seek work in different wards. Each hub has a different flavor:

#### Bazaar (Middle Dura) — The Honest Grind
- **Notice board** outside Gold & Gear pawn shop
- **Sharn Inquisitive office** — register as contractors
- **Guild halls** — the Bakers' and Brewers' Guild sometimes needs "problem solvers"
- **Street criers** — merchants shouting for caravan guards, delivery help, bouncers

#### Firelight (Lower Menthis) — The Gray Market
- **The King of Fire nightclub** — Boromar Clan Clan|Corrin Delmaco sometimes has "private contracts"
- **Back-alley fixers** — unlicensed job brokers who don't ask for names
- **Entertainment venues** — theaters, gambling dens, and brothels need security, discreet couriers, and "talent"

#### Tavick's Landing — The High-End Circuit
- **Skycoach docks** — pilot Vance needs gunners; pay is good, risk is high
- **Noble estates** — bodyguard contracts, party security, "find my idiot son" investigations
- **House enclaves** — Orien and Deneith outposts post bounties for "recovery operations"

#### The Cogs — Danger Pay
- **Industrial foremen** — monster clearing, equipment recovery, labor disputes gone violent
- **Blackbones** — temple jobs, monster bounties, smuggling routes
- **Daask territory** — the only jobs here are from Daask. Taking them is a statement.

### Mid-Session Quest Pacing
If the party has 2-3 quests and 7+ faction emissaries to meet, spread them across the week:

- **Day 1-2:** First quest. First faction visit (whoever you want to introduce early).
- **Day 3:** Second quest. 2-3 faction visits.
- **Day 4-5:** Third quest (optional). Remaining faction visits. The Threat (Scene 3) hits at the end of the week.

### Encounter: Street-Level Sharn
During the questing week, the party learns how Sharn's wards connect. Drop these travel snippets between quests:

- *"Bazaar takes 12 minutes. Long enough to notice the halfling in the corner hasn't blinked once. Tyrant watcher, or just weird?"*
- *"Firelight at dusk. Every street corner has a different kind of trouble — Boromar game, a Daask shakedown, a Tyrant identity exchange. You're learning to read the signs."*
- *"A skycoach passes overhead, close enough to see the passengers. Lady Elira ir'Orien. She doesn't see you. She's arguing with someone — a cloaked figure. The skycoach banks and vanishes into Upper Central."*
- *"The Cogs are a different city beneath the city. Hot, loud, dark except for forge-fires and everbright lanterns strung like festival lights. Every shadow could be a Daask monster. Every foreman could be on someone's payroll. You don't belong here — and everyone knows it."*

### Financial Progress Tracker
The party should end this scene with enough gold to make real choices. Track their progress:

- **Below 100 gp:** They can't even pay taxes. Desperation sets in. Crime becomes tempting.
- **100-175 gp:** Taxes and one renovation. The pub opens, barely functional.
- **175-300 gp:** Taxes, guild fees, kitchen. The pub is legitimate — and profitable.
- **300+ gp:** Comfortable. Multiple renovations. Faction respect increases — they're capable operators.
