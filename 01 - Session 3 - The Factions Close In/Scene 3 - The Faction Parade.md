# Scene 3 — The Faction Parade

Throughout the week, emissaries arrive. Play these as social encounters, not combat. Each faction wants something different.

### The Boromar Clan — Corrin Delmaco
**Approach:** Friendly. Professional. Brings a gift: a case of Aundairian wine and a list of reliable suppliers.
**Pitch:** "We've been here since before the towers touched the sky. You're new. You need friends. We're the best friends you'll find in Sharn — because we're honest about what we are."
**The String:** Boromar expect a 10% cut of pub profits. In exchange, no other syndicate bothers the party. This is "the Sharn tax." Everyone pays it. Refusing is an insult — and a declaration of war.

### Daask — A Threat Disguised as an Offer
**Approach:** A dead cockatrice left on the doorstep. Niho Koi arrives in person — as a beautiful half-elf, then dropping the illusion to reveal the oni beneath.
**Pitch:** "You're being watched by everyone. We're the only ones strong enough to make them stop. The Daughters value loyalty. Prove yours."
**The String:** Daask wants the party to move cargo through the Dragon's Rest cellar — monster parts, dragonshard dust, Droaam contraband. Refusal means the dead cockatrice is just the beginning.

### The Tyrants — Leara
**Approach:** A shifter woman who's been "living in the neighborhood for years." She's friendly. She's helpful. She knows the party's names, their war records, and exactly how much gold they have in their pockets.
**Pitch:** "We don't want your money. We don't want your pub. We want to know what you know — and we'll pay you in the same currency. Information is the only thing in Sharn worth more than dragonshards."
**The String:** The Tyrants want the party to be informants. Every interesting customer, every faction visit, every secret the party learns — they report it. In exchange, the Tyrants provide information the party can't get anywhere else.

### House Orien — Lady Elira's Letter
**Approach:** A formal letter delivered by courier. Sealed with the Orien unicorn.
**Pitch:** "I am grateful for your actions aboard the Firefly. If you wish to discuss your future in Sharn, I am at your service. — E.O."
**The String:** Lady Elira wants the shard back — quietly. She'll offer money, legitimacy, and political protection. the shard means giving up the only leverage the party has. House Orien cannot be trusted to keep their word about "no questions asked."

### House Deneith — Marshal Serik's Visit
**Approach:** Official. Serik arrives in uniform with two guards. He's not hostile — he's professional. He may even be sympathetic.
**Pitch:** "The Firefly investigation is ongoing. House Orien has filed no complaint. You are not suspects. However — Sharn is dangerous. House Deneith offers protection contracts to all legitimate businesses."
**The String:** The protection contract includes "security consultations" — Dragon's Rest, reporting everything to Serik's superiors. It also means no other faction attacks openly. The price: 50 gp/month and total loss of privacy.

### The Aurum — Lord Vex's Proxy
**Approach:** Indirect. A letter on gold-leaf paper, delivered by a servant who doesn't speak Common.
**Pitch:** "The item you carry has a market value that exceeds your imagination. We are prepared to make an offer. Name your price."
**The String:** The Aurum doesn't want The Siberys Shard|the shard for its power — they want it for the leverage it provides. the shard becomes a commodity and the party becomes rich — the shard themselves. And the Aurum will use the party's sale as evidence that they were always mercenaries, never heroes.

### The Twelve — A Stranger's Whisper
**Approach:** No one identifiable. A note left under a tankard. A whisper in a crowded street. A sending stone that activates once, delivering a message in a synthesized voice.
**Pitch:** "The shard is a Key. You are holding a word in the Draconic Prophecy. We can help you understand what that means. We can help you survive what comes next. But you must let us study it."
**The String:** The Twelve wants to study The Siberys Shard|the shard — and the party. They'll provide arcane resources, knowledge, and protection. But they'll also experiment. The party becomes research subjects. Cipher is always watching.

### The Raiders — Ash's Message (If Alive)
**Approach:** Crude. A former Raider — one of the twins, Mira or Voss — finds the party. They're battered, hungry, and desperate.
**Pitch:** "Ash is alive. Barely. He's in hiding. But he wants you to know: the Houses are lying about the Firefly. About the statue. About everything. We have proof. Help us expose them."
**The String:** The Raiders are broken and hunted. Allying with them means becoming fugitives by association. But they have truth no one else will share — and a loyalty the Houses can't buy.
