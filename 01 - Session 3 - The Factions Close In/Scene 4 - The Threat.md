# Scene 4 — The Threat

### Setup
One faction — whichever the party has most offended or ignored — makes a move. This is the session's action beat.

### Option A: Emmek Escalates
Emmek d'Ghallanda, Dragon's Rest before Boromar Clan|the Boromar Clan collect on his debts, hires thugs to "encourage" the party to sell. The thugs start a bar fight, break furniture, and rough up any staff or customers present.

**Combat:** 3-4 thugs (use bandit stats + one tougher leader). Non-lethal intent — they're here for intimidation, not murder.

**Aftermath:** The Sharn Watch arrives. Sergeant Kael (the corrupt-but-friendly watchman) offers to "handle it" — for a consideration.

### Option B: Daask Sends a Message
A monster — a troll or a pair of harpies — attacks the pub at night. This is Daask's response to refusal. The attack is brutal but brief — Daask wants the party scared, not dead.

**Combat:** 1 troll OR 2 harpies + 4 winged kobolds. The bridge location makes flight tactics devastating.

**Aftermath:** The pub is damaged. Neighbors saw the attack. The Sharn Inquisitive prints a story. The party now has a public reputation — and a public enemy.

### Option C: Boromar Collect
If the party refused the 10% cut, Boromar collectors arrive. Not thugs — professionals. They explain, politely, that refusing the Sharn tax is bad for business. They'll give the party one more week. After that, Dragon's Rest becomes a "fire hazard."

**No combat (yet).** This is intimidation. But the clock is now ticking.
