# Scene 5 — The Party Decides

By session's end, the party must make at least one decision:
- Which faction (if any) to accept as a patron
- Boromar Clan tax
- Whether to hire staff (and which ones)
- Whether to renovate or save gold for the taxes
- the shard (hide it, study it, try to sell it)

These decisions shape everything that follows. There are no right answers — only consequences.

---

## Session End

The pub is operational (barely). The factions are circling. The party has made at least one ally and at least one enemy. The shard continues to react — subtly — to the presence of dragonmarked individuals, aberrant marks, and the city itself.

### DM Reminders
- The faction scenes are the heart of this session. Give each faction representative personality and presence. Let the players feel the weight of every offer.
- The threat scene should feel like a consequence of player choices, not random violence. If they've made no enemies yet, Emmek is the default.
- The party should leave this session feeling like the pub is *theirs* — and like defending it will be a constant fight.
- The shard's reactions should be subtle: it warms near dragonmarks, hums near aberrant marks, goes cold near the Dhakaani door in the cellar.

### Next Session
The explosion. The faction war goes public. Dragon's Rest — and the party is pulled into the investigation.
