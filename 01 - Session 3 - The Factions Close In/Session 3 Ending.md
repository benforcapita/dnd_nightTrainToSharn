# Session 3 — Ending

The pub is operational (or close to it). The party earned their gold together — through honest work, shady deals, or a mix of both. The factions are circling, and the party has made at least one ally and at least one enemy. The threat has proven that the danger is real, not theoretical.

## What the Party Should Have by Session's End

- **Gold:** 175+ gp (enough for taxes, guild fees, and basic repairs) — or close enough that Session 4 can bridge the gap
- **A functional pub:** The taproom is open, the kitchen might be working, customers are starting to come
- **Staff:** At least 1-2 hires (and at least one of them reports to someone else)
- **Faction relationships:** One or two factions the party is leaning toward, one or two who've been offended
- **A clear enemy:** Someone made a move — Emmek, Daask, or Boromar — and the party knows who's coming for them
- **A sense of momentum:** They've gone from "we're going to lose this place" to "we're building something." That hope is about to be tested.

## DM Reminders
- The faction scenes are the heart of this session. Give each faction representative personality and presence. Let the players feel the weight of every offer.
- The threat scene should feel like a consequence of player choices, not random violence. If they've made no enemies yet, Emmek is the default.
- The party should leave this session feeling like the pub is *theirs* — and like defending it will be a constant fight.
- The shard's reactions should be subtle: it warms near dragonmarks, hums near aberrant marks, goes cold near the Dhakaani door in the cellar.
- **The money should feel earned.** This session is the payoff for Session 2's money bomb — the party worked together, and they solved the problem. Let them feel good about that... right before Session 4 blows everything up.

## Bridge to Session 4 — The Culmination

Session 4 is the culmination of everything Sessions 2 and 3 built. The party has a home, a community, and a fragile position in Sharn's ecosystem. Session 4 tears it open:

- An explosion rips through a nearby bridge, killing civilians and destroying property
- The blast was aimed at the party — someone was trying to deliver something (or kill someone) at the Dragon's Rest
- The shard reacts violently, revealing itself as a Key to something ancient and dangerous beneath Sharn
- The faction cold war goes hot — everyone knows the party is at the center of it
- The campaign pivots from "survive and build" to "investigate and pursue"

**Transition Line (to use at session's end):**
> *"You've done it. The taxes are paid. The kitchen is clean. The sign is hung. Customers are starting to trickle in — neighbors who've heard the pub is under new management, travelers who need a room, shifters who finally feel welcome somewhere.
>
> Torvin's bells ring every evening now. The whole bridge stops to listen.
>
> You've built something. Against the odds, against the city, against everyone who said four broke veterans couldn't make it in Sharn — you've built something.
>
> Enjoy tonight. Because tomorrow, the sky catches fire."*
