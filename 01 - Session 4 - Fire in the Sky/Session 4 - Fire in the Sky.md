# Session 4 — Fire in the Sky

## Session Goal
The faction war goes public. An explosion tears through a Sharn street near the Dragon's Rest. Someone was hunting the shard — openly, violently. The party investigates and discovers they're not just witnesses; they hold one half of something enormous, and every faction wants the other half. The race for the Key has begun.

## Pacing
Starts with a massive action setpiece, transitions to investigation, ends with a revelation. This session kicks off the Dragon Heist core.

---

## Scene 1 — The Explosion

### Setup
A normal day at the Dragon's Rest. Customers. Staff. The party going about their business. Then:

### Read Aloud
> *"The sound reaches you before the light — a thunderous CRACK that rattles the glasses behind the bar and sends birds scattering from the belfry.
> 
> Then the light: a boiling sphere of orange and violet fire that erupts from a bridge two spans over, maybe a hundred feet away. The shockwave hits a heartbeat later — windows shatter, customers scream, someone's drink shatters in their hand.
> 
> Through the smoke, you see bodies. Some are running. Some are not.
> 
> And in the center of the blast radius, something metallic gleams — a construct, humanoid, its chest blown open from the inside. It was carrying something. Something that was meant for you."*

### The Blast Site
The explosion occurred on **Bridge 52**, two spans from the Dragon's Rest. It was a magical detonation — a *fireball* spell, or something like it — that killed 3 civilians and destroyed a shopfront.

**At the center:** A dead **nimblewright** — a humanoid construct of Cannith design — its chest cavity blown outward as if the explosion originated from *inside* it. The construct was delivering something to the Dragon's Rest. Someone intercepted it.

### The Construct's Cargo
Scattered around the blast site:
- Shredded parchment with partial addresses: "...Dragon's R..." and "...Bridge 47..."
- A House Cannith delivery manifest addressed to "Occupants, The Dragon's Rest"
- A spellshard (magical storage device), cracked but potentially recoverable
- The construct's internal recording crystal — fractured but possibly still functional

### Witnesses
- A shifter child who saw "a woman with silver eyes" touch the construct moments before it exploded
- A Boromar lookout who saw Daask harpies in the area
- A House Phiarlan entertainer who was performing nearby and "saw everything" — but whose story changes each time they tell it
- The victims: three Sharn citizens, burned beyond recognition. One was carrying a letter of introduction to the party — they were coming to apply for a job at the Dragon's Rest.

---

## Scene 2 — First Responders

### The Sharn Watch Arrives
Within minutes, the Watch secures the area. Sergeant Kael (the corrupt watchman from the Dragon's Rest neighborhood) is among them. He's genuinely shaken — civilians died.

**Kael's Questions:**
- "You know anything about this?"
- "That construct — it was heading your way. Why?"
- "I'm supposed to report this upstairs. What do you want me to say?"

**Kael's Offer:** He'll "delay" his report for 24 hours — giving the party time to investigate before the Watch officially opens a case. In exchange, he wants a favor (to be named later). This is corruption, but it's also an opportunity.

### The Sharn Inquisitive Arrives
A journalist pushes through the crowd. "Anyone here from the Dragon's Rest? The address on the manifest — that's you, isn't it? What's your connection to the construct? Were you the target?"

The party can:
- Answer honestly (publicity, but also scrutiny)
- Dodge the question (the Inquisitive prints speculation instead)
- Bribe the journalist (creates a debt — journalists have long memories)
- Threaten them (creates an enemy with a printing press)

### Other Arrivals
- **A House Cannith representative:** They want the construct's remains. "Proprietary technology. We'll compensate you for your trouble."
- **A Deneith marshal (not Serik):** Officially taking over the investigation. Unofficially, they're reporting to someone above Serik's pay grade.
- **A faction operative:** Whichever syndicate the party has most engaged with sends someone to "help." This is surveillance disguised as support.

---

## Scene 3 — The Investigation

The party has 24 hours before the Watch makes this official. They need to learn:

1. **Who sent the construct?** (And why?)
2. **Who destroyed it?** (And how?)
3. **What was it carrying that was worth killing civilians over?**
4. **Why was it addressed to the party?**

### Investigative Leads

#### The Spellshard
If the party recovers the cracked spellshard, it requires a DC 14 Intelligence (Arcana) check to stabilize and read. It contains:

- A map of Sharn with several locations marked
- A name: **Hrabbaz** (Boromar enforcer) OR **Braala** (Daask contact) OR **Leara** (Tyrant operative) OR **Nightshade** (House Tarkanan agent) — pick the villain's faction
- Fragments of notes: "The Firefly cargo... statue... shard... the REAL target is..."
- A partially decoded Dhakaani inscription referencing "the Heart" and "the Key" as separate objects

#### The Recording Crystal (DC 16 Intelligence (Arcana) to repair)
If repaired, it shows the construct's final moments:
- The construct was sent to deliver the spellshard to the Dragon's Rest — a tip-off from an anonymous source about the Key's location
- A woman (the "silver eyes" witness) intercepted it on the bridge and touched its chest. The construct recorded her face, her hand — and the aberrant dragonmark on her skin before it exploded
- The woman is a House Tarkanan operative — or someone who wants the party to think she is

#### Witness Testimony
- **Shifter child:** "The woman — she wasn't angry. She was... sad. She touched the metal man and whispered something. Then she ran."
- **Boromar lookout:** "Daask's been running cargo through the Cogs. Heard they're looking for something that came off the Firefly."
- **Phiarlan entertainer:** Their third version of the story mentions "a symbol on the woman's hand — like a dragonmark, but wrong. All sharp edges."
- **Victim's letter:** The dead civilian was an old veteran who heard "the new pub owners are solid. They helped people on that train. I'm going to ask for work." They died because they were walking toward the Dragon's Rest.

#### The Construct's Origin
A DC 13 Investigation check on the construct's remains reveals a Cannith serial number. House Cannith (South) confirms the construct was dispatched from a Bazaar workshop — but the dispatch order was forged. Someone with Cannith clearance (or a very good forgery) sent it.

## Scene 4 — The Connection

### The Shard Reacts
When the party brings the spellshard, recording crystal, or other evidence near the Siberys shard, the shard reacts powerfully:

- It glows with internal golden light — not the steady hum of before, but a pulsing rhythm, like a heartbeat
- The character carrying it hears a voice — the Forge-Warden from their dream: *"They are looking for the door. But they do not have the Key. And they do not have me. Without the Heart, the Key is dead metal. Without the Key, the Heart beats for nothing."*
- For a moment, the shard projects a vision visible to everyone nearby: a Dhakaani door sealed with a single massive lock, deep beneath the city. Carved into the lock are two sockets — one shaped like a key, one shaped like the shard. The message is unmistakable: **both halves are required.**
- The vision ends. The shard goes cold. But the party now knows: they hold the power source. The Key is out there. And someone is trying to find it.

### The Attunement Threshold
If any character attempts to attune to the shard (requiring a short rest while holding it), they discover:

- The shard is not a key — it is the **Heart of Duur'Sharaat**, the power source that drives the Dhakaani vault
- Attunement grants the character a sense of direction — a pull toward something beneath Sharn. But the pull is vague, diffuse — the shard is searching for its other half, and can only point roughly downward
- The attuned character can now sense the presence of the **Key** — a distinct resonance separate from the shard. If the Key is nearby (within a few hundred feet), the shard will pulse like a heartbeat
- **Side effect:** The attuned character now dreams of the Forge-Warden every night. The dreams show fragments of the Key's hiding place — Dhakaani ruins, old maps, familiar Sharn landmarks seen from angles no living person remembers

## Scene 5 — The Faction Reaction

News of the explosion spreads fast. Within hours, every faction knows someone tried to deliver something to the party — and someone else blew it up.

### Faction Responses (choose based on main villain)

#### If Boromar is the Main Villain
Corrin Delmaco arrives, genuinely concerned. "That construct — it was carrying information about our operations. Someone inside the Clan is working against us. Against *you*. I need your help to find out who."

#### If Daask is the Main Villain
A dead bird — a messenger pigeon — is nailed to the Dragon's Rest door. Inside its leg capsule: a note. "The construct was ours. What it carried was ours. Return it, and we forget the explosion ever happened. — N.K."

#### If House Tarkanan is the Main Villain
The party receives a sending stone that activates once: a synthesized voice. "The woman with silver eyes was one of ours. She's gone rogue. Find her before she finds you. — O.N."

#### If the Tyrants are the Main Villain
Leara appears, looking genuinely worried — an unusual emotion for a Tyrant. "Someone is playing all of us. The explosion wasn't meant to kill the construct. It was meant to frame someone. I think it was meant to frame *you*."

### The Party's Choice
The party now has a clear direction: track the artifact trail through Sharn. The spellshard (or witness testimony, or the recording crystal) points them toward the first link in the chain — which leads to the Key.

But they're not the only ones looking. The factions are racing too. And most of them don't yet know that the Key is useless without the shard.

The hunt begins.

---

## Session End

The explosion has changed everything:
- The party is now publicly connected to the blast
- The shard has revealed itself as a power source — the Heart of Duur'Sharaat
- The Key is somewhere in Sharn, and everyone wants it
- The main villain has been identified (or hinted at)
- The artifact trail begins — the seasonal encounter chain

### DM Reminders
- The explosion should feel like a gut punch. Civilians died. One of them was coming to apply for a job. Let the players feel that.
- The shard's reaction is the moment the campaign pivots from "survive and hide" to "race and pursue." Play it for maximum impact.
- The two-half reveal (shard + key) should feel like both a relief and a ticking clock — they have something valuable, but it's only half the puzzle, and everyone else wants the other half
- The faction response scene sets up the alliance/enmity structure for the rest of the campaign. Choose carefully which faction approaches and how.
- The party should end this session with a clear goal: race for the Key. They have the power source — now they need the lock.

### Next Session
The encounter chain begins. The party follows the trail through Sharn's wards, chasing the Key while factions hunt the same prize. The race is on — and the party's hidden advantage (the shard) won't stay hidden forever.
