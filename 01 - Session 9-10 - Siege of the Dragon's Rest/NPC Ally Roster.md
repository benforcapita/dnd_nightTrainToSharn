# NPC Ally Roster — The Siege

## Overview
The party has spent Sessions 2-8 building relationships across Sharn. Before the siege begins, they have a window to call in favors. Not every NPC will come — only those whose loyalty the party has genuinely earned. The DM should track which NPCs the party has befriended, helped, or made deals with, and offer 3-5 allies from the roster below.

Each ally provides a specific mechanical benefit during the siege. The party chooses who to call and how to deploy them. Some allies can be assigned to specific defensive positions; others provide passive benefits.

---

## Core Allies (The Dragon's Rest)

### Torvin Stonehands — The Ghost
**Relationship trigger:** The party restored the belfry bells (Session 2-3) and treated him with kindness rather than trying to exorcise him.

**Siege benefit — The Bell-Ringer's Vigil:**
- Passive: Torvin rings the restored bells as an alarm system. The party gets 10 minutes of warning before each assault wave.
- Active (1/siege): Torvin manifests corporeally for one round and shoves one enemy out a window. 200-foot drop. No attack roll — this is narrative. Choose the moment for maximum impact. He bellows something in Dwarvish as he does it.
- After manifesting: Torvin is drained. He fades to a faint shimmer for the rest of the siege. He whispers: *"That was the last of me. The rest is yours."*

**If the party never restored the bells:** Torvin still warns of intruders (cold spots, phantom bell tones) but cannot manifest corporeally. His warnings are vague — "danger near the Bazaar door" rather than "six halflings approaching from the west."

### The Staff
**Relationship trigger:** The party hired staff during Session 2-3 and treated them well.

**Siege benefit — Hold the Line:**
- The staff barricade the interior, prepare bandages, and hold secondary positions.
- They provide 2d4 temporary HP to any party member who takes a short rest in the taproom between waves.
- One staff member (the cook, a retired Cyran sapper) has a surprise: a satchel charge. Single use — 4d6 fire damage in a 10-foot radius, DC 13 Dex save for half. She's been saving it "for the right moment."
- If the party mistreated their staff: Some quit before the siege. Others stay but are unreliable — one might be a faction informant.

### Riddick the Launderer
**Relationship trigger:** The party befriended him, bribed him, or protected his laundry from a faction shakedown.

**Siege benefit — The Gossip Network:**
- Riddick knows which factions are arriving and roughly when. He gives the party a rough order of battle before the siege starts.
- During the siege, Riddick's urchin runners (street kids who hang around his laundry) bring updates: "The harpies just pulled back — Daask is repositioning" or "There's shouting in the Boromar camp."
- The urchins can also carry messages to NPCs outside the siege zone — useful if the party needs to coordinate with an ally who hasn't arrived yet.

## Faction Patron Allies

The party has likely allied with one faction patron during Sessions 4-8. That patron commits resources to the defense — but expects something in return.

### Corrin Delmaco — Boromar Clan
**Relationship trigger:** The party made a deal with Boromar and honored it.

**Siege benefit — The Halfling Hand:**
- 4 Boromar enforcers (use **thug** stats with halfling traits) hold the Bazaar entrance.
- Corrin provides a sending stone linked to her. One use: she can call off Boromar forces if the party convinces her the coalition is about to collapse. Make a DC 15 Charisma (Persuasion) check.
- **The cost:** Corrin wants the schema — or at least a copy. The party will owe the Boromar Clan a major favor, collected in Arc 6.

### Niho Koi — Daask
**Relationship trigger:** The party made a deal with Daask. This is the darkest alliance — it means they accepted monster muscle and looked the other way on collateral damage.

**Siege benefit — Monstrous Reinforcements:**
- 2 gnoll enforcers and 1 harpy hold the Firelight entrance. The harpy's song disrupts enemy spellcasters (disadvantage on concentration saves within 60 feet).
- Niho Koi offers a "gift": a troll. The troll regenerates. It also eats anything it kills. The party must decide if they're comfortable with that.
- **The cost:** Daask will demand the schema in full. No negotiation. The Daughters of Sora Kell are watching. If the party refuses later, Daask becomes a permanent enemy — and Niho Koi does not forgive.

### Leara — The Tyrants
**Relationship trigger:** The party made information trades with the Tyrants and didn't betray them.

**Siege benefit — The Mask Network:**
- Leara provides real-time intelligence: which faction is about to attack, which entrance they're targeting, and what their numbers are. No check required — the Tyrants have operatives in every faction's camp.
- 2 changeling operatives infiltrate one enemy faction's staging area. At a critical moment, they sow confusion — a DC 15 Deception check to make one enemy squad attack another.
- **The cost:** The Tyrants want a copy of the schema AND the party's identities on file — descriptions, mannerisms, voices. The party will never know when a Tyrant is wearing their face. This has consequences in Arc 7-8.

### Oleander Nightbloom — House Tarkanan
**Relationship trigger:** The party sympathized with Tarkanan's cause or helped them against a dragonmarked house.

**Siege benefit — Aberrant Edge:**
- 2 Shroud operatives (aberrant-marked rogues with one random aberrant power each) hold the belfry as snipers.
- Oleander provides a single-use aberrant mark token. When activated, it creates a 20-foot radius *silence* effect for 1 minute — devastating against enemy spellcasters.
- **The cost:** Oleander asks the party to spare one specific enemy — a dragonmarked heir fighting alongside the Aurum or Deneith forces. He wants them alive. For *questioning*. The party must decide if they're willing to hand someone over to Tarkanan.

## Wildcard Allies

These NPCs aren't tied to faction patronage. They join the defense because of personal loyalty — or personal agendas.

### Marshal Serik ir'Deneith
**Relationship trigger:** The party earned Serik's respect (not his obedience — his *respect*). This happens if they acted with integrity, protected civilians, and showed him they're not just mercenaries.

**Siege benefit — The Marshal's Choice:**
- Serik arrives alone, in full Deneith plate, carrying Treaty. He says: *"I'm not here for Deneith. I'm here because you're worth defending. Don't make me regret this."*
- He holds whichever entrance the party assigns him to. Alone. He's CR 5 — he can hold a doorway against 3-4 enemies per round.
- If Serik falls: he gives the party his sealed letter (addressed to a Cyran orphanage) and tells them to open it. When they do (later), it contains the names of every Orien operative Deneith ever had killed — and an apology. *"We were following orders. That doesn't make it right."*
- Serik's presence may cause House Deneith forces outside to hesitate. They weren't ordered to fight one of their own marshals.

### Captain Ash
**Relationship trigger:** The party spared Ash on the Firefly (Session 1) and has maintained contact. OR the party freed him from whoever captured him during Sessions 5-8.

**Siege benefit — The Raider's Return:**
- Ash arrives with 4 raiders (use **bandit** stats with veteran traits) through the under-bridge trapdoor. They rappelled up from the Depths. *"You saved my crew. I save your pub. We're even."*
- Ash's raiders are mobile — they can reposition between the two entrances in 1 round using the bridge's exterior (risky: DC 13 Acrobatics or fall).
- Ash himself provides tactical direction: one party member per round can use their reaction to gain advantage on an attack roll.
- **Complication:** Ash is dying. If he takes more than half his HP in damage, he collapses after the siege. The party learns about his condition. He asks them to find his daughter in the lower wards "when this is over."

### Professor Nymm
**Relationship trigger:** The party protected Nymm during Sessions 2-4, helped him escape whoever was poisoning him, and treated his knowledge as valuable rather than annoying.

**Siege benefit — The Scholar's Wards:**
- Nymm fortifies the Dragon's Rest with arcane wards before the siege. Each entrance gets a *glyph of warding* (3d8 thunder damage, DC 14 Dex save for half). The glyphs trigger once each.
- Nymm identifies the sealed Dhakaani door in the cellar. He can't open it — "That requires the Key, and I suspect also the shard, working in concert" — but he confirms it's stable and safe to approach.
- During the siege, Nymm hides in the cellar with the shard (if the party entrusts it to him). He casts *identify* on it one more time and learns something new: the shard is not just a power source. It carries the consciousness-imprint of the Forge-Warden. The voice in the dreams is real.
- **Complication:** Nymm is not a fighter. If enemies reach the cellar, he has *invisibility* and *mirror image* prepared — but he'll flee rather than fight. The party must decide if they trust him with the shard.

### Lady Elira ir'Orien
**Relationship trigger:** The party stayed on good terms with Elira, didn't expose her role in the Firefly incident, and kept her reputation intact.

**Siege benefit — The Exit Strategy:**
- Elira doesn't send troops. She sends a single sending stone message: *"When you're ready to run — and you will be — Terminus Station. A private car. No questions. I owe you that much."*
- This is a genuine escape route. If the party decides the siege is unwinnable, they can flee to Terminus and board an Orien lightning rail out of Sharn. The campaign continues — but they lose the Dragon's Rest, the artifacts, and their reputation.
- **This is the safety valve.** The party should know they can escape. Choosing not to — choosing to hold their ground — is what makes them heroes.
- If the party refuses her offer publicly (in a way she hears about), Elira's respect for them grows. She sends one more message: *"Then I will make sure no one in Orien moves against you tonight. Consider it a professional courtesy."* This removes House Orien security forces from the siege entirely.

## Minor Allies

These are smaller relationships that still matter. The party can collect 1-3 of these depending on side encounters in Sessions 2-8.

### Watch Sergeant Kael
**Benefit:** Kael delays the Sharn Watch's "official response" by 4 hours. After that, the Watch arrives — not to help either side, but to arrest everyone. The party has a 4-hour window to win the siege or escape.

### The Shifter Community (Firelight Side)
**Benefit:** The shifters barricade the Bridge 47 approach on the Firelight side, slowing enemy reinforcement by 2 rounds per wave. They won't fight — they're civilians — but they'll buy time.

### A Grateful Veteran (from Session 1 or 4)
**Benefit:** One veteran the party helped — maybe someone they saved on the Firefly, or the friend of the civilian who died in the Session 4 explosion — shows up with a heavy crossbow and a grim expression. Holds a window position. Dies if the party doesn't protect them. Their death should hurt.

### The Baker on Bazaar Side
**Benefit:** A shopkeeper the party befriended. She sends her son with fresh bread and a pot of hot coffee between waves. Short rest benefits: +1d4 HP recovered. She also passes a note: "There's a man in Boromar colors asking about your cellar door. Thought you should know."

## Ally Selection — DM Guidance

**How many allies?** 3-5, chosen by the party based on relationships they've built. This isn't a shopping list — they only get the allies whose triggers they've met.

**How to present it:** At the start of Session 9, give the party a "war council" scene. They know the siege is coming (the shard has been humming, faction scouts have been spotted, Riddick's urchins bring warnings). They sit in the Dragon's Rest taproom, a map of Bridge 47 spread on the scarred table, and decide who to call and where to position them.

**The unrecruited allies:** NPCs the party didn't earn still exist. They might appear as complications — a Deneith marshal who arrests an ally, a Boromar contact who sold information to the enemy, a professor who got captured and must be rescued. The world doesn't stop because the party didn't befriend someone.
