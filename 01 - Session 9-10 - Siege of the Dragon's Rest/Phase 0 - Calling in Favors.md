# Phase 0 — Calling in Favors

## Setup
Session 9 opens in the Dragon's Rest taproom, late afternoon. The party has just acquired the Key (end of Session 8). They've barely had time to breathe. The shard is warm — warmer than usual. The Forge-Warden's voice in the attuned character's mind is no longer whispering. It's counting.

The party knows the factions are coming. They don't know exactly when. This is the war council.

## Read Aloud — Opening
> *"The shard sits on the scarred oak table, pulsing with a light that matches no lantern in the room. It's never done this before — not this brightly, not this insistently. The Key lies beside it: a Dhakaani seal-lock, ancient and cold, inert without the shard's current.
>
> Through the windows, Bridge 47 is quiet. Too quiet. The Bazaar market stalls closed early. The Firelight gamblers found somewhere else to be. Even the wind between the towers has stilled.
>
> Torvin's phantom bell rings once — a single, mournful tone.
>
> You have maybe an hour. Maybe less.
>
> Who do you call?"*

## The War Council

The party has 3-5 ally slots. They choose from the NPC Ally Roster based on relationships they've built. The DM should have a clear sense of which NPCs are available — track who the party befriended across Sessions 2-8.

### How It Works Mechanically

1. **The Map:** Lay out the Dragon's Rest floor plan (taproom main floor, upper rooms, cellar, belfry). The party assigns allies to defensive positions:
   - **Bazaar entrance (west):** Most exposed. Closest to Boromar and Deneith staging.
   - **Firelight entrance (east):** Closest to Daask staging. The shifter community is here.
   - **Belfry (above):** Sniper position. Overlooks both approaches. Tarkanan controls the rooftops.
   - **Cellar (below):** Where the sealed Dhakaani door is. Where the shard should be kept. Defensible but a dead end.
   - **Trapdoor (under-bridge):** Emergency escape to the Depths. Also an infiltration vector.

2. **The Allies Arrive:** Each NPC the party calls responds in their own way. Play these scenes — they're the emotional payoff for Sessions 2-8 of relationship building. Some NPCs come immediately. Others have conditions. Some can't come but send help.

3. **The Countdown:** The DM tracks real time (roughly). After 45-60 minutes of in-game preparation, the coalition arrives. The party's preparation quality depends on how many allies they called and how well they positioned them.

### Ally Arrival Scenes

Play these based on who the party calls:

**Serik:** Arrives in full armor, alone. Stands in the doorway of the taproom, silhouetted against the Bazaar side. Long pause. *"I read the report on what happened to the civilian who was coming to apply for work. The one who died in the explosion. You kept their letter."* He's been watching. He knows. *"Where do you need me?"*

**Ash:** Doesn't arrive through the door. The party hears a whistle from below — Ash and his crew are at the bottom of the trapdoor rope ladder, 200 feet down in the Depths. *"Permission to come up, or should we stay low and hit them from behind when they breach?"* The party decides.

**Nymm:** Arrives with scroll cases tucked under both arms and a nervous twitch. Immediately starts examining the sealed cellar door. *"Fascinating. The resonance pattern matches the shard's crystalline lattice. When the door opens — and it will — you'll need to be ready for what's on the other side. Do you have a plan for that, or are we improvising?"*

**Leara:** Appears in the taproom as if she was always there. She was in the form of a chair. Or a customer. Or a party member. *"The Boromar are bringing clawfoots. Daask has a troll. Tarkanan's on the roof. The Aurum is watching from a skycoach. And I'm here because you're interesting. What do you need to know?"*

**Corrin Delmaco:** Sends a runner first — a halfling child who delivers a sending stone. When activated: *"The Rake wants me to pull our people back. I told them no. We had a deal. But if you don't hold that bridge, I can't protect you from what comes after. — Delmaco."*

**Elira:** Doesn't arrive. The sending stone activates once: *"I can't send forces without questions I can't answer. But no one in Orien will move against you tonight. That's all I can give. Don't waste it. — E."*

### Preparation Actions

Before the coalition arrives, the party can take one preparation action each:

- **Fortify:** Barricade an entrance. DC 13 Strength (Athletics). On success, enemies must spend 1 round breaking through before entering.
- **Ward:** Set a trap or glyph (requires spell slots or Nymm's help). Deals 2d8 damage to first enemy through.
- **Scout:** Get eyes on the approaching forces. Learn which faction is attacking from which direction and in what strength.
- **Inspire:** Rally allies and staff. All allied NPCs gain 5 temporary HP for the first wave.
- **Hide the artifacts:** Conceal the Key and the shard somewhere in the Dragon's Rest. DC to find them is 15 + character's Intelligence (Stealth) modifier.

## The Shard's Warning

During the war council, the shard pulses. The attuned character receives a vision — brief, sharp, like a memory that isn't theirs:

> *"You see the Dragon's Rest from above — as if you're floating over Bridge 47. Torches approach from both sides. Bazaar and Firelight. Rooftop shadows. A skycoach descending. The vision pulls back, further and further, until you see the city from a mile up — and a line of golden light running from the shard, through the taproom floor, through the cellar, through the sealed door, down and down into darkness.*
>
> *The Forge-Warden's voice: 'They come for the door. They do not know what is behind it. Show them.'"*

## Closing the Phase

When preparations are complete, move to Phase 1. The first faction forces appear at the edge of Bridge 47.
