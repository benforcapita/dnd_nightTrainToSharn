# Phase 1 — The Coalition Gathers

## Setup
The coalition arrives. Not in a rush — they're establishing positions, testing defenses, and more importantly, figuring out who's in charge. The factions don't trust each other, and no single leader has emerged. This phase is about tension and posturing before the violence starts.

The party should feel the weight of what's outside: six factions, dozens of enemies, and a bridge with only two exits.

## Read Aloud — The Arrival
> *"They come from both sides.
>
> From the Bazaar: halflings in Boromar green, moving with the easy confidence of people who own the street. Behind them, two clawfoot dinosaurs pace with the twitchy hunger of animals who've been trained to kill on command.
>
> From Firelight: the heavy footsteps of ogres, the cackle of gnolls, the leathery wingbeats of harpies circling overhead. At the rear, a troll lumbers forward — each step leaving a wet print on the bridge stone.
>
> Above: shadows on the rooftops. Tarkanan. You catch the glint of a crossbow, the flicker of an aberrant mark.
>
> And hovering above it all: a skycoach, burnished and sleek, the Aurum crest visible on its hull. Lord Vex is watching.
>
> The factions stop fifty feet from each entrance. They've drawn an invisible line.
>
> No one crosses it. Not yet."*

## The Parley (Optional)

Before the assault begins, the faction leaders attempt to negotiate — with each other, more than with the party. This is the party's first opportunity to exploit tension.

### Who Speaks

A Boromar lieutenant (not Corrin — she's too smart to stand in the open) shouts across the bridge toward the Daask position: *"Garra! We agreed — simultaneous breach. Your harpies go in first, clear the belfry. Then our clawfoots take the taproom. That was the deal."*

Garra shouts back: *"The deal changed. My troll goes first. Unless you want to argue with him."*

The troll grunts.

Oleander's voice drifts down from the rooftops, calm and cultured: *"Children. We are here for the schema, not to measure each other's claws. Unless the Boromar wish to demonstrate why halflings should not negotiate with monsters?"*

Lord Vex, from the skycoach, through a speaking trumpet: *"I have half a million galifars that says this coalition doesn't last the hour. Any takers?"*

### The Party's Opening

During this bickering, the party can:

- **Observe:** Passive Insight reveals the dynamic. Garra and the Boromar lieutenant genuinely despise each other. Oleander is playing both sides. Vex is here to profit, not fight. The Tyrants are watching — Leara's operatives are visible if the party knows what to look for (DC 15 Perception: a Boromar enforcer whose face doesn't quite fit, a gnoll who blinks too often).
- **Call out:** Shout something from the Dragon's Rest windows. A well-timed insult, a negotiation offer, or a revelation about one faction's secret plans. This can trigger fault lines directly — see Faction Coalition Dynamics.
- **Send an emissary:** A party member or allied NPC can approach one faction leader under flag of parley. This is dangerous but effective — a face-to-face conversation can plant seeds of betrayal that pay off later.

### The Tyrant Among Them

At some point during the parley, the party notices something: one of the faction soldiers — doesn't matter which faction — moves wrong. Their posture shifts mid-sentence. Their face flickers for a split second.

A Tyrant operative. Embedded in one of the assault forces.

The party can:
- Expose them: Shout it out. The host faction turns on the Tyrant. Chaos for 1 round. The Tyrants lose an operative. Fault line triggered.
- Ignore them: The Tyrant reports back to Leara. She knows the party saw. She's impressed they stayed quiet. She sends a sending stone message: *"Smart. We'll talk after."*
- Signal them: Make eye contact. The Tyrant nods slightly. For the rest of the siege, this operative feeds the party information through subtle gestures — which squad is attacking next, which entrance is the feint.

## The First Breach

The parley ends when someone loses patience. It might be:
- Garra, who gets bored of talking and sends the troll at the Firelight door.
- Oleander, who decides the talking has gone on long enough and drops a *darkness* spell on the bridge.
- The party, who fires the first shot.
- The Tyrant operative, who triggers something on purpose.

### Read Aloud — The First Blow
> *"The troll moves first.
>
> Not because Garra ordered it — because it's hungry, and bored, and there's a door in front of it that wasn't there a thousand years ago when trolls didn't have to wait for halflings to finish talking.
>
> It charges.
>
> And the siege of the Dragon's Rest begins."*

## Transition to Phase 2

The assault begins. Move to Phase 2 — The Assault. The coalition stability track starts at 5 (Stable).

## DM Notes
- The parley is a roleplay scene, not a combat scene. Let the faction leaders bicker. Let the party listen, plan, and plant seeds. Don't rush into combat until the tension is at its peak.
- The Tyrant operative among the enemy forces is a gift to the party. Don't make it too hard to spot — the point is the choice of what to do with the information, not the roll to get it.
- The first breach should feel inevitable, not scripted. Let it come from whichever direction feels right — the troll's impatience, Oleander's calculation, or the party's own initiative.
- This phase should take about 30-45 minutes at the table. Long enough to feel the weight of the coalition, short enough that players don't get restless.
