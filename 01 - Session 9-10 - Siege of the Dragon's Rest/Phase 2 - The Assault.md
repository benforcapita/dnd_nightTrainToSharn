# Phase 2 — The Assault

## Structure
The assault is not a single continuous combat — it's 3-5 waves with breathing room between them. Each wave tests a different defense. Between waves, the party can attempt to trigger fault lines, reposition allies, and recover.

The DM controls pacing: if the party is overwhelmed, give them a longer break. If they're cruising, shorten the interval. The goal is tension, not TPK.

---

## Wave 1 — Probing Attack (Coalition Stability: 5)

**The scenario:** The coalition sends a mixed force to test defenses. They're not committing their best — they want to see what the party has.

**Attackers (split between Bazaar and Firelight entrances):**
- 4 Boromar enforcers (use **thug** stats with halfling Nimbleness: can move through spaces of Medium+ creatures)
- 4 Daask gnolls (use **gnoll** stats; Rampage triggers on kills)
- 2 harpies (flying; Luring Song targets defenders near windows)

**Tactics:** The harpies circle the belfry, trying to lure defenders out the windows. The gnolls charge the Firelight door. The Boromar enforcers approach the Bazaar door cautiously, using cover. No faction commits their heavy hitters.

**Coalition behavior:** Factions are coordinated but visibly uncomfortable. The Boromar lieutenant shouts orders the gnolls ignore. A harpy nearly collides with a Tarkanan rooftop operative.

**After the wave:** A 10-minute lull. The coalition regroups. The party can:
- Attempt one fault line trigger (roleplay or skill challenge).
- Take a short rest (if they have time — the DM decides if the lull is long enough).
- Reposition allies.
- The shard pulses once. The attuned character hears: *"They test your walls. They do not yet test your will."*

---

## Wave 2 — Coordinated Breach (Coalition Stability: 4-5)

**The scenario:** The coalition has seen the party's defenses. Now they commit. This wave is designed to break through one entrance.

**The DM chooses which entrance the coalition targets based on which seems weaker.** The other entrance gets a token force — 2 enemies, easily held by whichever ally is stationed there. The main force hits the target entrance with overwhelming numbers.

**Attackers (main entrance):**
- 6 mixed faction soldiers (Boromar or Daask, DM's choice)
- 1 heavy: a clawfoot dinosaur (if Boromar) OR an ogre (if Daask)
- 1 spellcaster: a Boromar magewright (casts *magic missile*, *fog cloud*) OR a Daask harpy (Luring Song + claw attacks)

**Attackers (secondary entrance):**
- 2 faction soldiers (token force, easily repelled)

**Special event — The Troll:** The Daask troll breaks formation and attacks the secondary entrance on its own initiative. Garra didn't order this. The troll is just hungry. This is a gift to the party: the troll is dangerous but fighting alone, and its disobedience creates tension between Garra and the other coalition leaders.

**Tactics:** The heavy unit targets the door/barricade. The spellcaster provides support. The soldiers try to overwhelm defenders with numbers. If the entrance falls, enemies pour into the taproom — the fight becomes a room-to-room brawl inside the Dragon's Rest.

**After the wave:** Another lull, 5-15 minutes. The party can:
- Attempt one fault line trigger.
- If the taproom was breached, they now have the option to fall back to the upper floor or cellar. Allies reposition.
- Torvin, if present and not yet used, whispers: *"They're arguing out there. The monster woman and the halfling. I can't hear the words but I can hear the tone."* (This tips the party toward the Boromar/Daask fault line.)

---

## Wave 3 — The Push (Coalition Stability: 3-4)

**The scenario:** The coalition is frustrated. They've lost troops, they're arguing among themselves, and the party is still standing. This wave is their attempt to end it — they commit heavy forces to BOTH entrances simultaneously. The party cannot defend both with equal strength. They must choose which entrance to hold and which to abandon — or split the party.

**Attackers (Bazaar entrance):**
- 4 Boromar enforcers
- 1 clawfoot dinosaur
- 1 Boromar magewright (if still alive; otherwise, the Boromar lieutenant enters combat — use **veteran** stats)

**Attackers (Firelight entrance):**
- 4 Daask gnolls
- 1 ogre
- 1 harpy (if still alive)
- The troll, if it survived Wave 2

**Tarkanan joins:** Oleander decides the coalition needs a push. 2 Tarkanan Shroud operatives (aberrant-marked rogues) descend from the rooftops and enter through the upper floor windows or the belfry. They don't engage the main defense — they're hunting for the artifacts. If the party hid the shard or Key, the Shroud operatives search for it. If they find it, they signal Oleander and attempt to escape with it.

**Critical moment — The Tyrant Operative:** If the party spotted the Tyrant operative in Phase 1 and didn't expose them, the operative chooses this moment to act. A Boromar enforcer or Daask gnoll suddenly turns on their own squad — stabbing the squad leader, then vanishing into the chaos. The squad is thrown into confusion for 1 round (no attacks, Dodge action only). Leara's voice, through a sending stone: *"A gift. You owe me one."*

**After the wave:** Longer lull, 15-20 minutes. The Dragon's Rest is damaged. The taproom is a wreck. At least one ally might be down. The party can:
- Attempt ONE fault line trigger. This is the moment when fault lines are most likely to break — the coalition is stressed, bleeding, and looking for someone to blame.
- Short rest (if time allows).
- Make a desperate move: send someone out the trapdoor to flank, parley with a faction leader, or threaten to destroy the shard.

---

## Wave 4 — Desperation or Fracture (Coalition Stability: 1-3)

**If the coalition is still stable (3+):** They commit everything. Every remaining faction leader enters combat personally. This is the hardest fight of the siege — a boss wave with multiple CR 3-5 enemies. The party will need to use every ally and every resource.

**If the coalition is fracturing (1-2):** This wave is chaos. Factions fight each other as much as the party. The DM rolls on the Chaos Table below at the start of each round.

### Wave 4 Chaos Table (d6, roll each round if stability ≤ 2)
1. **Boromar turns on Daask.** 1d4 Boromar enforcers and 1d4 Daask gnolls fight each other in the street. Neither attacks the party this round.
2. **Tarkanan assassinates a coalition leader.** Oleander's operatives eliminate a Boromar or Daask squad leader. That faction's remaining forces hesitate (disadvantage on attack rolls for 1 round).
3. **The Tyrants withdraw.** Leara's operatives pull back simultaneously across all fronts. The party loses their intel stream but the coalition loses coordination — all remaining waves have a 1-round gap.
4. **Vex makes an offer.** The Aurum skycoach descends. Lord Vex shouts: "Half a million to whoever brings me the shard intact — and I don't care which side you're on." 1d4 faction soldiers defect and attack their own side, trying to reach the shard for Vex.
5. **The Watch arrives early.** Sergeant Kael couldn't hold them. A squad of 6 Sharn Watch guards arrives and attempts to arrest everyone. They target the faction forces first (the party is inside a building; the faction forces are in the street). This removes 1d6 faction soldiers from the siege as they flee or fight the Watch.
6. **The shard erupts.** The shard pulses with blinding golden light. Every creature within 60 feet must make a DC 14 Wisdom save or be stunned for 1 round. The attuned character is immune. The Forge-Warden's voice, audible to everyone: *"YOU ARE NOT WORTHY."* This resets the battlefield — and terrifies the coalition.

### If the Coalition is Fully Broken (Stability 0)
Wave 4 doesn't happen. The siege dissolves into a three-way free-for-all in the street: Boromar vs. Daask vs. whoever is left. The party watches from the ruins of their taproom. This is the victory lap — let them savor it.

## Between-Wave Actions

After each wave, the party gets a window to act. These are the mechanical options:

### Trigger a Fault Line
The party chooses one fault line from Faction Coalition Dynamics and attempts to trigger it. This is a roleplay moment supported by a skill check. Success reduces coalition stability by 1-2 points. Failure means the attempt was noticed — the targeted faction becomes hostile to the party specifically, and that faction's attacks are now directed at the character who made the attempt.

### Rally an Ally
One allied NPC who is bloodied or frightened regains composure. The party member makes a DC 13 Charisma (Persuasion) check. On success, the ally recovers 2d8 HP and gains advantage on their next attack.

### Repair Defenses
Rebuild a barricade, reset a trap, or reinforce a door. DC 13 Strength (Athletics) or Intelligence (tool check). Restores one defensive position to full effectiveness.

### Scout the Coalition
Get eyes on the enemy. Stealth check (DC varies by approach). Learn which faction is preparing to attack next, from which direction, with what forces. This lets the party position allies optimally for the next wave.

### Threaten the Shard
Hold the shard up at a window and make it clear the party will destroy it if the assault continues. This is a bluff — the shard can't easily be destroyed. But the coalition doesn't know that. DC 15 Charisma (Intimidation or Deception). On success, the next wave is reduced by 1d3 enemies (factions hesitate). On failure, the coalition attacks with renewed fury — the next wave gains +1 enemy (they called the bluff).

---

## The Tipping Point

At some point during the assault — probably after Wave 2 or 3 — the party should realize they can win. The coalition is bleeding. The fault lines are showing. The party's allies are holding.

The DM's job is to time this moment. Make the early waves hard enough that victory feels impossible. Then, as fault lines trigger and the coalition fractures, the tide turns. The party goes from barely surviving to controlling the battlefield.

This is the emotional arc of the siege: despair → hope → triumph.

---

## DM Notes
- Waves 1-3 are mandatory. Wave 4 only happens if the coalition hasn't fractured by then. If the party is clever about triggering fault lines, they may never face Wave 4 — and that's the intended outcome. The siege should be won through social engineering, not just combat endurance.
- The assault is a marathon, not a sprint. Use the lulls for roleplay, for the shard's visions, for Torvin's whispers, for the staff bringing water and bandages. The Dragon's Rest should feel *alive* during the siege — it's not just a map, it's a character.
- Kill an ally if it serves the story. Not randomly — purposefully. The veteran who showed up with a crossbow. The cook with the satchel charge. Someone the party cares about. Their death raises the stakes and makes the victory matter.
- Track the coalition stability track publicly or privately — your choice. Public tracking lets the party see their progress. Private tracking preserves mystery. Either works.
