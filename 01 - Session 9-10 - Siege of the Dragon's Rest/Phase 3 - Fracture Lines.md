# Phase 3 — Fracture Lines

## Setup
The coalition is cracking. Whether through the party's manipulation, the inherent instability of enemies who hate each other, or the sheer attrition of the assault, the factions are turning on one another. This phase is the climax of the siege — the moment the party's strategy pays off and the battle becomes a brawl between enemies.

This phase is not a separate combat wave. It's what happens *between* waves, or instead of Wave 4, when coalition stability drops to 2 or below. It can bleed into Wave 4 (the chaos table version) or replace it entirely.

---

## The Breaking Point

When the coalition fractures, the DM describes the moment. Choose the fault line that broke first and make it cinematic.

### If Boromar vs. Daask Broke First

> *"Corrin Delmaco steps out from behind the Boromar line. She's been watching from the rear, letting her people do the bleeding. But something's changed.
>
> She's looking at Garra. And Garra is looking back.
>
> 'Your troll,' Corrin says, her voice carrying across the bridge with the clarity of someone who's done waiting. 'Your troll ate two of my people. Not during the breach — during the lull. He was hungry and they were close. You want to explain that, Garra?'
>
> Garra snarls. 'They shouldn't have been close.'
>
> 'No,' Corrin says. 'They shouldn't have.' She draws a blade you've never seen before — thin, silver, humming with abjuration magic. 'But we're going to discuss it anyway.'
>
> And then the Boromar enforcers turn on the Daask gnolls, and the bridge becomes a warzone."*

### If Tarkanan Broke First

> *"Oleander's voice drifts down from the rooftop — still calm, still cultured, still utterly terrifying.
>
> 'Lord Vex,' he calls. 'Your skycoach has been hovering above my operatives for two hours. I find this distracting. I also find the Aurum's role in the dragonmark trade personally offensive. Shall we settle both issues at once?'
>
> Vex's response is lost in the sudden eruption of violet-black energy from the rooftop — Oleander's aberrant mark, activated. Two Shroud operatives leap from the rooftop onto the skycoach's hull.
>
> Vex's war mage responds with a bolt of lightning. The skycoach lurches. The duel begins."*

### If the Tyrants Broke First

> *"It happens without warning. One of the Boromar enforcers — the one who's been standing too still, blinking too slowly — pulls a dagger and drives it into the back of the Boromar lieutenant's knee. The lieutenant goes down screaming. The enforcer's face flickers — changeling features, pale and featureless — and then they're gone, vanished into the chaos.
>
> Across the bridge, a Daask gnoll does the same thing to Garra's second-in-command. And on the rooftop, one of Oleander's Shroud operatives shoves the other off the edge.
>
> Leara's voice, from everywhere and nowhere: 'The Faceless Council sends its regards. This coalition was always a bad idea. We simply... accelerated its conclusion.'"*

### If the Party Made It Happen

If the party triggered the fracture through roleplay, a deception, or a well-timed revelation, give them the spotlight. Let the player describe what they did — their words, their action, their moment of strategic brilliance — and then show the consequences.

---

## The Free-for-All

Once the first fault line breaks, the coalition unravels quickly. The DM describes the battlefield:

- The Bazaar approach: Boromar enforcers fighting Daask gnolls. The clawfoot and the troll are tearing into each other. Corrin and Garra are locked in single combat.
- The rooftops: Tarkanan operatives engaging the Aurum skycoach. Oleander and Vex's war mage trading spells. Vex himself shouting orders, then screaming, then silent.
- The bridge itself: faction soldiers who don't know who to fight anymore, some still targeting the Dragon's Rest, others turning on their "allies."
- Deneith: If they haven't already withdrawn, the marshals form a shield wall at the edge of the combat zone. They're protecting civilians. They're not engaging either side. The commanding marshal is shouting: "Let them kill each other! Hold the perimeter!"

### What the Party Does

During this chaos, the party has options:

1. **Let them fight.** Hold the Dragon's Rest and watch the coalition destroy itself. Safe option. The party conserves resources for the descent.
2. **Pick off stragglers.** Target weakened faction leaders or isolated squads. Risky but satisfying — the party can eliminate future threats while they're distracted.
3. **Save someone.** If an NPC the party respects (Corrin, if she's been honorable; the Deneith commander; a civilian caught in the crossfire) is in danger, the party can intervene. This builds relationships that matter in Arc 6-8.
4. **Make a statement.** The party emerges from the Dragon's Rest — battered, bloody, standing together in the wreckage of the bridge. They hold up the shard. It glows. The Forge-Warden's voice speaks through it. Everyone stops fighting and stares. This is a power move that will be remembered.

---

## The Coalition Collapses

After 3-5 rounds of the free-for-all, the coalition is done. Survivors retreat. The DM describes the aftermath:

- Boromar pulls back to the Bazaar, carrying their wounded. Corrin, if she survived, gives the party a single nod — acknowledgment, not forgiveness.
- Daask melts into the Firelight shadows. Garra, if she survived, snarls something in Gnoll. The harpies scatter. The troll's body lies on the bridge, already beginning to rot.
- Tarkanan vanishes from the rooftops. Oleander, if he survived, is gone — but a single black feather drifts down onto the Dragon's Rest belfry. A calling card. He'll be back.
- The Aurum skycoach retreats skyward. Vex, if he survived, will send a letter within the week: "The offer still stands. The price, however, has gone up. — V."
- The Tyrants were never really there. Leara's voice, on the sending stone: *"Well played. We'll be watching your descent with great interest."*
- Deneith marshals lower their shields. The commanding marshal approaches the Dragon's Rest. *"You held. I don't know if that makes you heroes or fools. Either way — the Watch will be here in twenty minutes. I suggest you be somewhere else."*

## Read Aloud — The Morning After
> *"Dawn comes to Bridge 47.
>
> The Dragon's Rest is still standing. The windows are shattered. The taproom floor is scarred with claw marks and scorch marks and bloodstains that will never come out of the wood. The sign — 'The Drag   Rest' — now has a harpy feather embedded in the 'D.'
>
> But the hearth is still warm. The bells are still in the belfry. The staff is still alive.
>
> Torvin's ghost is fainter than before — a shimmer of cold air near the chimney. He doesn't speak. He doesn't need to.
>
> Outside, the bridge is littered with the debris of a battle no faction will ever officially acknowledge. The Sharn Inquisitive will call it 'a gas leak.' The Watch will file no charges. The syndicates will count their dead and plan their next moves.
>
> But you held. Against six factions. Against the weight of a city that wanted what you carried.
>
> And beneath your feet, in the cellar, a Dhakaani door that has been sealed for a thousand years is glowing."*

---

## Transition to Phase 4

The siege is over. The party is battered but alive. The sealed door in the cellar is humming audibly now — the shard has opened it. Below: Duur'Sharaat.

Move to Phase 4 — Victory and Descent.

## DM Notes
- The fracture scene should feel earned. If the party triggered it through roleplay, let the table savor the payoff. If it happened organically through the chaos table, make it feel inevitable — the coalition was always doomed, and the party was smart enough to survive until it collapsed.
- The free-for-all isn't a combat the party needs to win — it's a scene they witness and optionally participate in. Don't run it as a full tactical combat unless the party actively engages.
- The morning-after read-aloud is the emotional closure of the siege. Give it space. Let the table sit with what they've accomplished.
- The descent into Duur'Sharaat should feel like a reward, not an obligation. The party *earned* this. No external pressure. No ticking clock. Just them, the shard, the Key, and the unknown.
