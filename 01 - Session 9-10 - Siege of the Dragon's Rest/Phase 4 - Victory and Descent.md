# Phase 4 — Victory and Descent

## Setup
The siege is over. The coalition is broken. The Dragon's Rest is damaged but standing. The party has a narrow window before the Sharn Watch arrives, before the factions regroup, before the city remembers what just happened and comes looking for answers.

And the sealed Dhakaani door in the cellar is open.

This phase is the bridge between the siege and the dungeon. It's a scene of aftermath, recovery, and the weight of what comes next.

---

## The Cellar Door

When the party descends to the cellar, they find the sealed stone door — the one marked with faded Dhakaani runes, the one the previous owner never opened, the one that's been waiting since Session 2 — is no longer sealed.

The shard opened it. Not through any action the party took — simply through proximity and intention. The door recognized the shard (the power source) and the Key (the lock mechanism). When both were present, it opened.

### Read Aloud — The Open Door
> *"The cellar is cold and quiet. The rats have fled — even they knew something was coming.
>
> The Dhakaani door stands open. Not cracked, not ajar — fully open, the stone slabs retracted into the walls like they were waiting for this moment. The runes along the frame are glowing with the same golden light as the shard.
>
> Beyond the door: a spiral stairway, carved downward through solid stone. The air that rises from it is warm and dry and smells of old metal — forge-ash, machine oil, and something that might be ozone.
>
> The shard in your hand is humming. Not a warning. A welcome.
>
> The Forge-Warden's voice, clearer than it's ever been: *'You held the door above. Now enter the door below. Duur'Sharaat remembers you. It has been waiting a thousand years.'"*

## Aftermath Scenes

Before the descent, the party has a few minutes. Play one or two of these scenes based on which allies survived and which relationships matter most.

### If Serik Survived
Serik is sitting on the taproom steps, cleaning blood off Treaty. He doesn't look up when the party approaches.

*"Deneith will discharge me for this. Dereliction of duty. Unauthorized engagement. Fraternizing with — " he gestures vaguely at the party. "Whatever you are."*

He sheathes the sword.

*"I knew that when I came. Don't look so guilty. A man should get to choose which orders to follow, at least once before he dies."*

He stands, grimaces at a wound in his side, and offers the party his hand — the first time he's ever done that.

*"Whatever's down there — come back. Someone in this city still needs to file proper paperwork."*

**If Serik died:** His sealed letter is on his body. The party finds it. When they open it, later, it contains the names of Orien operatives Deneith killed — and an apology. *"We were following orders. That doesn't make it right. If you're reading this, I finally stopped following them. — Serik."*

### If Ash Survived
Ash is at the bottom of the trapdoor, under the bridge, staring at the Depths below. His raiders are scattered around him, tending wounds. He doesn't turn when the party approaches.

*"This is where I tell you we're even. That my crew and I are gone by morning and you'll never see us again."*

He's quiet for a moment.

*"But there's something down there. Isn't there? Under the city. Something that matters more than any of this."*

He turns. His eyes are tired but steady.

*"The schema. The Storm Spire. It's real. You knew that before anyone else did. And you're going down there to get it."*

He reaches into his coat and pulls out a compass — old, military-issue, the glass cracked.

*"This pointed me home after the war. It still works. Take it. In case whatever's down there makes it hard to find your way back."*

He presses it into a party member's hand. Then he limps away down the bridge, his raiders falling in behind him.

**The Compass:** A mundane compass with a *guidance* enchantment (1/day, as the cantrip). It always points toward the Dragon's Rest.

### If Nymm Survived
Nymm is in the cellar, staring at the open door. He's been standing there for a while. His spectacles are fogged.

*"I've studied Dhakaani seals for forty years. Forty years. And I've never — "*

He gestures helplessly at the door.

*"This is the discovery of a lifetime. The discovery of several lifetimes. The workshop. The schema. The Prophecy fragments. It's all real, and it's all down there, and I'm — "*

He laughs, a little brokenly.

*"I'm too old to go with you. My bones would give out on the third stair. And frankly, I'm terrified. I've spent my whole career chasing this, and now that it's here, I don't know if I want to see what's inside."*

He turns to the party, serious now.

*"But you should go. You've earned it. And when you come back — bring me something. A chip of stone from the forge. A fragment of Dhakaani script. Something I can hold in my hands and know that it was real."*

He clasps his ink-stained hands together.

*"I'll be here when you return. Someone has to tell the university what happened. And someone — " he glances upward at the ruined taproom — "has to help rebuild."*

### Torvin's Farewell
If Torvin manifested during the siege, he is now barely visible — a shimmer of cold air near the hearth.

His voice is a whisper: *"The bells will ring on their own now. The Watch is coming, but they'll find only smoke and stories. Go. Go down. The forge-warden is waiting."*

The cold spot fades. The hearth fire flickers, then steadies.

Torvin is not gone — he's bound to the Dragon's Rest — but he's dormant. The party may not hear from him again for a long time.

### The Staff's Promise
The cook — the retired Cyran sapper — is sweeping glass off the taproom floor. She looks up when the party enters.

*"You're going down there."* It's not a question.

*"We'll keep the lights on. The hearth burning. The doors barred. When you come back — and you will come back — there'll be hot food and cold ale and a bar tab that you'll be paying off for the rest of your lives."*

She smiles. It's the first time she's smiled since the siege started.

*"Go. We've got this."*

## The Descent

When the party is ready, they descend. No external pressure. No combat waiting. Just the stairs, the darkness, and the shard's light.

### Read Aloud — The Descent
> *"The stairs spiral downward through stone that predates Sharn, predates the Five Nations, predates every kingdom that has ever claimed this land. The walls are carved with Dhakaani script — not warnings, but names. The names of the forge-wardens who built this place, who sealed it, who died protecting it.
>
> The shard illuminates each name as you pass. It knows them. It remembers.
>
> The air grows warmer. The smell of forge-ash intensifies. Somewhere ahead, a faint orange glow — fire elementals, still bound to the forges after a thousand years.
>
> The stairs level out. A stone archway ahead, flanked by two Dhakaani constructs — guardians, dormant, their runes dark. As you approach, the shard pulses. The constructs' runes flicker, then glow. They do not attack.
>
> One of them speaks, its voice the grinding of ancient stone: *'Forge-Warden recognized. Welcome home.'*
>
> Beyond the archway: a vast circular chamber, darkness stretching in all directions, and at its center — a forge fire that has not gone out in a thousand years.
>
> Duur'Sharaat."*

## Session 10 Ends Here

The descent is the final beat of Sessions 9-10. The party has survived the siege, bid farewell to their allies (temporarily or permanently), and entered the lost workshop.

Session 11 opens inside Duur'Sharaat — the dungeon proper begins.

---

## DM Notes
- The aftermath scenes are optional — play the ones that matter. If an ally died, give their death a moment. If the party is eager to descend, let them. But don't skip the emotional closure entirely — the siege was a defining event, and the table deserves to feel its weight.
- The descent read-aloud is the capstone. Deliver it slowly. Let the silence between sentences build. This is the payoff for 10 sessions of buildup.
- The constructs saying "Forge-Warden recognized" is a reveal: the shard doesn't just contain the Forge-Warden's consciousness — it *is* the Forge-Warden, in a sense. The voice in the dreams, the visions, the warnings — it's been her all along. This pays off in the dungeon when they find her tomb.
- The party should end Session 10 with hope and dread in equal measure. They've won. They've survived. And they're standing at the threshold of something ancient, powerful, and very, very dangerous.
