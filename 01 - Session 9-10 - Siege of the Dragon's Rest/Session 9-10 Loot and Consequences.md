# Session 9-10 — Loot and Consequences

## Experience and Milestones

This is a major milestone session. The party should level up after the siege (before or after the descent, DM's choice). Recommended: level 5 → level 6.

### Milestone Achievements
- Held the Dragon's Rest against a multi-faction siege
- Broke the coalition through strategy, diplomacy, or combat
- Opened the sealed Dhakaani door (or witnessed it open)
- Descended into Duur'Sharaat

## Material Rewards

### From the Siege Itself
- **Faction gear:** 2d4 sets of faction armor/weapons (Boromar leathers, Daask gnoll blades) — sellable for 50-100 gp total, or usable as disguises.
- **Lord Vex's calling card:** If the Aurum skycoach was damaged or Vex fled, the party finds an Aurum signet ring on the bridge. Worth 200 gp, or usable as leverage with the Aurum.
- **The troll's hide:** If the troll died, its hide can be harvested (DC 15 Survival). Worth 300 gp to an alchemist or armorer.
- **Gratitude of the neighbors:** The shifter community and Riddick's urchins pool their savings — 50 gp and a handmade banner reading "THE DRAGON'S REST — WE HELD." It's not worth much. It's worth everything.

### From the Dhakaani Door
- The door itself is now open permanently. It responds to the shard — no other key required. This gives the party a back entrance to Duur'Sharaat from their own cellar. This is a massive strategic asset in later arcs.

### From Allies (Choose Based on Who Survived)
- **Ash's Compass:** A mundane compass with *guidance* 1/day. Always points to the Dragon's Rest.
- **Serik's Letter:** Contains the names of Orien operatives Deneith killed — massive leverage with House Orien or Deneith in Arc 6-7.
- **Nymm's Research Notes:** If Nymm survives and the party brings him something from the workshop, he becomes a permanent research contact at Morgrave University — free *identify* and *legend lore* services.
- **The Staff's Loyalty:** The Dragon's Rest staff are now fanatically loyal. They will defend the pub, hide the party, and lie to the Watch without being asked.

## The Shard

The shard has changed:
- It now glows consistently when near Dhakaani ruins or other Keys.
- The Forge-Warden's voice is clearer, more present. She speaks in full sentences now, not fragments.
- The attuned character can sense the direction and approximate distance to Duur'Sharaat at all times.
- New vision: during the next long rest, the attuned character dreams of the Forge-Warden's final moments — sealing the workshop, hiding the Key, giving her life to power the shard. *"I did not die. I became something else. You carry me now. Do not waste what I gave."*

## The Dragon's Rest

The pub is damaged but standing. Repairs will cost 500 gp and take 2 weeks (or 1 week with the party's help). The damage includes:
- Shattered windows (all of them)
- Scorched taproom floor (permanent — becomes part of the pub's character)
- Damaged bar (functional but scarred)
- Bloodstains that won't come out (Torvin's ghost seems to like them — they remind him of the old days)

**Renovation bonuses** (if the party invests extra):
- **Reinforced doors (200 gp):** Both entrances gain AC 18 and 30 HP.
- **Belfry defenses (150 gp):** The bells can now be rung from the taproom via a pulley system. Torvin is pleased.
- **Cellar vault (300 gp):** A reinforced safe room in the cellar, connected to the Dhakaani stairway. Perfect for hiding artifacts or people.
- **Memorial plaque (10 gp):** A small plaque listing the names of allies who died defending the pub. The neighbors bring flowers.

## Story Consequences

### Immediate
- The Sharn Watch arrives 20 minutes after the siege ends. They find a ruined bridge, dead syndicate soldiers, and a pub full of veterans who "have no idea what happened, officer." Kael files a report that blames "inter-syndicate violence." No charges.
- The Sharn Inquisitive runs a front-page story: "BRIDGE 47 BLOODBATH — SYNDICATE WAR ERUPTS IN BAZAAR." The Dragon's Rest is mentioned only as "a local establishment caught in the crossfire." The party's names are not printed — someone pulled strings. (Elira? Leara? Cipher?)
- Word spreads through Sharn's underworld: the Dragon's Rest is not to be touched. The party held against six factions. That reputation matters.

### Arc 6-8 Implications
- The faction that lost the most in the siege becomes the primary antagonist for the next arc.
- The faction the party made a deal with expects payment. That debt will be collected.
- The Lord of Blades has been watching through warforged informants. He knows about the open door. He's interested.
- The Emerald Claw's Keeper Malachi received a report: "The Dhakaani workshop beneath Sharn is accessible. The party holds the key." Undead don't rush — but they do plan.
- The dragon of the Chamber (introduced in Arc 7) felt the shard activate when the door opened. She's been tracking it. She knows where the party is.
