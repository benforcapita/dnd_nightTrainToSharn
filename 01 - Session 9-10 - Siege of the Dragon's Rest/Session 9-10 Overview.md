# Session 9-10 — The Siege of the Dragon's Rest

## Session Goal
The party has both the Key and the shard. Every faction in Sharn knows it. The syndicates form a desperate, unstable coalition to take the Dragon's Rest by force. The party must hold their home, call in every favor they've earned, and exploit the fault lines between factions who hate each other more than they want the prize. This is the penultimate battle of Act 1. Survive, and the sealed Dhakaani door in the cellar opens — Duur'Sharaat awaits.

## Where This Sits in the Campaign
- **Sessions 5-8:** The hunt for the Key. The party follows the seasonal villain chain, navigates faction politics, and acquires the Key to Duur'Sharaat. They now possess *both* the shard (power source) and the Key (lock mechanism).
- **Sessions 9-10:** THIS — the siege. All the faction tensions built over Sessions 2-8 converge on one location.
- **Session 11+:** The descent into Duur'Sharaat. The party earned their passage. No external pressure. The dungeon itself is the climax of Act 1.

## Pacing
Two sessions. Session 9 covers the pre-siege rally, the coalition's arrival, and the first assault waves. Session 10 covers the fracture, the three-way brawl, and the descent.

## Scene Sequence

1. **Phase 0 — Calling in Favors** (opening of Session 9)
2. **Phase 1 — The Coalition Gathers** (mid Session 9)
3. **Phase 2 — The Assault** (late Session 9 into early Session 10)
4. **Phase 3 — Fracture Lines** (mid Session 10)
5. **Phase 4 — Victory and Descent** (end of Session 10)

## The Core Tension
The factions are not allies. They are rivals forced into a temporary coalition because no single syndicate can take the Dragon's Rest alone — the party has fortified it, recruited allies, and holds both artifacts. The coalition is held together by fear (that the party will escape into Duur'Sharaat with the prize) and mutual suspicion (that whoever breaches first will double-cross the others).

The party's job is not just to survive waves of attackers. It's to **find the cracks in the coalition and split it apart.** Every faction has a reason to betray every other faction. The party just needs to give them the opportunity.

## DM Notes
- This is the payoff for every NPC relationship the party has built. If they befriended Torvin the ghost — he matters now. If they earned Serik's respect — this is where he chooses a side. Let the players feel the weight of their social choices.
- The siege should feel overwhelming at first, then winnable as the coalition fractures. Don't pull punches on the early waves — let the party sweat.
- The Dragon's Rest's unique features (two entrances, belfry, trapdoor, ghost, sealed cellar door) should all see mechanical use. This is why you built this location.
- The sealed Dhakaani door in the cellar should hum audibly during the siege. The shard recognizes what's coming. The Forge-Warden's voice grows clearer.
- The party should end Session 10 standing before the open door, the shard warm in their hands, the city quiet behind them — and the weight of what they're about to enter settling over the table.
