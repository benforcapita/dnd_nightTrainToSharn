# Duur'Sharaat — The Lost Workshop

## Overview
**Duur'Sharaat** ("Deep Forge" in the Dhakaani tongue) is a legendary goblin artificer workshop sealed beneath Old Sharn since the War of the Mark. Halas Tarkanan's destruction of Old Sharn buried it deeper than anyone has ever excavated — and the Dhakaani seals have kept it hidden from dragonmarked house archaeologists for centuries.

The workshop contains the **Storm Spire Schema** — a complete blueprint for a device capable of replicating (or countering) the magical phenomenon that created the Mourning. It is the most valuable artifact in Sharn, and possibly in Khorvaire.

## The Seven Keys
Duur'Sharaat is sealed by seven locks, each requiring a different Key. The Keys are **Siberys dragonshard fragments** — pieces of the same crystal that fell from the Ring of Siberys millennia ago. The Dhakaani artificers shattered the original crystal into seven fragments and distributed them among the empire's greatest forge-wardens.

**The party's shard is one of the seven Keys.** The other six are lost — hidden in Dhakaani ruins across Khorvaire, held by dragons of the Chamber, or buried with their forge-wardens. Only one Key is needed to open the main vault, but all seven are needed to unlock the workshop's deepest secrets.

## Entrance Location

The entrance to Duur'Sharaat is in the cellar of **The Dragon's Rest** — a sealed Dhakaani door that opens when both the Key and the shard are present. This is not one of the four seasonal entrances listed below (those were the original design); the siege of the Dragon's Rest (Sessions 9-10) is what opens the way.

### (Original) Alternative Entrance Locations
The entrance to Duur'Sharaat varies depending on which syndicate is the main villain (the season). When a character attuned to the shard approaches the correct location, the shard guides them.

| Season / Villain | Entrance Location |
|---|---|
| **Spring / Boromar** | Beneath the **City of the Dead**, the cliffs of Sharn where the dead are interred |
| **Summer / Tyrants** | Beneath the **Scarlet Flame brothel** in Firelight (Lower Menthis) |
| **Autumn / Tarkanan** | Beneath the **Gorgon's Run** — the abandoned Cannith facility where Tarkanan tests its weapons |
| **Winter / Daask** | Beneath the **Pool of Onatar's Tears** in Blackbones (the Cogs) |

Each entrance is a Dhakaani-sealed portal requiring the shard as a key. Touching the shard to the seal opens a passage downward — deeper than the Cogs, deeper than the Depths, into the ruins of Old Sharn where the workshop was built.

## The Workshop: Dungeon Overview

Duur'Sharaat is a 6-8 room dungeon designed for a party of 4-5 characters at level 4-5. It should take 1-2 sessions to complete.

### Room 1: The Descent

A spiral stairway carved through solid stone. Dhakaani runes line the walls. As the party descends, the runes begin to glow — responding to the shard.

**Environmental Effect:** The deeper they go, the warmer the shard becomes. The air thickens. Arcane casters feel a pressure in their temples — the workshop's magic is still active after millennia.

**Encounter:** A **Dhakaani Welcoming Construct** — a stone golem designed to test worthiness. It doesn't attack immediately. It speaks (in Goblin): *"Speak the name of the forge, or be unmade."*

**Solution:** The shard provides the answer. When held up, it projects the word "Duur'Sharaat" in glowing Dhakaani script. The construct bows and steps aside.

### Room 2: The Ward of Echoes

A long hallway lined with alcoves. In each alcove: a skeleton in Dhakaani ceremonial armor, still standing at attention. The hall is a memorial to the forge-wardens who died here during the War of the Mark.

**Environmental Effect:** The shard resonates with the hall's magic. As the party walks through, they hear fragments of the forge-wardens' final moments — shouted orders, spell incantations, the sound of stone collapsing. The echoes are real enough to require a DC 13 Wisdom save to avoid being overwhelmed by the dying emotions.

**Encounter:** One skeleton is not dead — not fully. A **Forge-Warden Wraith** (use wraith stats, add fire resistance and a once-per-combat *heat metal* spell) guards the hall. It attacks anyone who doesn't bear a Key. The shard-bearer is safe. The others must fight or flee.

### Room 3: The Crucible Chamber

A vast circular room with a central forge pit that still burns — blue flame fueled by bound fire elementals. Ancient Dhakaani smithing tools hang on the walls. Ingots of rare metals (mithral, adamantine, byeshk) sit in orderly rows.

**Treasure:** The ingots are worth 2,000 gp total — but they're heavy (200 lbs minimum). The smithing tools include a set of **Dhakaani Smith's Tools** (grant advantage on checks to repair or create metal items).

**Environmental Effect:** The heat requires a DC 12 Constitution save every 10 minutes or suffer one level of exhaustion. The forge is still functional — the party could theoretically use it to craft something.

**Encounter (Optional):** If the party lingers too long (20+ minutes), a **Fire Elemental** emerges from the forge. It's not hostile by nature — it's confused and angry after millennia of servitude. A DC 15 Charisma (Persuasion) check can calm it. If combat erupts, the fire elemental fights until reduced to half HP, then retreats into the forge.

### Room 4: The Blueprint Gallery

A long corridor with walls covered in Dhakaani schemas — floating blueprints of weapons, tools, and machines. Some are damaged; many are intact.

**Treasure:** A character who spends 1 hour studying the gallery and succeeds on a DC 16 Intelligence (Arcana) check can copy a schema for one of the following:
- **Dhakaani War Bow** — a longbow that deals an additional 1d4 force damage
- **Sentinel Armor** — scale mail that grants advantage on Perception checks to detect hidden creatures
- **Forge-Warden's Hammer** — a warhammer that casts *heat metal* once per day

The gallery also contains historical records — the Dhakaani artificers' final project logs. They reveal that the Storm Spire Schema was created as a *defensive* weapon: a tool to counter daelkyr flesh-warping magic. The schema was sealed because the artificers realized it could be inverted — used to create instead of counter. They feared what their own creation could become.

### Room 5: The Guardian's Gate

The final door before the vault chamber. A massive stone door carved with the image of a Dhakaani forge-warden holding a hammer and a shard.

**The Lock:** The door has a depression shaped exactly like the party's shard. Placing the shard in the depression opens the door — but only if the person placing it has attuned to the shard. Anyone else attempting to use the shard triggers the guardian.

**The Guardian:** A **Stone Golem** (use clay golem stats, increase AC to 18) animates from the door itself. It attacks anyone trying to force the door without proper attunement. If the party has attuned to the shard, the guardian bows and opens the door.

**DM Note:** If the main villain arrives while the party is here, the guardian becomes a tactical asset. The party controls whether the door opens. The villain may try to force it — triggering the guardian against them.

### Room 6: The Vault of the Storm Spire

The heart of Duur'Sharaat. A domed chamber, 60 feet across, with the **Storm Spire Schema** floating at its center — a 15-foot-diameter sphere of light and runes rotating in three dimensions.

**The Schema:** The Storm Spire blueprint. When a character approaches, the schema responds to their presence. It can be "claimed" by any character who touches it and succeeds on a DC 15 Intelligence (Arcana) check — the schema downloads directly into their mind. Once claimed, the floating sphere collapses, and the schema exists only in the claimer's consciousness.

**The Cost:** Claiming the schema deals 4d10 psychic damage (DC 15 Intelligence save for half). The schema is vast — the claimant gains the blueprint but also nightmares, headaches, and the constant sensation of being watched by the forge-wardens. The schema cannot be forgotten or removed except by *wish* or divine intervention.

**Alternative:** The schema can be copied (DC 20 Intelligence (Arcana), 1 hour) instead of claimed. The copy is stable but incomplete — it reveals the general principles but not the exact specifications. Enough to prove the schema exists, but not enough to build the Storm Spire.

**The Vault's Other Treasures:**
- A chest of 5,000 gp in ancient Dhakaani coinage (collector's value: 7,500 gp)
- A **Ring of the Forge-Warden** — grants fire resistance and the ability to cast *identify* at will
- A **Spellshard of Duur'Sharaat** — contains the complete history of the workshop and its artificers
- **Six empty pedestals** — where the other six Keys once rested. They've been removed. Someone — or something — collected them long ago.

### Room 7: The Forge-Warden's Tomb (Secret)

A hidden chamber behind the vault (DC 18 Investigation to find). The tomb of the last Forge-Warden, who sealed herself inside when Halas Tarkanan's destruction collapsed Old Sharn.

**The Forge-Warden's Body:** A goblin woman in ornate ceremonial armor, still holding her forge hammer. A spellshard embedded in her chest contains her final message: *"We sealed the workshop to keep the schema from the War of the Mark. We failed to keep it from the future. Use it better than they would have. Or destroy it. But do not let it gather dust while the world burns. — Forge-Warden Zala."*

**Treasure:**
- **Forge-Warden's Hammer** (+1 warhammer, casts *heat metal* 1/day)
- **Armor of the Last Forge** (+1 half plate, grants advantage on saves against being moved or knocked prone)
- A **sealed scroll case** addressed to "the dragon above" — a message to the Chamber. If opened, it reveals that the Dhakaani knew about the Draconic Prophecy and were actively working to *change* it.

## The Final Confrontation (Post-Siege)

**Note:** In the revised campaign structure, the external faction pressure has already been resolved during the Siege of the Dragon's Rest (Sessions 9-10). The party enters Duur'Sharaat on their own terms. The final confrontation below is preserved as an optional fallback — use it only if a faction managed to follow the party into the dungeon, or if the DM wants to reintroduce an antagonist.

### Original Final Confrontation

When the party exits the vault (or while they're inside it), the main villain arrives with their forces. The villain has been tracking the party — or tracked the shard's activation when the door opened.

### Arrival by Season

| Season | Villain | Forces |
|---|---|---|
| **Spring** | The Rake (Boromar) | 4 halfling thugs, 2 clawfoot riders, 1 magewright accountant |
| **Summer** | The Faceless Council (Tyrants) | 3 changeling assassins, 2 doppelganger bodyguards, 1 charmed beast |
| **Autumn** | Oleander Nightbloom (Tarkanan) | 3 Shroud operatives (aberrant-marked), 2 warforged soldiers, Buzz |
| **Winter** | Niho Koi (Daask) | 2 harpies, 3 winged kobolds, 1 troll |

Additionally, roll 1d4: on a 1-2, a **secondary faction** arrives during the fight, creating a three-way battle.

### Tactical Options for the Party
- **Fight:** The villain is powerful but beatable. This is a boss fight — hard, but fair for a level 5 party.
- **Negotiate:** The villain wants the schema, not the party's deaths. A deal might be possible — but it will cost everything.
- **Destroy:** The party can destroy the schema (DC 18 Arcana to destabilize it, or simply smash the floating sphere with the Forge-Warden's Hammer). This prevents anyone from getting it — but makes enemies of everyone.
- **Escape:** The workshop has secondary tunnels. The party can flee with the schema (or without it) and leave the villain to deal with the guardian.

### Aftermath
- If the party escapes with the schema: Merrix d'Cannith pays. The party is rich, famous, and a target forever.
- If the villain gets the schema: The campaign enters Arc 6 (The Next War) immediately — the party must stop the schema from being used.
- If the schema is destroyed: The shard goes cold. The Prophecy shifts. The dragons notice.
- If the party negotiates a deal: They keep the schema (or a copy) but owe a favor to whoever they dealt with. A favor that will be collected.

## DM Notes
- Duur'Sharaat should feel ancient, sacred, and sad. The Dhakaani weren't villains — they were artisans who feared their own creation.
- The dungeon is not a meat grinder. Many encounters can be bypassed with the shard or with clever negotiation.
- The final confrontation is the campaign's midpoint climax. Everything before was setup. Everything after is consequence.
- Plant seeds for Arc 6-8: the empty pedestals, the scroll case, the Forge-Warden's knowledge of the Prophecy.
