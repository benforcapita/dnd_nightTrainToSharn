# [[The Firefly|Firefly] Train Map

## Overview
[[The Firefly]] is arranged in a linear fashion from front (engine) to rear (observation).
Combat and movement between cars creates tactical opportunities and hazards.

## Car Layout (Front to Rear)

```
[ENGINE] === [CONDUCTOR STONES] === [SECURITY] === [CARGO] === [CREW] === [DINING] === [LOUNGE] === [PASSENGER] === [PASSENGER] === [OBSERVATION]
```

## Movement Between Cars
- **Normal conditions:** DC 10 Acrobatics to move quickly; no check for careful movement
- **During combat:** DC 12 Acrobatics; failure means stuck in junction for the turn
- **During [[Environmental Hazards]]:** DC 14 Acrobatics or Strength (Athletics)
- **Falling:** If a creature falls between cars, they hit the rails. Make a Dexterity save DC 15 or take `6d6 bludgeoning + 2d6 lightning damage` and be thrown from the train.

## Roof Access
- Ladder in each car (Engine and Observation excluded)
- Roof is flat but narrow (2.5 feet wide)
- Moving on roof at speed: DC 14 Strength (Athletics) or fall off
- Fighting on roof: Disadvantage on ranged attacks; advantage on shove attempts

## Strategic Notes
- **The Cargo Car** is the objective for both sides
- **The Security Car** is where [[Marshal Serik ir'Deneith|Serik] ir'Deneith|Serik]] makes his stand
- **The Observation Car** is the safest place to retreat — and the most exposed
- **The Engine** can be sabotaged to stop the train (or destroy it)

## Visual Reference
- The train is painted [[House Orien]] black and emerald
- Lightning arcs constantly beneath the chassis
- At night, the train glows faintly blue from the conductor stones
- The cars are slightly wider than standard lightning rails — "luxury class"
