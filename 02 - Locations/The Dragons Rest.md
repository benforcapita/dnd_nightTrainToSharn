# The Dragon's Rest

## Overview
A three-story tavern built into the span of **Bridge 47** — one of Sharn's countless connector bridges between towers. The Dragon's Rest occupies the bridge itself, with entrances on both sides: one in **Bazaar (Middle Dura)** and one in **Firelight (Lower Menthis)**. It is simultaneously a neighborhood pub, a smuggler's dream, and the worst building on the block to defend.

The party receives the deed after the Firefly incident — either as guilt-compensation from House Orien, a gift from Lady Elira ir'Orien, or as a reward from a grateful NPC whose life they saved on the train.

## The Building

### Main Floor: The Bridge Taproom
The heart of the pub. A long, narrow room that literally spans the bridge — windows on both sides look out over the drop between towers. The bar runs the length of the north wall. Tables crowd the south windows. At the center, a massive hearth with a chimney that rises through all three floors.

**Current State:** Grim. The previous owner was a Cyran refugee who drank the profits. Tables are scarred with knife marks. The hearth hasn't been cleaned in a year. But the bones are good — dwarven stonework, solid oak beams, and the best view of the Dagger River anywhere in the ward.

**Renovation Costs:** ~1,000 gp to fully restore (bar, kitchen, furniture, sign, license). A faction patron might offer to cover it — with strings.

### Upper Floor: The Bridge Rooms
Four rentable rooms for travelers. Each has a window, a bed, and a lock that works (mostly). One room has a secret: a false wall that opens into the bridge's maintenance crawlspace — perfect for smuggling, hiding, or escaping.

**Current State:** Dusty, drafty, but habitable. One room has a bloodstain that won't scrub out. The previous owner never explained it.

### Lower Floor: The Under-Bridge Cellar
A stone cellar built into the bridge's understructure. Cold, dry, perfect for ale storage. Also contains:
- A trapdoor that opens to *nothing* — a 200-foot drop to the lower wards (or a rope ladder escape route)
- A sealed stone door marked with faded Dhakaani runes (the previous owner never opened it; neither should the party — yet)
- Rats. So many rats.

**Current State:** The cellar connects to something older. The sealed door is a plot hook for later (Duur'Sharaat entrance? An old smuggling tunnel? Both?).

### The Belfry
A small turret above the third floor. Originally held signal bells for bridge traffic. Now holds pigeons and a fantastic 360-degree view of the Bazaar and Firelight wards.

**Current State:** The bells are jammed with rust and — on closer inspection — old bone fragments. Someone died up here. The party can restore the bells (4 hours/day for a week with tinker's tools) to use them as an alarm system.

## Why a Bridge Pub Is Perfect

- **Two entrances, two wards, two jurisdictions.** The Sharn Watch in Bazaar doesn't talk to the Watch in Firelight. Clever players can exploit this.
- **Smuggler's dream.** Cargo comes in one door, goes out the other, and technically never "entered" either ward.
- **Hard to siege. Hard to defend.** No single faction can block both entrances simultaneously — but neither can the party.
- **The drop.** Anything (or anyone) that goes out a window falls 200+ feet into the Depths. The perfect disposal method. Also a great assassination vector.

## The Neighbors

| Neighbor | Location | Relationship |
|---|---|---|
| **Emmek d'Ghallanda** | Across the bridge (Bazaar side) | Rival pub owner. Runs the Golden Hearth, a successful Ghallanda establishment. Wants the Dragon's Rest to fail. Will spread rumors, poach staff, and report "code violations." |
| **The Shifters** | Firelight side of the bridge | A small shifter community. Initially wary of newcomers. Leara (Tyrant operative) lives among them. Potential allies or spies. |
| **Riddick the Launderer** | Below the bridge (Bazaar) | Runs a laundry. Washes Boromar coats. Oblivious, bribable, and full of gossip. |
| **The Boromar Drop** | Two bridges over | A gambling den. The Boromar presence is casual but constant. |
| **Watch Sergeant Kael** | Bazaar watchpost | Corrupt but friendly. Takes bribes in the form of free drinks. Actually competent — don't underestimate him. |

## The Ghost
The Dragon's Rest has a ghost. Not a violent one — a sad one.

**The Bell-Ringer:** The previous-previous owner, a dwarf named **Torvin Stonehands**, who was thrown from the belfry during a thieves' standoff 50 years ago. His spirit lingers. He manifests as cold spots, phantom bell tones, and the occasional apparition of a dwarf staring sadly at the hearth.

Torvin is not hostile. He misses the bells. If the party restores them, he becomes a **helpful spirit** — providing warnings about intruders, guiding them to hidden things, and on one critical occasion, shoving an assassin out a window.

**If the party tries to exorcise him:** Torvin *begs* to stay. This is his home. The moral choice is clear — but exorcism is simpler.

## The Rivalry: Emmek d'Ghallanda
Emmek runs the **Golden Hearth**, a successful and well-lit tavern across the bridge. He's a halfling of House Ghallanda with a perfect health-inspection record and a simmering resentment of competition.

**His Tactics:**
- Files complaints with the Bakers' and Brewers' Guild
- Sends shills to start bar fights
- Spreads rumors that the Dragon's Rest is haunted (it is, but he exaggerates)
- Tries to buy the deed from the party at a "generous" price
- Eventually escalates to sabotage — or hires someone who will

**His Secret:** Emmek owes money to the Boromar Clan. He's under pressure to expand or face consequences. The Dragon's Rest is his planned expansion. He's not evil — he's desperate.

## DM Notes
- Let the party love this place. Let them name it (or rename it). Let them hire staff with personalities. Let them build something worth protecting.
- Every staff hire is an opportunity for a faction spy.
- The pub's bridge location makes it a natural crossroads — travelers, traders, and trouble all pass through.
- When the explosion happens in Session 4, it should feel like a violation of *their home*.
- The sealed Dhakaani door in the cellar is a long-term mystery — don't open it until Arc 5 or later.
- The ghost Torvin is a gift to the DM. Use him for tone, for warnings, and for one beautiful moment when the party realizes they're not alone — and never were.

Three stories of weathered stone, soot-stained windows, and a sign that once read 'The Dragon's Rest' before time and weather eroded it to 'The Drag  Rest.'
> 
> The bridge hums beneath your feet — not magic, just the weight of traffic, the vibration of industry, the constant motion of Sharn. On one side, the Bazaar's market noise. On the other, Firelight's perpetual twilight.
> 
> A dwarf-shaped depression in the belfry stone catches your eye. It looks almost like someone was pressed into the stone from a great height.
> 
> Welcome home."*
