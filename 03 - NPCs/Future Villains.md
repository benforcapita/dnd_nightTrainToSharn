# Future Villains

## Overview
[[The Firefly]] incident is only the beginning. These are the powers that will rise to oppose — or manipulate — the players as the campaign unfolds.
Introduce them slowly. Let the players discover their influence before meeting them face to face.

---

## Lord Vex ir'Tharn — [[The Aurum]] Mastermind
- **Role:** [[The Aurum|Aurum]] Concordian; architect of the artifact trade in [[Sharn]]
- **Appearance:** Perfectly groomed, eternally smiling, eyes like a shark's
- **Philosophy:** "Power is not taken. It is acquired, like any other commodity."
- **Threat:** He wants the shard not for its power, but for its leverage. He will trade anything — lives, nations, futures — for the right price.
- **Connection to Session 1:** He may have tipped off [[Captain Ash|Ash]]. He definitely profited from the chaos.

**Hebrew Voice:** Merchant-class Hebrew, polished, every sentence a negotiation.
> *"אתם נושאים משהו ששייך לי. אני לא כועס. אני רק רוצה לדבר על תנאים."*
("You are carrying something that belongs to me. I am not angry. I only want to discuss terms.")

---

## The Lord of Blades (Distant Threat)
- **Role:** Warforged revolutionary; leader of a cult that believes warforged are the next dominant species
- **Appearance:** A towering warforged with blades fused to his arms — more myth than man
- **Philosophy:** "The flesh-built world is dying. We are the replacement."
- **Threat:** If the shard can enhance or empower warforged, he will tear [[Sharn]] apart to find it.
- **Connection to Session 1:** One of [[The Raiders]] may have been a sympathizer. Or one of the players' warforged contacts.

**Hebrew Voice:** None — he does not speak. He broadcasts. His voice is a machine grinding out prophecy.

---

## Keeper Malachi — The Emerald Claw
- **Role:** Agent of the Order of the Emerald Claw; undead fanatic
- **Appearance:** Pale, aristocratic, smells of formaldehyde and roses
- **Philosophy:** "Karrnath will rise again. The dead do not forget. The dead do not forgive."
- **Threat:** The shard may have properties related to the creation of undead. Or the prevention of them. Either way, Malachi wants it.
- **Connection to Session 1:** He has agents in [[Thronehold]] and [[Sharn]] watching the train.

**Hebrew Voice:** Archaic, poetic, every sentence sounds like a funeral oration.
> *"המוות הוא לא סוף. הוא מדרגה. והמדרגה הבאה... טובה יותר."*
("Death is not an end. It is a step. And the next step... is better.")

---

## Cipher — [[The Twelve]]'s Shadow
- **Role:** An anonymous operative within [[The Twelve]]; identity unknown even to other members
- **Appearance:** Unknown. Possibly multiple people using the same title.
- **Philosophy:** "The dragonmarked houses must evolve. Those who resist evolution are weeds."
- **Threat:** Cipher wants the shard to consolidate draconic power — or destroy a rival house.
- **Connection to Session 1:** Cipher authorized [[The Statue]] transport. Cipher may have sent [[Professor Nymm]] the information. Cipher is watching.

**Hebrew Voice:** Genderless, synthesized, every word measured and cold.
> *"אתם לא רואים אותי. אין לי שם. אך אני רואה אתכם. והפרטים שאני אוסף... הם כסף."*
("You do not see me. I have no name. But I see you. And the details I collect... are currency.")

---

## The Keeper of [[Sharn|The Cogs]]
- **Role:** A mysterious figure in [[Sharn]]'s undercity; possibly a dragon, possibly a mad prophet, possibly both
- **Appearance:** No one who sees them clearly survives to describe them
- **Philosophy:** "The [[Prophecy Fragments|Draconic Prophecy]] is not a book. It is a conversation. And you have just joined it."
- **Threat:** The shard has awakened something. The Keeper knows what.
- **Connection to Session 1:** [[The Statue]] was headed to [[Sharn]] for a reason. [[Sharn|The Cogs]] were its destination.

**Hebrew Voice:** Echoing, layered, as if spoken by multiple throats at once.
> *"השבר שאתם נושאים הוא לא אבן. הוא מילה. וכל מילה בנבואה... גורל."*
("The fragment you carry is not a stone. It is a word. And every word in the prophecy... is fate.")

---

## DM Notes
- Reveal villains slowly — rumors first, agents second, faces last
- Each villain should feel inevitable, not random
- Let villains be right about some things; moral ambiguity keeps players engaged
- Consider giving each villain a personal connection to a player character
