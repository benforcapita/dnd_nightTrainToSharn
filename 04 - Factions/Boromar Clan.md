# Boromar Clan

## Overview
Sharn's oldest and most entrenched criminal syndicate. Halfling-run, dinosaur-riding, and deeply woven into the City Council. The Boromar Clan has run Sharn's underworld for generations through a combination of bribery, smuggling, gambling, and the occasional assassination dressed as an accident.

They are the devil the city knows — and prefers.

## Philosophy
"Everything has a price. We just know what yours is."

The Boromar Clan doesn't start wars. They end them — profitably. They believe in stability, because chaos is bad for business. If they control the crime, they control the peace. And if they control the peace, they control the city.

## Structure
- **The Boromar Family** — halfling blood ties, including the aging patriarch Saiden Boromar (on the City Council)
- **The Rake** — the Clan's spymaster and enforcer, who handles the dirty work Saiden pretends not to know about. The Rake is the main villain if the Boromar Clan is the primary antagonist
- **Corrin Delmaco** — a rising star in the Clan, more pragmatic than cruel. A potential ally or patron
- **The Clawfoot Riders** — halfling cavalry mounted on trained clawfoot dinosaurs; terrifying in enclosed spaces
- **Hrabbaz** — a half-orc enforcer and loan shark; muscle with brains

## Territory
- **Bazaar (Middle Dura)** — the economic heart of their operations
- **Firelight (Lower Menthis)** — entertainment, gambling, the King of Fire nightclub
- **Little Plains (Middle Menthis)** — Talenta halfling community, Clan roots
- **Skyway** — the Cloud Dragon restaurant, where the elite make deals they can't make in public

## Role in the Campaign

### As the Main Villain
The Boromar Clan wants the schema to sell to the highest bidder. They don't want to use it — they want to profit from it. The Rake negotiates with Karrnath, Droaam, and the Lord of Blades simultaneously, playing all sides. If they get the schema, Sharn's underworld becomes Khorvaire's great power broker — and Saiden Boromar becomes the most powerful person on the continent.

### As a Secondary Faction
They offer the party protection, money, and underworld access. Everything comes with strings, but the strings are clearly labeled. They can be trusted to act in their own interest — which means they can be predicted.

### Patron Approach
Corrin Delmaco visits the Dragon's Rest with a bottle of Aundairian wine and a proposal: "We watch your back. You watch ours. When the time comes, you remember who treated you like family."

## Key Members
- **Saiden Boromar** — aging patriarch, City Council member, grandfatherly smile, ice-cold eyes
- **The Rake** — spymaster and enforcer; charming, dangerous, always three moves ahead
- **Corrin Delmaco** — pragmatic operator; genuinely likes the party, but loyalty to the Clan comes first
- **Hrabbaz** — half-orc muscle; surprisingly philosophical, unshakably violent
- **Morkos of Mror** — bartender at the King of Fire; knows everyone's secrets, paid to keep them

## Boromar Clan as Villain — Encounter Chain (Spring)

The schema has passed through: Hrabbaz → Sundry's Pawnshop → a Lyrandar skycoach pilot → House Jorasco → Aurora Gallery auction.

### Encounter Path
1. **Find Hrabbaz** — any tavern. DC 16 Charisma check or fight. He carries a spellshard with a map.
2. **Sundry's Pawnshop (Bazaar)** — Sundry sold the artifact to a Lyrandar heir; Daask showed up looking for it too
3. **Skycoach Chase** — The Lyrandar pilot works for Hruitt, an owl-shifter city council member. Board the skycoach mid-flight while Daask harpies attack
4. **House Jorasco** — Hruitt takes the party to the House of Healing; Boromar halflings eavesdrop
5. **Aurora Gallery (Platinum Heights)** — The artifact is being auctioned. The party must outbid, steal, or negotiate
6. **Cloud Dragon Heist (Skyway)** — If the auction fails, the Clan has taken the artifact to the Cloud Dragon. Break in or negotiate

### Weather: Heavy Rain
Disadvantage on Perception (sight/hearing). Open flames extinguished. Visibility 60 ft. Rain falls noon to midnight. Fog midnight to noon.

### Finale Location: King of Fire Nightclub (Firelight)
The Rake's headquarters. A multi-level nightclub with a stage, VIP balcony, security rooms, and a hidden vault. Behind the bar: the first components of the Storm Spire — the Rake is already trying to build it.
