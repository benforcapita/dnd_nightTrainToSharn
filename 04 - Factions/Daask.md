# Daask

## Overview
The monstrous fist of Droaam in Sharn. Daask is less a crime syndicate and more an invasion force wearing a gang's clothing. Founded by the Daughters of Sora Kell, Daask operates with the backing of a nation of monsters — harpies, medusas, trolls, ogres, and worse.

They are not subtle. They are not patient. They are not afraid. And they are growing.

## Philosophy
"We were made to rule. You were made to submit."

Daask doesn't negotiate — they demand. They don't bribe — they threaten. They view the other syndicates as temporary inconveniences and the city of Sharn as a future territory of Droaam. Every crime they commit is a memo to Breland: *you cannot stop us.*

## Structure
- **The Daughters of Sora Kell** — Sora Teraza, Sora Katra, and Sora Maenya; the hag rulers of Droaam who direct Daask's long-term strategy
- **Niho Koi** — an oni mage, Daask's chief operative in Sharn. Cunning, powerful, and utterly devoted to the Daughters. The main villain if Daask is the primary antagonist
- **Garra** — a gnoll enforcer; brutal, loyal, and not as stupid as she looks
- **The Monstrous Ranks** — harpies, medusas, trolls, ogres, minotaurs, harpies, and worse. Daask doesn't recruit — they *breed*
- **Khyber's Gate Network** — underground routes through the Cogs and Depths that Daask uses to move forces unseen

## Territory
- **The Cogs** — industrial underbelly, Daask's primary territory
- **Khyber's Gate** — monster community beneath Sharn; Daask's homeland in the city
- **Ashblack** — heavy industry; Daask's forge and armory
- **Blackbones** — the Pool of Onatar's Tears; a site of monstrous pilgrimage
- **Malleon's Gate** — goblinoid district with Daask sympathies

## Role in the Campaign

### As the Main Villain
Daask wants the schema to arm Droaam's monster legions for continental conquest. The Storm Spire — a device that controls elemental storms — would give Droaam the power to challenge the Five Nations militarily. Niho Koi dreams of the day Sharn's towers fall and monsters rule the ruins.

### As a Secondary Faction
They offer muscle without morality. They don't ask for loyalty — they demand obedience. They are useful as a wrecking ball against other factions, but alliance with Daask means accepting that innocent people will die.

### Patron Approach
Niho Koi appears at the Dragon's Rest in the form of a beautiful half-elf — then drops the illusion. "You have something we want. We have something you need: the power to survive what's coming. The Daughters are watching, little mortals. Don't disappoint them."

## Key Members
- **Niho Koi** — oni mage; shapeshifter, spellcaster, strategic genius; covered in magical tattoos that grant devastating abilities
- **Garra** — gnoll enforcer; runs Daask's street operations
- **Braala** — retired hobgoblin prizefighter in Malleon's Gate; moves artifacts for Daask
- **The Harpy Chorus** — Daask's aerial surveillance and strike force; their song precedes raids
- **The Duergar Courier** — an unnamed duergar with a tentacle whip who moves sensitive cargo through the Cogs

## Daask as Villain — Encounter Chain (Winter)

The schema has passed through: Braala → warforged courier → Sharn Watch custody → duergar courier → Pool of Onatar's Tears → House Jorasco → Hruitt's skycoach → Aurora Gallery.

### Encounter Path
1. **Braala (Malleon's Gate)** — retired prizefighter who handed off the artifact to a warforged
2. **The Cogs / Ashblack** — the warforged was arrested by the Sharn Watch (tipped by Boromar). Interrupt a torture session.
3. **Pool of Onatar's Tears (Blackbones)** — a duergar took the artifact here seeking a "blessing." The temple forge priest provides directions.
4. **House Jorasco (High Walls)** — the duergar went for healing. High Walls is on lockdown drill — no one leaves.
5. **Skycoach Chase** — Hruitt's skycoach, Daask harpies and winged kobolds attack
6. **Aurora Gallery (Platinum Heights)** — the artifact goes to auction

### Weather: Heavy Snow
Snow blankets Sharn. Airship traffic increases (elemental heat keeps them clear). Everbright lanterns doubled for Long Shadows season. Visibility reduced. Cold damage risks for unprepared characters.

### Finale Location: Storm Spire (beneath the Cogs)
Daask isn't waiting to auction the schema — they're building the Storm Spire *right now* in the industrial depths beneath Sharn. The party must descend into the Cogs, fight through Daask's territory, and destroy the half-built weapon before it activates.
