# The Tyrants

## Overview
A criminal syndicate of doppelgangers, changelings, and shapeshifters. The Tyrants are the spies of Sharn's underworld — information brokers, identity thieves, and blackmail artists. They don't fight wars. They don't claim territory. They claim *people*, one stolen identity at a time.

No one knows how many Tyrants exist. No one knows who is a Tyrant and who isn't. The paranoia they generate is itself a weapon.

## Philosophy
"Identity is a commodity. We are the market."

The Tyrants don't want to rule Sharn. They want to own the *idea* of Sharn — to be so embedded in every faction, every house, every institution that the city cannot function without them. Every secret they learn is leverage. Every identity they steal is capital. Every faction is a customer, whether they know it or not.

## Structure
- **The Faceless Council** — the Tyrants' leadership; their true identities unknown even to each other. They communicate through dead drops, intermediaries, and impersonated strangers
- **Leara** — a shifter face for the Tyrants who operates openly in the shifter communities; a potential patron contact. She appears as whatever the viewer most trusts
- **The Diamond Theater Troupe** — a legitimate theater company (House Phiarlan-adjacent) that the Tyrants use as a front for artifact movement and identity exchange
- **The Mask Network** — changelings embedded in every faction, every house, and possibly the City Council itself

## Territory
- **They are everywhere.**
- **Lower Northedge** — shifter communities, Leara's base
- **Diamond Theater (Downstairs, Lower Menthis)** — performance venue and artifact exchange point
- **Bear's Rest (North Market)** — a tavern that serves as a Tyrant dead drop
- **Hollow Tower (Hareth's Folly, Middle Dura)** — Carnival of Shadows rehearsal space

## Role in the Campaign

### As the Main Villain
The Tyrants want to auction the schema to *all* parties simultaneously — Karrnath, Droaam, the Lord of Blades, the Aurum, the Twelve, and whoever else will pay. They don't want to build the Storm Spire. They want to sell the *blueprint* to everyone who can build it and let them destroy each other. The chaos creates infinite opportunities for identity theft, infiltration, and profit.

### As a Secondary Faction
They offer information — the most valuable currency in Sharn. Need to know who really runs a particular ward? The Tyrants know. Need an identity that will get you into a Cannith facility? The Tyrants can provide it. But they never give without receiving — every deal with the Tyrants means giving them another piece of yourself.

### Patron Approach
Leara appears as someone the party trusts implicitly — their old commander, a dead friend, each other. Once the illusion drops, she smiles. "We've been watching since you stepped off that train. You're interesting. We like interesting. Let's talk about mutual benefit."

## Key Members
- **The Faceless Council** — never seen; never the same
- **Leara** — shifter operative, Tyrant recruiter, face of the organization when they need one
- **The Ringmaster** — House Phiarlan ringmaster who moves Tyrant artifacts through Carnival of Shadows performances
- **Dolessen the Smoking Flute** — a dragonborn bard who can soothe beasts and extract secrets
- **Daca** — a cryptic gnome at Daca's Watch who speaks in riddles and knows where everything is hidden

## The Tyrants as Villain — Encounter Chain (Summer)

The schema has passed through: Leara → Bear's Rest → Ringmaster → chimera → Daca's Watch → Dolessen → Diamond Theater.

### Encounter Path
1. **Bear's Rest (Lower Northedge)** — Leara gave the artifact to a Phiarlan ringmaster
2. **Hollow Tower (Middle Dura)** — Carnival of Shadows rehearsal. A changeling-infiltrated circus. A spooked chimera steals the artifact.
3. **Daca's Watch (Middle Northedge)** — a cryptic gnome hoarded by the chimera; speaks in riddles about Dolessen
4. **Broken Anchor (Middle Menthis)** — Dolessen the Smoking Flute can soothe the beast
5. **Chimera Chase** — the beast drops the artifact; Tyrant changelings snatch it and flee through Sharn's bridges
6. **Diamond Theater (Lower Menthis)** — the artifact is a stage prop in a performance. The audience is all shapeshifters. The play is a trap.

### Weather: Heat Wave
Daytime temperatures swelter. Tempers flare — DC 12 Wisdom saves to avoid violent impulses during social encounters. Exhaustion risks for heavy armor. The heat makes everyone irritable — perfect cover for Tyrant manipulations.

### Finale Location: Diamond Theater (Lower Menthis)
A multi-level theater where the audience, the cast, and the crew are all Tyrants in various stolen identities. The artifact is the centerpiece prop. The party must navigate a building full of shapeshifters who can look like anyone — including each other. The final confrontation is a nightmare of mistaken identity.
