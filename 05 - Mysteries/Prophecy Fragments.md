# Prophecy Fragments

## Overview
The Draconic Prophecy is not a single text but a pattern — written in the stars, the weather, the arrangement of dragonshards, and the course of history.
[[The Siberys Shard]] is a fragment of that pattern. A word. A note. A warning.

## What the Players Might Learn
Over time, through research, NPC whispers, and direct experience, the players may discover:

### Fragment 1 — The Watcher Dreams
> *"When the three moons align above the broken tower, the Watcher shall wake and the Dreamer shall sleep. What was bound shall be loosed, and what was loosed shall seek the sky."*

**Possible meaning:** [[The Statue]] (the Watcher) and the shard (the Dreamer) are connected. When separated, something changes. Something dangerous.

### Fragment 2 — The [[Timeline|Mourning]]'s Echo
> *"The silver city wept crystal tears, and the war ended not with victory but with silence. The tears fell into darkness, and in darkness they remember."*

**Possible meaning:** The [[Timeline|Mourning]] and the shard are related. The shard may be a "memory" of what destroyed Cyre.

### Fragment 3 — The Houses of Shadow
> *"Twelve candles cast thirteen shadows. The brightest flame feeds the darkest corner. When the merchant becomes the king, the sentinel shall fall."*

**Possible meaning:** [[The Twelve]] (or the dragonmarked houses) are part of the Prophecy. Their rise to power was predicted — or caused.

## How to Use Prophecy
- Prophecies should be ambiguous — multiple interpretations are valid
- Let players argue about what they mean
- Sometimes the Prophecy is self-fulfilling — because people believe it, they make it true
- Dragons do not share prophecy freely. If a dragon speaks to the players about this, they are already in terrible danger.
