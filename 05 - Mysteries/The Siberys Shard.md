# The Siberys Shard — Heart of Duur'Sharaat

## What The Party Knows
The shard was hidden inside [[The Statue]]. [[The Raiders]] were specifically targeting it. The shard radiates faint magical energy. Nobody aboard [[The Firefly]] officially acknowledged its existence.

> **Hebrew / עברית:**
> השבר הוטמן בתוך הפסל. השודדים היו מכוונים ספציפית אליו. השבר פולט אנרגיה קסומה עמומה. אף אחד על גחלילית לא הודה בקיומו רשמית.

## What Is Actually True
The shard is a **power source**, not a key. It is the *Heart of Duur'Sharaat* — the living energy core that once powered the greatest Dhakaani workshop beneath Old Sharn.

The actual **Key** to Duur'Sharaat is a separate artifact, hidden elsewhere in the city. The Dhakaani Forge-Warden who sealed the workshop deliberately separated the Key from its power source. Without the shard, the Key is inert — a beautifully crafted piece of Dhakaani metalwork with no function. With the shard, it can open the vault and activate whatever lies within.

The shard contains crystallized fragments of the Draconic Prophecy and carries the consciousness-imprint of the Forge-Warden who gave their life to charge it.

## The Two-Half Dynamic
- **The Shard (party has it):** Power without purpose. The party can feel it pulling toward something beneath the city, but without the Key, they can't reach it.
- **The Key (hidden in Sharn):** Purpose without power. Whoever finds it holds the door — but can't turn the lock. The factions will race for it. The party needs to get there first, or at least prevent any one faction from holding both halves.
- **Together:** The vault opens. The Storm Spire Schema is accessible. The Prophecy advances.

## Campaign Implications
- The party starts as accidental couriers, not targets. Factions don't know they have the shard — and even if they suspect, the shard alone isn't enough to open the vault.
- The real heat begins when the race for the Key goes public (Sessions 5-8). Factions hunt the Key. The party hunts the Key. Whoever finds it may not realize they still need the shard.
- Once a faction acquires the Key and realizes they need the shard to activate it, the party becomes the most hunted people in Sharn. The dynamic flips from "race" to "siege."
- If the party gets the Key first, they hold both halves — and every faction in the city comes for them at once.

## The Key's Location
The Key is hidden in [[Sharn]]. Possible locations (DM picks or rolls):
- **The Depths (the Cogs):** Sealed in a Dhakaani ruin beneath a foundry — claimed by Daask as territory
- **Old Sharn (Fall):** Buried in the collapsed ruins of the ancient goblin city, beneath the foundations of modern Sharn
- **Morgrave University:** Catalogued and forgotten in a sub-basement archive — mislabeled as "Dhakaani ritual object #447"
- **A private collection:** Owned by a noble who has no idea what it is — the Aurum or the Tyrants may have already identified it
- **The Draconic Prophecy chamber:** Hidden in a Chamber of the Dragonmarked vault; the dragons have been waiting for someone to bring the shard

## Campaign Possibilities (Updated)
- **[[Prophecy Fragments|Draconic Prophecy]] connection** — the shard contains fragments of the Prophecy; when it powers the Key, the next word will be spoken
- **Ancient [[The Statue|Dhakaani]] seal** — [[The Statue]] was built to transport the shard safely, not contain it. The [[The Statue|Dhakaani]] wanted it kept far from the Key
- **Cannith interest** — House Cannith wants the shard as a power source for new warforged; they may not even know about the Key
- **Dragonmarked enhancement** — the shard might amplify or alter dragonmarks; the houses would kill for this
- **Two-part race structure** — the campaign naturally splits: (1) the race for the Key through Sharn's wards, (2) the siege when factions realize who holds the power
- **Resonance with the [[Timeline|Mourning]]** — the shard hums at a frequency that matches descriptions of the [[Timeline|Mourning]]'s edge

## IMPORTANT
Do not fully explain the shard early. Mystery is more important than answers.
Let the players speculate. Let them fear it. Let them covet it.
The truth should be earned, not given.

The Key should be introduced as a rumor, then a trail, then a target. Let Session 4's explosion be the moment the party realizes they have *half* of something enormous — and the other half is out there, with everyone hunting it.
