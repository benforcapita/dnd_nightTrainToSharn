# War Trauma Hooks

## Overview
The [[Timeline|Last War]] broke everyone in different ways. These hooks help players roleplay the psychological aftermath without making the game miserable.
Trauma is not a gimmick. It is a lens through which the character sees the world.
Use these to create moments of connection, vulnerability, and growth.

## Flashback Triggers

### The Sound of Thunder
Your character associates loud noises with artillery. When thunder rolls or something explodes nearby, they must make a **Wisdom save (DC 12)** or be overwhelmed for a round — unable to act, only react.
**Growth moment:** The first time they stand their ground through thunder without faltering.

### The Smell of Burning
Your character remembers a battlefield where warforged incinerators turned a forest to ash. The smell of smoke or burning triggers nausea and rage.
**Growth moment:** Using fire as a tool instead of fleeing from it as a memory.

### The Empty Chair
Your character sets an extra place at meals. For a friend who died. For a unit that no longer exists.
Other characters notice. No one asks. But someone eventually sits there. And it means something.

### The Names Before Sleep
Your character whispers names before sleeping — the names of the dead. Not a prayer. A roll call.
If interrupted, they cannot sleep properly. If they finish, they sleep deeply.
**Growth moment:** Adding a new name — someone they saved — to the list.

## Behavioral Hooks

### Hypervigilance
You always sit with your back to the wall. You count exits in every room. You know where everyone's weapons are.
**In play:** Advantage on Perception checks to notice threats. Disadvantage on checks to relax or trust.

### Survivor's Guilt
You don't believe you deserve the peace others died for. You take unnecessary risks. You volunteer for danger.
**In play:** Once per session, you can choose to take damage intended for an ally. You cannot explain why. You just do.

### Emotional Numbness
Joy and grief feel distant, like watching someone else's life. Violence feels immediate.
**In play:** Advantage on saves against fear. Disadvantage on Persuasion checks requiring warmth or empathy.

### Startle Response
You flinch when touched unexpectedly. You reach for a weapon that isn't there.
**In play:** If surprised, you draw a weapon automatically (if wearing one). This can create social complications.

## Shared Trauma Moments
- **The Song:** Two characters realize they know the same marching song from different sides of the war. They sing it together. Different lyrics. Same melody.
- **The Scar:** Comparing wounds. Not as competition — as cartography. "This one is Saerun Road. This one is a bar fight in Wroat. This one... I don't remember getting."
- **The Dream:** Both characters dream of the same battlefield on the same night. They wake up and look at each other differently.
