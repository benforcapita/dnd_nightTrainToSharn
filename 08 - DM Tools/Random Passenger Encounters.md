# Random Passenger Encounters

## How to Use
Roll d12 when players wander the train, look for someone to talk to, or you need to populate a car with a face. Each passenger comes with a **secret** — something a DC 14 Insight check or clever roleplay might uncover.

Combine with [[Improvised NPC Names]] for a full NPC on the fly.

## Passengers (d12)

| d12 | Passenger | What They Show | What They Hide |
|-----|-----------|----------------|-----------------|
| 1 | **The Journalist** — a sharp-eyed half-elf with a notepad that never leaves their hand. From the *Korranberg Chronicle*. | Friendly, professional curiosity. *"Just writing a piece on the maiden voyage. Human interest."* | They're investigating House Orien's finances. Someone tipped them off about "off-the-books cargo." They'll trade information for information. |
| 2 | **The Deserting Soldier** — a human in civilian clothes that don't quite fit. Sits rigidly, scans every entrance. | Polite but evasive. *"Visiting family in Sharn."* | They're AWOL from the Brelish army. Their unit was ordered to do something they couldn't live with. The military police are waiting at Terminus Station. |
| 3 | **The Cannith Clerk** — a young gnome with ink-stained fingers and a manifest scroll they check obsessively. | Nervous, officious. *"I'm just here to verify the cargo at arrival. Routine. Completely routine."* | They've been ordered to falsify the cargo report. The crate doesn't contain what the manifest says. They don't know what it DOES contain — and they're terrified to find out. |
| 4 | **The Widow** — an older dwarf woman in mourning grey, traveling alone with a small chest. | Quiet, dignified, appreciates company. Offers hard candies from a tin. | The chest contains her husband's war medals — and a journal detailing the massacre of his unit by "friendly fire" ordered by a Deneith commander. She's going to Sharn to publish it. |
| 5 | **The Fixer** — a smooth-talking changeling in an ever-shifting face, currently wearing the guise of a Brelish merchant. | Charming, generous, buys drinks. *"I'm in acquisitions. If you ever need something — or need something to disappear — I know people."* | They're a Boromar Clan operative, on the train to make contact with the raiders. They don't know the heist is about to happen — but they'll recognize the opportunity when it does. |
| 6 | **The Priest** — a Silver Flame acolyte, human, young, visibly out of their depth among luxury. | Earnest, kind, slightly annoying. Offers blessings. Asks too many questions about the war. | They're not a real priest. They're a deserter who stole the robes. The real acolyte's body is in a ditch near Thronehold. Guilt is eating them alive. |
| 7 | **The Collector** — an impeccably dressed elf with a Dragonmarked House signet ring (not Orien, not Deneith — Phiarlan?). | Aloof, cultured, bored. *"I travel for art. The journey is the only interesting part."* | They're a spy. Not for any House — freelance. They've been hired by the Aurum to confirm whether "the package" is real. They'll vanish during the heist and reappear in Sharn with a job offer. |
| 8 | **The Engineer's Daughter** — a teenage human girl who keeps trying to sneak toward the engine car. | Rebellious, curious, quick with a lie. *"My father works on the train. I just wanted to see it."* | Her father DID work on the train — as one of the elemental binders. He died three weeks ago in an "accident" in the engine room. She's here to find out what really happened. She has his notes. |
| 9 | **The Veteran's Buddy** — a scarred half-orc who recognizes one of the players. *"Saerun Road? No... Vathirond, right? I remember your unit. You held the bridge."* | Warm, nostalgic, wants to drink and swap war stories. | They're not a veteran. They're a Deneith intelligence operative, planted to get close to the "honored guests" and assess whether any of them are security risks. They're very, very good at their job. |
| 10 | **The Courier** — a wiry human with a locked satchel chained to their wrist. Never lets go of it. | Professional, curt. *"I don't open the bag. I don't know what's inside. I just deliver."* | The satchel contains a letter from Lord ir'Wynarn of Breland to a contact in Sharn — authorizing "extraordinary measures" to secure any artifacts recovered from the Firefly. If the players steal or read it, they learn they're being hunted before they arrive. |
| 11 | **The Artist** — a kalashtar painter, staring out the observation car window with unfocused eyes. | Dreamy, philosophical. *"The landscape looks like a wound healing. Don't you think? The war carved lines into the earth. Now the grass fills them in. But the shape is still there."* | They're not just philosophizing. They can sense the shard — not consciously, but as a presence pressing on the edge of their dreams. Their sketches, if examined, show the same symbol over and over: a circle with a crack through it. The shard's mark. |
| 12 | **The Chef** — a halfling with flour on their apron and a cleaver that looks well-used for more than vegetables. | Boisterous, generous, insists players try the soup. *"House Orien hired me for the maiden voyage. It's an honor! The pay is terrible."* | They're a former Boromar enforcer who went straight. They recognize the Fixer (result 5). They'll quietly warn a player: *"That one's trouble. I know the type. Watch your back."* If the heist goes badly, they'll fight — and they're very good with that cleaver. |

| 2 | Has a nervous cough that gets worse near the cargo car |

| 6 | Keeps touching a scar on their forearm when they lie |

## Hebrew Notes

> **נוסעים אקראיים** — הטילו d12 כשהשחקנים משוטטים ברכבת ומחפשים עם מי לדבר. כל נוסע מגיע עם חזות וסוד. חברו עם שמות מאולתרים ל-NPC מלא על המקום.

> **טיפ:** אל תשתמשו ביותר מדי נוסעים. 2-3 מספיקים לסשן. תנו לכל אחד רגע לזרוח. האירוע המרכזי (השוד) יגיע בכל מקרה — הנוסעים האלה בונים את העולם שלפני הפיצוץ.
