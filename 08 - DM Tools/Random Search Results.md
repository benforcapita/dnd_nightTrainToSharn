# Random Search Results

## How to Use
Roll d20 when players search a fallen enemy, a passenger's abandoned luggage, a crew compartment, or a noble's cabin. Each result includes what they find and a story fragment attached to it. Nothing in this table is critical to the plot — everything adds texture.

Works alongside the guaranteed loot in [[Session 1 Loot and Consequences]].

## Searching Bodies & Belongings (d20)

| d20 | What You Find | Story |
|-----|--------------|-------|
| 1 | **A letter, never sent.** Folded and refolded a hundred times. Addressed to someone in a town that no longer exists. | The ink is smeared in places — rain, or tears. The last line: *"I'm coming home. Wait for me."* The date is from three years ago. |
| 2 | **A House Orien signet ring.** Gold, but the crest has been deliberately scratched off. | Belonged to a disgraced Orien official. Whoever carried it wasn't supposed to have it. A DC 14 History check recognizes the scratched crest: the official was executed for treason during the war. |
| 3 | **Three dragon's teeth.** Actual dragon teeth — small, from a wyrmling, strung on a leather cord. | They're warm to the touch. An Arcana check (DC 15) reveals faint divination magic: the teeth were used for fortune-telling by a tribe in the Shadow Marches. They still whisper when held. |
| 4 | **A deck of marked cards.** Three-Dragon Ante deck with tiny pinpricks in the corners. | The marks are professional, almost invisible. The deck belonged to a gambler who was doing too well in the first-class lounge. The Deneith guards were about to have words with them. |
| 5 | **A vial of alchemist's fire.** Wrapped in silk, hidden in a boot. | Not unusual for an adventurer. What's unusual is the label: *Property of House Cannith — Experimental Division.* The formula is unstable. On a hit, it deals `2d6 fire` instead of `1d4`, but the thrower must make a DC 12 Dexterity save or take `1d4 fire` from splashback. |
| 6 | **A child's drawing.** Crayon on torn paper. A family — mother, father, child, all holding hands. The father figure has been crossed out in heavy black strokes. | Found in a raider's pocket. Even raiders have kids. Even raiders have exes. The drawing smells faintly of smoke. |
| 7 | **A silver mirror, cracked.** Small, ornate, elven craftsmanship. | The crack runs exactly through the center. When a player looks into it, their reflection seems... delayed. A fraction of a second behind. A DC 13 Arcana check detects faint illusion magic — this mirror was used to practice disguises. |
| 8 | **A key that fits nothing on the train.** Iron, old, with a bow shaped like a screaming face. | It's heavy for its size. The metal is cold even when held. A History check (DC 16) identifies the screaming face as Dhakaani — a ward against the daelkyr. Whatever this key opens, it's not on this train. |
| 9 | **Love letters. Seven of them.** Tied with a blue ribbon. All from the same person. All unopened. | They're addressed to a passenger who isn't on the manifest — but their handwriting matches the manifest entries. The passenger was traveling under a false name. The letters are from someone in Karrnath. The last one is postmarked a week ago. |
| 10 | **A war medal.** Brelish Silver Star for Valor. The name on the back has been scratched off. | The medal is real — but the bearer didn't earn it. They bought it from a veteran who needed drinking money. They wear it to board the train, to sit in the first-class car, to pretend they belong. The name underneath the scratches: *Sergeant Aldric Kern.* |
| 11 | **A sending stone.** One of a pair. It's warm. Active. | Someone is listening. A DC 15 Perception check picks up faint breathing from the stone. If the player speaks into it, a voice responds: *"You're not who I was expecting. Who are you?"* The other stone is in Sharn. |
| 12 | **A poisoner's kit.** Three vials, neatly labeled in gnomish script: *Sleep, Forget, Comply.* | One vial is half-empty. Someone on this train has already been dosed. Which vial? With what effect? The kit's owner is either the Fixer passenger ([[Random Passenger Encounters]] #5) or someone more dangerous. |
| 13 | **A map of the train.** Hand-drawn, with annotations in Dwarvish: *"Cargo seal here — arcane, Class IV. Bypass through ventilation shaft. Guard rotation: every 2 hours. Marshal's quarters: avoid."* | The handwriting is Captain Ash's. This is a raider briefing document. It lists the guard rotation times — the heist is planned for the next rotation gap. It also has a note: *"Non-combatants: do not engage unless necessary."* |
| 14 | **A jar of lightning.** Literally — a glass jar with a tiny arc of electricity suspended inside. Faintly warm. | A souvenir from the conductor stone array. An Arcana check (DC 14): it's harmless, but it's also a minor elemental fragment. If the jar breaks, the spark escapes and the nearest metal object becomes electrified for one minute (`1d4 lightning` on touch). |
| 15 | **A signet ring from a House that doesn't exist.** The crest shows a dragonmark that isn't one of the Twelve — something older, stranger. | A History check (DC 18): this is the Mark of Death, the lost dragonmark of House Vol. The bloodline was exterminated thousands of years ago. This ring should not exist. It's warm against the skin and pulses like a heartbeat. |
| 16 | **A small pouch of dragonshards.** Three Eberron shards, rose-pink, worth about 50 gp total. | Standard currency for anyone dealing in arcane goods. But one shard has a hairline crack running through it — and a faint inscription in Draconic: *"Property of the Aurum — Return to Midden Lane, Sharn."* Someone will come looking for these. |
| 17 | **A journal.** Leather-bound, half-filled. The entries start mundane — weather, food, complaints about travel. Then they change. | The handwriting becomes erratic. The last entries: *"The humming is worse at night. I asked the attendant and she said she couldn't hear it. I'm the only one. Why am I the only one? There's something in the engine. It knows I can hear it. It's been talking to me."* |
| 18 | **A set of manacles.** Deneith-issue, magically reinforced. The key is still in the lock. | They're warm. Recently used. A Perception check (DC 12) notices fresh scratches inside the cuffs — someone struggled. Whoever was in these is now loose on the train. |
| 19 | **A charcoal portrait.** A sketch of one of the players. Recent — they're wearing the same clothes. The artist captured something in their expression they didn't know was showing. | On the back, a single word in Elvish: *"Interesting."* The artist is the Collector passenger ([[Random Passenger Encounters]] #7) — or someone who shares their interests. Either way, the player has been noticed by someone with resources. |
| 20 | **Nothing.** The pocket is empty, the drawer is bare, the compartment yields nothing but dust. | But a player with passive Perception 15+ feels it — the sensation of having just missed something. Someone was here first. Someone cleaned up. The absence is itself a message: *you are not the only one searching.* |

## Hebrew Notes

> **תוצאות חיפוש אקראיות** — הטילו d20 כששחקנים מחפשים גופות, תאים, מזוודות או בקתות. כל תוצאה היא פריט קטן עם סיפור קטן. אף אחד מהם לא קריטי לעלילה — כולם מוסיפים רבדים.

> **טיפ:** תנו לשחקנים למצוא 2-3 דברים במהלך הסשן. יותר מזה הופך לקטלוג. כל פריט צריך להרגיש כמו חלון קטן לעולם — לא כמו רשימת מכולת. אם שחקן מתחבר לפריט, תנו לו להפוך לחלק מהסיפור.
