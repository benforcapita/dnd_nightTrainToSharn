# Random Sharn Arrival Events

## How to Use
Roll d20 as the players step off The Firefly into [[Terminus Station]], or as they navigate their first hours in [[Sharn]]. These events bridge the train action and the city noir — establishing tone, introducing threads, and making Sharn feel alive, dangerous, and already watching.

Use 2-3 of these during the arrival sequence. Don't roll them all — pick what fits the energy of the session.

## Arrival Events (d20)

| d20 | Event                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| --- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **A hand on your shoulder.** A player feels a touch in the crowd — turns, and no one is there. But tucked into their pocket or belt is a folded note: *"We know what you're carrying. Meet us at the Broken Anvil at midnight. Come alone. — A friend."*                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
| 2   | **The journalist swarm.** Three reporters from different papers descend, shouting questions: *"Is it true there were casualties? Did you see the raiders? Was it House Orien negligence?"* One of them is from the *Korranberg Chronicle* — and they're not taking "no comment" for an answer.                                                                                                                                                           |
| 3   | **The cordon tightens.** Deneith guards form a checkpoint at the platform exit. Every passenger is questioned, every bag searched. The players must pass through — and the shard is in someone's pocket. DC 14 Sleight of Hand to hide it, DC 15 Deception to talk past the guards, or DC 14 Investigation to spot a gap in the cordon.                                                                                                                  |
| 4   | **A familiar face.** Captain Ash — if he survived — is being dragged away in manacles by two Deneith guards. He catches a player's eye. No words. Just a look. It could mean: *"Say nothing."* Or: *"I'll find you."* Or: *"Help me."*                                                                                                                                                                                                                   |
| 5   | **The pickpocket.** A grimy child bumps into a player and apologizes profusely. A moment later, the player notices something small is missing — a coin purse, a trinket, or (DC 14 Perception) nothing at all. The child stole something and *put it back* — with a tiny brass gear now tucked into the player's pocket. The gear is warm. It ticks.                                                                                                     |
| 6   | **Lady Elira's farewell.** Lady Elira, surrounded by Orien handlers, pauses as she passes the players. Her public smile doesn't waver, but her eyes hold: *"House Orien does not forget those who... assisted us. If you find yourselves in need of work, my office is in Upper Central. Ask for the Dragon's Rest enclave."* An invitation — or a leash.                                                                                                |
| 7   | **The body on the platform.** A covered stretcher is wheeled past. A hand slips out from under the sheet — wearing a ring the players recognize from the train. A passenger who didn't make it. A Deneith guard mutters: *"Heart failure. Happens."* The guard won't meet anyone's eyes.                                                                                                                                                                 |
| 8   | **The urchin's offer.** A street kid (halfling, barefoot, sharp-eyed) tugs at a player's sleeve. *"You're new. You're lost. You're carrying something heavy — I can smell it. Twenty silver and I'll show you a route out of the station that no guard watches. Five more and I'll tell you where to fence it."* The kid isn't lying. They know the station's every shadow.                                                                              |
| 9   | **A sending stone rings.** If the players picked up the sending stone from [[Random Search Results]] #11 — or if you want to introduce one — it vibrates. A voice, tinny and urgent: *"Don't stay in the station. They're already organizing a search party. Get to Lower Dura before nightfall. Trust no one in uniform."* The voice is unfamiliar. The warning is real.                                                                                |
| 10  | **The Orien inspector.** A House Orien official in a crisp uniform flags down the players. *"You were on the manifest. Honored guests. We'll need your statements for the incident report — just a formality. And of course, any... items you may have recovered from the attackers should be logged with security."* The official's smile doesn't reach their eyes. They know about the shard. They're fishing.                                         |
| 11  | **The crying mother.** A woman pushes through the crowd, wild-eyed, clutching a photograph. *"My daughter was on that train! Her name is Mira! Did you see her? Please — please, did anyone see her?"* Mira wasn't on the train. She died five years ago in the war. The mother has been coming to Terminus Station every day since, hoping for a different train, a different ending.                                                                   |
| 12  | **A watcher in the shadows.** A figure in a grey cloak stands motionless at the edge of the platform, watching the players. When noticed, they don't flee — they raise a finger to their lips, then melt into the crowd. A DC 16 Insight check: this wasn't a threat. It was a warning. Someone is already protecting the players — or marking them.                                                                                                     |
| 13  | **The courier's delivery.** A breathless messenger pushes through the crowd. *"[Player name]? Package for you."* A small box. Inside: a single copper coin, minted in Cyre before the Mourning, and a card that reads: *"Welcome to Sharn. The game begins at dawn. — The Aurum."*                                                                                                                                                                       |
| 14  | **City Watch checkpoint.** Two Watch officers — bored, overworked, underpaid — block the exit. *"Routine. Name, business in Sharn, place of residence."* They're not looking for the shard. They're not even paying attention. But one of them is wearing a ring with the Vol crest (see [[Random Search Results]] #15 reference). He doesn't know what it means. Someone gave it to him.                                                                |
| 15  | **The veteran's salute.** An old soldier — missing an arm, wearing a faded Brelish uniform — is begging near the station entrance. When the players pass, he straightens. Salutes with the arm that isn't there. *"Saerun Road. I remember you. You held. You held when everyone else ran."* He doesn't remember them — he says this to every veteran who passes. But the players don't know that.                                                       |
| 16  | **A skycoach accident.** A skycoach — one of Sharn's flying taxi boats — clips a tower and spirals down toward the platform. It crashes through the station's glass ceiling, showering the crowd in shards. DC 12 Dexterity save to avoid `1d6 slashing` from falling glass. In the chaos: a player notices someone using the distraction to slip a note to a Deneith guard.                                                                             |
| 17  | **Professor Nymm vanishes.** If Nymm survived, the players spot him hurrying through the crowd, clutching his notes. He sees them — freezes — and then ducks into a side passage marked *"Employees Only."* If they follow: he's gone. Only a faint smell of ozone and old books remains. And a single page torn from his notebook, fluttering to the ground: a sketch of a dragonmark that isn't one of the Twelve.                                     |
| 18  | **The Boromar greeting.** A well-dressed halfling in a green velvet coat appears at the players' side like they'd been there the whole time. *"Word travels fast in Sharn. Especially about interesting arrivals. The Boromar Clan extends its warmest welcome — and its most sincere advice: don't sell to the first bidder. We'll be in touch."* They're gone before anyone can respond.                                                               |
| 19  | **The shard reacts.** As the players step onto Sharn soil for the first time, the Siberys shard pulses — warm, then hot, then *singing* at a frequency only the carrier can hear. Every magewright and arcane-sensitive being within 100 feet pauses, looks around, feels something they can't name. The pulse lasts three seconds. Then silence. But those three seconds were noticed.                                                                  |
| 20  | **A dragon's shadow.** High above the station, something massive passes between the towers — too fast, too large for a skycoach. Its shadow sweeps across the platform. Everyone looks up. No one sees it. But for a heartbeat, the whole station falls silent. And a player with passive Perception 16+ hears a voice in their mind, ancient and calm: *"The shard is not yours. It is not anyone's. But it has chosen to travel with you. Be worthy."* |

## The First Night in Sharn (d8)

Roll or choose once the players find lodging:

| d8 | What Happens |
|----|-------------|
| 1 | Someone tries the door at 3am. They don't enter. They just... check that it's locked. |
| 2 | A message is slipped under the door: a time, a place, and the words *"Come alone or not at all."* |
| 3 | The innkeeper asks too many questions — but their prices are suspiciously low. |
| 4 | A player dreams of the shard. It speaks in a language they don't know but somehow understand. |
| 5 | A street musician outside plays a Cyran funeral hymn. The same one from the train. |
| 6 | The room next door is occupied by a Deneith guard from the train. They don't recognize the players. Yet. |
| 7 | A rat watches from the windowsill. Just a rat. Except it doesn't blink. |
| 8 | Nothing happens. The night is quiet. The bed is warm. And somehow, that's the most unsettling thing of all. |

## Hebrew Notes

> **אירועי הגעה לשארן** — הטילו d20 כשהשחקנים יורדים מהרכבת או בשעותיהם הראשונות בעיר. האירועים האלה מגשרים בין האקשן של הרכבת לנואר של העיר — מבססים טון, מציגים חוטי עלילה, וגורמים לשארן להרגיש חיה, מסוכנת, וכבר צופה.

> **טיפ:** השתמשו ב-2-3 מהאירועים במהלך הסצנה. אל תטילו את כולם — בחרו מה שמתאים לאנרגיה של הסשן. תנו לשארן להציף אותם. העיר אנכית, אפלה, ותמיד — תמיד — מישהו מסתכל.
