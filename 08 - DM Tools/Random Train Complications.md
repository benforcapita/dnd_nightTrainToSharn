# Random Train Complications

## How to Use
Roll d12 when something goes wrong beyond the planned heist — or when player actions create unexpected chaos. These stack with the [[Environmental Hazards]] table (which fires at initiative count 20). Use complications when:
- A player rolls a natural 1 on a significant check
- The fight spills into a new car
- You need to raise stakes mid-combat
- The players try something reckless and it goes sideways

## Complications (d12)

| d12 | Complication                                                                                                                                           | Effect                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| --- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **Elemental Breach.** The bound elemental in the engine partially escapes. A wave of freezing mist rolls through the train.                            | All creatures within the affected car take `1d4 cold damage` at the start of their turn for `1d4` rounds. Visibility reduced to 10 feet. The mist whispers in Primordial.                                                                                                                                                                                     |
| 2   | **Coupling Failure.** A connecting platform between two cars buckles and begins to tear.                                                               | The gap between cars widens by 5 feet. Jumping across now requires DC 15 Athletics. At the end of `1d4` rounds, the coupling snaps entirely — anyone on the platform must make DC 14 Dexterity save or fall from the train (`6d6 bludgeoning + 2d6 lightning`).                                                                                               |
| 3   | **Cargo Shift.** A stack of crates, furniture, or luggage breaks loose and slides across the car.                                                      | A 15-foot line of difficult terrain appears. Creatures in the path make DC 13 Dexterity save or take `2d6 bludgeoning` and be knocked prone.                                                                                                                                                                                                                  |
| 4   | **Spell Backlash.** The shard inside the statue pulses in response to magic being cast nearby.                                                         | All active spells within 60 feet of the cargo car are affected: concentration saves are at disadvantage for one round. Any spell cast in the next round triggers a wild magic surge (roll on any wild magic table, or use: `1d6` force damage to caster, `1d4` lightning damage to everyone within 10 feet).                                                  |
| 5   | **Passenger Panic.** A group of civilians breaks from hiding and tries to run — directly through the combat zone.                                      | `2d4` commoners enter the battlefield. They are frightened, uncontrollable, and provide half cover to anyone behind them. Any attack that misses a target within 5 feet of a civilian hits the civilian instead.                                                                                                                                              |
| 6   | **The Marshal's Ultimatum.** Marshal Serik, if alive, decides the situation is out of control. He shouts: *"Seal the cars! No one gets to the cargo!"* | Heavy arcane doors begin closing between cars at initiative count 20. Players have one round to move before their current car is sealed (DC 18 Thieves' Tools or DC 16 Arcana to reopen). Anyone caught in a closing door takes `2d6 force damage` and is restrained (DC 15 Strength to break free).                                                          |
| 7   | **Roof Collapse.** A section of the ceiling gives way — either from roof combat or structural stress.                                                  | A 10-foot radius area becomes impassable rubble. Creatures underneath make DC 14 Dexterity save: success = move to nearest safe square, failure = `3d6 bludgeoning` and restrained under debris (DC 15 Athletics to escape).                                                                                                                                  |
| 8   | **Fire in the Lounge.** An overturned lamp, a stray firebolt, or the hearth elemental breaking containment ignites furnishings.                        | Fire spreads `1d4` squares per round. Any creature starting its turn in a burning square takes `1d6 fire damage`. Smoke grants light obscurement after 2 rounds. The fire elemental in the hearth (if present) may break free as a hostile creature (use **Fire Snake** statblock or equivalent CR 1 elemental).                                              |
| 9   | **Deneith Reinforcements.** A second squad of Deneith guards was stationed in the security car. They enter the fight — but whose side are they on?     | `1d4+1` Deneith Guards (see [[Deneith Guard Statblocks]]) arrive in `1d3` rounds. Roll d6: 1-3 = they fight the raiders, 4-5 = they try to arrest everyone, 6 = they've been bought by Captain Ash and turn on the Marshal.                                                                                                                                   |
| 10  | **Raider Backup.** Captain Ash's crew had a second team on standby. They board from the rear.                                                          | `1d4` Raiders (see [[Train Raider Statblocks]]) enter from the observation car in `1d3` rounds. They're fresh, coordinated, and going straight for the cargo car.                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| 11  | **Statue Activation.** The violence near the cargo car wakes something in the Dhakaani statue. It wasn't just a container — it was a guardian.         | The statue animates partially: spectral Dhakaani runes pulse along its surface. All creatures within 30 feet make DC 13 Wisdom save or be frightened for 1 minute (save ends at end of turn). The statue can't move or attack, but it *sees* — and it will remember the players.                                                                              |
| 12  | **The Train Speeds Up.** Whether through damage to the controls or the elemental's panic, the train accelerates dangerously.                           | The train's speed doubles. All Dexterity saves related to train movement are at disadvantage. The wind outside becomes a roar. If the train reaches a curve at this speed (3 rounds), everyone makes DC 15 Dexterity save or falls prone and slides `2d4 x 5 feet`. If the speed isn't addressed within 6 rounds, the train will derail at the next junction. |

## Hebrew Notes

> **סיבוכי רכבת אקראיים** — הטילו d12 כשמשהו משתבש מעבר לשוד המתוכנן, כששחקן מטיל 1 טבעי, או כשמגיע רגע להעלות את ההימור. סיבוכים אלו נערמים על מפגעי הסביבה.

> **טיפ:** סיבוך אחד באמצע הקרב משנה הכל. שניים זה תוהו ובוהו. אל תערמו יותר מדי — תנו לשחקנים רגעים לנשום בין אסון לאסון. זכרו: הרכבת היא דמות, אבל לא הדמות הראשית. השחקנים כן.
