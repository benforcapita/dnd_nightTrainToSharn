# Random Train Events

## How to Use
Roll d20 once per "quiet" stretch of the journey (Scenes 2-3), or whenever pacing needs a nudge. These are flavor events — small moments that build atmosphere, offer roleplay hooks, or plant seeds. Most don't demand combat.

## The Journey (d20)

| d20 | Event                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| --- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | **The hum stops.** For three heartbeats, the conductor stones go silent. Every passenger feels it — a pressure lifting they didn't know they were carrying. Then it resumes, slightly louder than before.                                                                                                                  |
| 2   | **Someone is watching.** A passenger in a grey coat keeps glancing at the players, then looking away. If confronted, they stammer: *"Sorry — you look like someone I knew. Someone who didn't come back."*                                                                                                                 |
| 3   | **The tea is wrong.** A player's drink tastes faintly of copper and something floral. A Perception check (DC 14) reveals a crew attendant slipping a small vial back into their pocket. It's not poison — it's a mild truth serum (DC 12 Con save or disadvantage on Deception for 1 hour). Someone is testing the guests. |
| 4   | **A child is crying.** A young girl, maybe 7, has lost her stuffed owlbear. It's wedged behind a seat in the observation car. If returned: her mother, a war widow, quietly thanks them. *"It's all she has left of him."* The owlbear has a small pocket sewn inside — contains a folded letter the mother never read.    |
| 5   | **Lightning on the horizon.** A storm brews in the distance — purple-white lightning that doesn't touch the ground. Professor Nymm, if nearby, mutters: *"That's not weather. That's a manifest zone. Someone's been opening doors they shouldn't."*                                                                       |
| 6   | **The wrong stop.** The train slows unexpectedly near a ruined waystation. No announcement. The conductor stones flicker. After two tense minutes, speed resumes. A crew member later shrugs: *"Happens sometimes. The elemental gets... curious."*                                                                        |
| 7   | **A card game invitation.** Three off-duty Deneith guards invite a player to a game of Three-Dragon Ante. Stakes are low (5 sp), but the conversation is valuable — they gossip about the cargo, the Marshal's temper, and a "weird little scholar" who tried to bribe his way into the cargo car.                         |
| 8   | **Something moves outside.** Through the window, a player spots a shape keeping pace with the train — large, winged, silhouetted against the clouds. It banks away before anyone else sees. A wyvern? A griffon? Something else? It won't appear again... this session.                                                    |
| 9   | **The lights flicker in sequence.** Not random — a pattern. Three short. Three long. Three short. Someone on this train is signaling. An Arcana check (DC 15) identifies it as an old military distress code from the Last War.                                                                                            |
| 10  | **A noble complains.** Loudly. About the "charity cases" aboard. About the smell. About the war. If the players don't intervene, a Deneith guard quietly escorts the noble away. If they do intervene, the noble remembers their faces — for better or worse.                                                              |
| 11  | **The string quartet plays a Cyran funeral hymn.** An odd choice for a celebratory voyage. One violinist has tears streaming down her face. She won't explain. She simply says: *"It felt right."*                                                                                                                         |
| 12  | **A locked compartment clicks open.** In the passenger car hallway, a door that was firmly shut now stands slightly ajar. Inside: an empty cabin, neatly made. On the pillow: a single fresh flower — a variety that doesn't grow in this season.                                                                          |
| 13  | **Conductor stone inspection.** Two hooded Orien engineers walk the length of the train, pressing copper rods against the walls and listening. They ignore all questions. One of them has burns on their hands — old ones, star-shaped.                                                                                    |
| 14  | **A toast.** A boisterous dwarf merchant stands and raises his glass: *"To the ones who fought! To the ones who fell! And to the bastards who sent them — may they choke on their profits!"* The dining car goes dead silent. Lady Elira does not applaud.                                                                 |
| 15  | **The wind speaks.** Near the connecting platform between cars, the wind howls through a gap with an almost-voice — words in a language no one recognizes. An Arcana or History check (DC 16) suggests it might be Old Dhakaani, carried from somewhere far away.                                                          |
| 16  | **A crew attendant is crying in a supply closet.** Soft, stifled sobs. If discovered, she begs the player not to tell anyone. *"I heard something in the engine room. It spoke my name. It knew my brother's name. He died at Vathirond. How could it know?"*                                                              |
| 17  | **A small fire.** A candle tips in the lounge. Panic for thirty seconds before it's stamped out. But in those thirty seconds, a player notices a passenger who didn't react at all — just watched the flames with something like hunger. That passenger won't be on the train after the heist.                             |
| 18  | **The rain begins.** Not the gentle rain of Thronehold Station. This is a deluge — hammering the roof, streaming down windows, turning the world outside into grey static. The train feels smaller. More isolated. More vulnerable.                                                                                        |
| 19  | **A gift.** An anonymous package appears at a player's seat — a small box wrapped in silver paper. Inside: a single dragonmark focus crystal, warm to the touch, carved with the Mark of Sentinel. No note. No explanation. Deneith is watching.                                                                           |
| 20  | **The shard stirs.** Even though the players haven't reached the cargo car yet, a player with passive Perception 15+ feels a pulse — not a sound, not a vibration, but a *pull* in their chest toward the rear of the train. It passes after a moment. It leaves a metallic taste in their mouth.                          |

## Hebrew Notes

> **אירועי רכבת אקראיים** — הטילו d20 במהלך הקטעים השקטים של המסע. כל אירוע הוא זעיר, אך בונה אווירה, נותן ווים למשחק תפקידים, או שותל זרעים לעתיד. רובם אינם דורשים קרב. הרכבת חיה — היא נושמת, היא רואה, היא לוחשת.

> **טיפ:** בחרו 3-4 אירועים מראש ופזרו אותם לאורך הסשן. אל תטילו ליד השולחן לכל אירוע — זה שובר קצב. השתמשו בטבלה בין הסשנים כדי להכין, או הטילו פעם אחת באמצע הסשן להפתעה.
