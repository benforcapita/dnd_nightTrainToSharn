# Sharn Side Quests — Pub Fund Edition

## Overview
Sessions 2 and 3 are about rebuilding the Dragon's Rest and exploring Sharn. The party needs gold — lots of it — for taxes (100 gp), licenses (75 gp), renovations (up to 1,000 gp), and hiring staff. These quests let them earn money while discovering the city's geography, politics, and secrets.

Each quest includes a **payout**, a **ward/location** it reveals, a **contact** who may recur, and optional **faction hooks** that tie into the larger campaign.

## The Sharn Inquisitive Classifieds — Quest Board

When the party asks "where can we find work?", they discover the **Sharn Inquisitive** runs a classified section in every edition. Bounties, jobs, and odd requests posted by citizens. This is the legitimate quest board — the Watch looks the other way at most of it.

### Tier 1: Street Level (50-75 gp payout)
These jobs get the party's boots on the ground in different wards.

#### The Missing Mule — 60 gp
**Location:** Bazaar → Middle Dura → Lower Tavick's Landing  
**Contact:** Olarid, a dwarf merchant whose cargo mule vanished between Bazaar and Tavick's. The mule was carrying a crate of preserved Aundairian wine worth triple the stated reward.  
**The Job:** Track the mule through three wards. Witnesses saw a goblin leading it toward the lower levels.  
**The Truth:** A small Daask crew snatched it — not for the wine, but because the crate was a dead drop containing a message for Niho Koi. If the party recovers it, they find a note mentioning "the Firefly shard" before Daask arrives to reclaim it.  
**Faction Hook:** Early Daask foreshadowing. The party learns Daask is watching them.

#### The Bell Tower Haunting — 50 gp
**Location:** A derelict temple in North Market (Lower Northedge)  
**Contact:** Father Moras, an elderly priest of the Sovereign Host. The temple's bell rings at midnight — but no one is pulling the rope. The shifter community nearby is spooked.  
**The Job:** Investigate the haunting. Discover the cause.  
**The Truth:** A young shifter named **Kel** has been sneaking in to ring the bell as a memorial for his brother, killed in the Last War. He's not a ghost — he's just grieving. The party can turn him in (full reward, but the shifters hate them), help him find closure (half reward, but Leara notices their compassion), or expose the temple's secret: Father Moras was a war profiteer who sold bad medicine to both sides.  
**Faction Hook:** Leara (Tyrants) is watching. If the party shows kindness to the shifters, her approach in Session 3 is warmer.

#### The Cannith Courier — 75 gp
**Location:** Bazaar → The Cogs (Ashblack)  
**Contact:** A House Cannith clerk who needs a prototype delivered to an overseer in the Cogs. "Don't open the box. Don't shake the box. Don't even look at the box too hard."  
**The Job:** Descend into the Cogs for the first time. Navigate industrial hazards, find the overseer.  
**The Truth:** The box contains an experimental weapons component — a miniature conductor stone that's unstable. Every time the party takes damage, roll a d20. On a 1, the stone discharges, dealing 2d6 lightning damage in a 10-foot radius. The overseer is a warforged named **Cutter** who works for Merrix d'Cannith directly. Cutter mentions in passing that they've "heard about that train business — bad luck, that."  
**Faction Hook:** Introduction to the Cogs (Daask territory), Cannith politics, and a recurring NPC (Cutter).

#### The Soggy Sahuagin — 55 gp
**Location:** Ships' Towers (Lower Tavick's Landing), then Dagger River docks  
**Contact:** A fishmonger named Pella whose shop is being harassed by a sahuagin who crawls up from the river every night to steal her best catch.  
**The Job:** Catch or drive off the sahuagin.  
**The Truth:** The sahuagin — **Old Saltbreath** — isn't stealing fish. He's looking for something dropped in the river: a Tyrant artifact that fell during a chase. If the party talks to him instead of fighting, he offers them a deal: help him find the artifact, and he'll tell them who really runs the Lower Northedge docks. The answer: the Tyrants, through a network of shifter dockworkers.  
**Faction Hook:** Intro to the Tyrants' dock operations. Old Saltbreath becomes a recurring contact if treated well.

### Tier 2: Rising Reputation (100-200 gp payout)
These jobs require more skill, involve faction politics, and pay enough to cover a renovation phase.

#### The Missing Heir — 150 gp
**Location:** Skyway (Upper Central) → various wards  
**Contact:** Lady Elara ir'Talan, a minor noble whose 19-year-old son vanished from a Skyway party three nights ago. The Sharn Watch won't help — "he's an adult, he probably just ran off." She disagrees.  
**The Job:** Find the son, bring him home.  
**The Truth:** The son, **Theran**, has joined House Tarkanan. He manifested an aberrant dragonmark at the party and fled before his family could turn him in to the "purification" clinics. He's hiding in Shae Lias, terrified and furious. The party can drag him home (Lady Elara pays double, but she'll send him to a clinic that "treats" aberrant marks — which means lobotomy), convince him to return on his own terms, or help him disappear.  
**Faction Hook:** Deep intro to House Tarkanan's recruitment methods and the moral horror of the aberrant-mark purges. Oleander Nightbloom notices the party's involvement.

#### The Boromar Ledger — 200 gp
**Location:** Bazaar → Firelight → Lower Menthis  
**Contact:** A anonymous letter slipped under the Dragon's Rest door. "The Boromar Clan's tax records for the last six months are kept in a counting-house in Firelight. Retrieve them. We pay 200 gp. No questions asked. — A Friend."  
**The Job:** Break into the counting-house and steal the ledger.  
**The Truth:** The "Friend" is **Daask** — Niho Koi wants the ledger to identify Boromar weaknesses. The counting-house is guarded by 4 halfling thugs and a clawfoot dinosaur. The ledger reveals that **Emmek d'Ghallanda** owes the Boromar 500 gp and is three months delinquent — information the party can use against their rival. If the party completes the job, Daask considers them useful. If they refuse, Niho Koi notes their "lack of ambition."  
**Faction Hook:** Early Daask cooperation offer. Emmek's debt exposed. The party gets leverage.

#### The Morgrave Acquisition — 175 gp
**Location:** Morgrave University (Upper Menthis) → The Depths (Old Sharn ruins)  
**Contact:** Professor Talia ir'Wynarn, a Morgrave archaeologist who needs "discreet escorts" for an expedition into the Old Sharn ruins beneath the university.  
**The Job:** Protect the professor and her team while they excavate a newly discovered chamber.  
**The Truth:** The chamber is a Dhakaani outpost — a minor relay station, not Duur'Sharaat, but connected to the same network. The walls are covered in runes that match the party's shard's glow. One rune, when touched, activates a recording: a goblin voice saying *"The Keys are scattered. The Forge-Warden waits. Do not let the dragon-marked find the deep forge."* Professor Talia copies the runes for her research but doesn't understand their significance. The party does.  
**Faction Hook:** First concrete link between the shard and a physical Dhakaani site. The shard glows warmly throughout the chamber. The Morgrave connection may lead to Professor Nymm later.

#### The Skycoach Circuit — 100 gp (repeatable)
**Location:** Various skycoach docks across Sharn  
**Contact:** A grizzled skycoach pilot named **Vance** who needs "extra hands" for a dangerous route.  
**The Job:** Ride shotgun on a skycoach making deliveries through hostile territory.  
**The Truth:** The route passes through Daask airspace (or Boromar territory, or Tyrant surveillance). Each run has a 50% chance of an aerial encounter — harpies, winged kobolds, or a rival skycoach. Vance teaches the party how Sharn's vertical geography works: which wards are safe, which bridges are neutral, which towers are controlled by which faction.  
**Faction Hook:** Vance is a neutral contact who knows all the smugglers' routes. He becomes a recurring transport asset if befriended.

#### The Golden Hearth Sabotage — 100 gp (but costs something else)
**Location:** The Golden Hearth (Bazaar)  
**Contact:** A halfling named **Miri Boromar** (distant cousin to the main family) who wants Emmek's pub to "have an accident."  
**The Job:** Sabotage the Golden Hearth's kitchen inspection. Plant vermin. Spoil the ale. Make Emmek look bad.  
**The Truth:** Miri is working for the Boromar, who want Emmek to default on his debt so they can seize his pub. If the party does this, they eliminate their rival — but they've committed a crime for the Boromar, who now own a piece of them. Emmek will eventually learn who sabotaged him.  
**Faction Hook:** Moral test. Easy money, but at what cost? The Boromar now have leverage.

## The Dhakaani Echoes (Optional — Drop In Anywhere)

The shard reacts to certain locations around Sharn. These are not quests per se, but environmental storytelling moments the DM can insert during any outing.

| Location | Shard Reaction | What It Reveals |
|---|---|---|
| **Dava Gate (Middle Dura)** | Warm hum, faint glow | This gate was built over a Dhakaani waystation. The shard briefly shows the original goblin structure beneath the human architecture. |
| **The Broken Anvil (Blackbones, Cogs)** | Sharp pulse, brief vision | A Dhakaani forge once stood here. The shard shows a goblin smith shaping a Key — one of the seven. This Key is no longer here. |
| **A random bridge (any ward)** | Cold silence | The shard goes dead. Investigation reveals the bridge stones contain trace metals from the Mourning — debris imported from Cyre after the disaster. The shard *grieves*. |
| **The City of the Dead cliffs** | Strongest reaction yet — almost painful | Duur'Sharaat's entrance (Spring season) is near. The shard is pulled toward the cliffs like a compass needle. |

## DM Notes

- **Spread the quests across wards.** The party should see as much of Sharn as possible before the faction war consumes their attention.
- **Every quest plants a seed.** The missing mule mentions the Firefly. The Bell Tower ties to the Tyrants. The Cannith Courier introduces the Cogs. Nothing should be random — everything connects.
- **Let them feel the financial pressure.** The taxes, the guild fees, the renovation costs — these are real constraints. Quests aren't optional; they're survival. This makes every gold piece feel earned.
- **Moral choices matter.** The sabotage job, the missing heir, the shady work — these test who the party is. Are they heroes, mercenaries, or something in between?
- **Track which quests they take and how they handle them.** Faction attitudes shift accordingly. A party that helps the shifters gets a warmer Leara. A party that works for Daask gets colder Deneith stares.
