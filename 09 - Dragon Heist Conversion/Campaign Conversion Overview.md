# Campaign Conversion — Waterdeep: Dragon Heist → Night Train to Sharn

## What This Is
This document maps **Waterdeep: Dragon Heist** (via the Eberron conversion guide *Sharn Schema Scandal*) onto the existing Night Train to Sharn campaign. Session 1 — "The Firefly" — is unchanged. Everything from Session 2 onward builds the Dragon Heist faction-war structure on top of the existing Eberron-noir foundation.

## The Core Merge

| Dragon Heist / Schema Scandal | Night Train to Sharn |
|---|---|
| **Forgotten Relics** (E:RftLW intro) | **Session 1: The Firefly** — the train heist introduces the party, the shard, the mystery, and the tone |
| **Trollskull Manor / Bazaar Belltower** | **The Dragon's Rest** — a bridge pub spanning two towers, gifted to the party as compensation/hush money after The Firefly |
| **Volo** | **Professor Nymm** (owes the party — or wants to study the shard) or **Lady Elira ir'Orien** (gratitude/guilt) |
| **The Stone of Golorr** | **The Siberys Shard** — the shard is the key. When attuned, it reveals the location of **Duur'Sharaat**, the lost Dhakaani workshop beneath Sharn |
| **The Vault of Dragons (500k gp)** | **Duur'Sharaat** — a legendary Dhakaani creation forge and schema archive sealed beneath Old Sharn. Inside: the lost **Storm Spire Schema**, worth half a million galifars to Merrix d'Cannith — and worth a kingdom to everyone else |
| **4 Villains** (Xanathar, Cassalanters, Jarlaxle, Manshoon) | **4 Sharn Syndicates** — Boromar Clan, Daask, House Tarkanan, The Tyrants — each with their own plan for the schema |
| **Factions** (Harpers, Lords' Alliance, Zhentarim, etc.) | **8+ Eberron Factions** — the 4 syndicates PLUS the user's existing factions: House Orien, House Deneith, The Twelve, The Aurum, The Raiders |

## Key Structural Decisions

### 1. The Shard IS the Map/Key
The Siberys shard the party stole from the train is not just a valuable dragonshard. When properly attuned, it reveals a map to **Duur'Sharaat**, a Dhakaani workshop buried beneath Old Sharn that contains schemas for weapons of mass creation — including the legendary **Storm Spire**, a device capable of replicating or controlling the Mourning itself.

### 2. The Pub Is a Bridge
Instead of Trollskull Manor or a bell tower, the party receives **The Dragon's Rest** — a tavern built into one of Sharn's countless bridges spanning two towers. It's a fixer-upper with history, mystery, and enormous potential. It becomes their base, their income source, their sanctuary, and their trap.

### 3. ALL Factions Compete
The campaign uses every faction. Not just the 4 syndicates from Schema Scandal — House Orien wants the shard back quietly, Deneith has contracts to fulfill, the Aurum wants leverage, the Twelve wants research material, and the Raiders (if Ash survived) want to expose House crimes. The party sits at the center of a 9-faction tug-of-war.

### 4. Four Seasons, Four Villains, One Campaign
Each syndicate has its own plan to claim the schema. Select ONE as the main antagonist, but the other three remain active — they don't vanish. They harass, negotiate, betray, and ally depending on player choices. The "season" (which syndicate reaches the finish line first) determines the encounter flavor and finale location.

## The Shard's True Nature

The DM should reveal this gradually:

- **Session 1:** The shard is valuable. It's the heist target.
- **Session 2:** The shard hums. It's warm. The dream shows a Dhakaani workshop.
- **Session 3:** The shard reacts near the sealed cellar door. Near dragonmarks. Near aberrants.
- **Session 4:** The shard reacts to the explosion — it *recognizes* something. Reveals itself as a Key. Full attunement begins.
- **Session 5-6:** Attunement reveals fragmented visions: a workshop, ancient goblin artificers, a machine humming with power. Sense of direction toward the Depths.
- **Session 7-8:** Full attunement reveals the map to Duur'Sharaat and the shard's name: **"The Key of the Last Word"** — a phrase from the Draconic Prophecy.
- **Session 9-10:** Inside Duur'Sharaat, the shard opens the final vault. It is a Prophecy fragment crystallized — a warning, a blueprint, and a choice.

## The Storm Spire Schema

What's actually in the vault:

- A complete Dhakaani schema for a device that could replicate — or *counter* — the Mourning
- House Cannith has been searching for this for decades; Merrix d'Cannith will pay 500,000 gp
- The Boromar Clan wants to sell it to the highest bidder
- Daask wants it to arm Droaam for conquest
- House Tarkanan wants to use it to destroy the dragonmarked houses
- The Tyrants want to auction it to multiple buyers simultaneously — chaos as currency
- The Twelve wants to study and weaponize it
- The Aurum wants to control who has it — and profit from that control
- The Lord of Blades wants it to create an army of warforged that never stops
- The Emerald Claw wants it as a tool of Karrnathi resurrection

## Campaign Grandeur: Making It Epic

- **All villains are active.** Even if only one reaches Duur'Sharaat, the others are scheming, fighting, and reacting throughout the entire campaign.
- **Faction alliances are fluid.** The Boromar Clan might help the party against Daask one week and betray them the next.
- **The shard is alive.** It learns. It reacts. It shows the party things they might not want to see.
- **The stakes are apocalyptic.** The Storm Spire schema isn't just money — it's a tool that could cause a second Mourning, or prevent one.
- **Player choices reshape Sharn.** Who gets the schema (or who the party destroys it in front of) determines the future of the city, the houses, and possibly the continent.
