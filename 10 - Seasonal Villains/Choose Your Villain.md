# Choose Your Villain — The Four Seasons

## Overview
One of the four Sharn crime syndicates will be the **primary antagonist** of the Dragon Heist arc (Sessions 4-10). The others remain active but secondary — they may ally with or oppose the party depending on circumstances.

Choose your main villain based on:
- **Player preference:** Which faction did they engage with most in Sessions 2-3?
- **Tone:** Which flavor of villainy suits your table?
- **Complexity:** How much political intrigue do you want?

---

## Spring: The Boromar Clan
**Villain:** The Rake (spymaster and enforcer)  
**Tone:** Crime thriller. Money, leverage, and betrayal.  
**Complexity:** High. The Boromar are embedded in Sharn's politics. Every door the party opens leads to three more.

**The Rake's Plan:** Sell the schema to the highest bidder. The Rake is simultaneously negotiating with Karrnath, Droaam, and the Lord of Blades — whoever pays first gets the Storm Spire.

**Villain Personality:** The Rake is charming, patient, and utterly ruthless. They view violence as a failure of imagination. They'll offer the party a deal before fighting them — and they'll mean every word of it. But they'll also have three backup plans for when the deal falls through.

**Encounter Chain Flavor:** City-spanning chase through Bazaar, skycoach battles, high-society infiltration at the Aurora Gallery, a heist at the Cloud Dragon restaurant in Skyway.

**Finale:** The King of Fire nightclub in Firelight. Multi-level combat through a Boromar stronghold disguised as entertainment.

**Weather:** Heavy rain. Disadvantage on sight/hearing. Fog. Visibility 60 ft.

---

## Summer: The Tyrants
**Villain:** The Faceless Council (changeling/doppelganger leaders)  
**Tone:** Paranoia thriller. Who is real? Who is a Tyrant? Can you trust your own reflection?  
**Complexity:** Extreme. The Tyrants are everywhere. The party will question every NPC they meet for the rest of the campaign.

**The Tyrants' Plan:** Auction the schema to *all* buyers simultaneously — Karrnath, Droaam, the Houses, the Aurum, everyone. Let them destroy each other. Profit from every side of the war.

**Villain Personality:** The Faceless Council never appears directly. Their agents are everywhere. The villain the party fights is the *organization*, not any individual — though Leara and the Ringmaster serve as faces for the confrontation.

**Encounter Chain Flavor:** Circus infiltration, chimera chase through Sharn, cryptic riddles, shapeshifter paranoia, a theater where the audience is the enemy.

**Finale:** The Diamond Theater in Lower Menthis. The party must find the real artifact (and the real villain) in a building full of shapeshifters — including themselves (mirror duplicates, doppelgangers of party members).

**Weather:** Heat wave. Tempers flare. DC 12 Wisdom saves to avoid violent impulses in social encounters.

---

## Autumn: House Tarkanan
**Villain:** Oleander Nightbloom (drow aberrant-mark assassin)  
**Tone:** Revenge tragedy. Terrorism, trauma, and the cost of generational violence.  
**Complexity:** Medium-High. Tarkanan's cause is sympathetic. Their methods are not. The party must decide where the line is.

**Oleander's Plan:** Build a weapon that neutralizes dragonmarks — all of them. Use the Storm Spire schema to create an anti-mark field that renders every dragonmarked house powerless. Then: retribution for centuries of purges.

**Villain Personality:** Oleander is calm, cultured, and utterly sincere. He's not a cackling villain — he's a trauma survivor who has dedicated his life to a cause. He'll explain his reasoning to the party. He'll offer them a place at his side. And he'll poison their drinks while offering it.

**Encounter Chain Flavor:** Poisoned cocktails, mirrored portals, abandoned Cannith facilities, the ghosts of purged aberrant-mark bearers, a final confrontation in a temple of trauma.

**Finale:** Gorgon's Run — an abandoned Cannith forge beneath Sharn. Tarkanan is already testing the anti-mark field on captured House heirs. The party must stop a massacre — or decide it's justified.

**Weather:** Cool winds. Whispers on the breeze. Everbright lanterns flicker from aberrant magic interference.

---

## Winter: Daask
**Villain:** Niho Koi (oni mage, Droaam's agent)  
**Tone:** Monster movie. Overwhelming force. The fear of being hunted.  
**Complexity:** Low-Medium. Daask is direct. They don't scheme — they smash. But the politics of Droaam (the Daughters of Sora Kell) add layers.

**Niho Koi's Plan:** Arm Droaam's monster legions for continental conquest. The Storm Spire, in Droaam's hands, becomes a weapon that can level cities. Niho Koi dreams of the day the Five Nations bow to the Daughters.

**Villain Personality:** Niho Koi is ancient, powerful, and patient in the way that only immortals are. They shapeshift freely, appearing as anything from a beautiful half-elf to a child to their true oni form. They respect strength. They despise weakness. They're building the Storm Spire *right now* — the party is racing a clock they didn't know was ticking.

**Encounter Chain Flavor:** Descent into the Cogs, monster combat, prison break, temple negotiation, skycoach battle in a snowstorm, industrial horror as the Storm Spire takes shape.

**Finale:** Beneath the Cogs, in Daask's industrial territory. The Storm Spire is already half-built. The party must fight through a monster army and destroy the weapon before it activates.

**Weather:** Heavy snow. Airship traffic increased. Everbright lanterns doubled for Long Shadows season. Cold damage risks.

## Secondary Villains
Even when one syndicate is the main villain, the others are active:

- **Boromar (if not main):** Corrin Delmaco helps the party — for a price. The Clan is happy to see a rival syndicate destroyed. But Boromar will claim the schema if the party seems weak.
- **Daask (if not main):** Monstrous interference. Harpies attack during chases. Trolls block bridges. Daask doesn't care who wins — they want the schema and they want it now.
- **Tarkanan (if not main):** Oleander offers intelligence about the main villain. His price: help with a "small matter" — assassinating a dragonmarked house heir. The party must decide if using Tarkanan is worth the blood on their hands.
- **Tyrants (if not main):** Leara sells information. Everything has a price. The Tyrants play both sides — and every side thinks they're the only one getting Tyrant intelligence.

---

## The Other Factions
The user's existing factions are also active throughout:

- **House Orien:** Wants the shard back quietly. Lady Elira's gratitude has limits.
- **House Deneith:** Marshal Serik is hunting the truth — and his superiors are hunting the party.
- **The Twelve:** Cipher is watching. Always. The shard is a research opportunity they won't ignore.
- **The Aurum:** Lord Vex offers to buy the schema when the party finds it. He'll even let them name their price. He's also bribing their staff.
- **The Raiders:** Ash (if alive) knows something about the Dhakaani that no one else does. He's willing to share — if the party helps him first.
- **The Lord of Blades (distant):** Not yet directly involved. But warforged NPCs are reporting to him. He's aware of the shard. He's aware of Duur'Sharaat. He's waiting.
- **The Emerald Claw (distant):** Keeper Malachi has agents in Sharn. They're watching the faction war with professional interest. Undead don't rush.

## Villain Encounter Chain Reference
For detailed encounter chains, refer to the Sharn Schema Scandal conversion guide PDF, which provides full stat blocks, maps, and encounter details for each season's chain. The chain structure is:

1. **Find the courier** (Hrabbaz / Braala / Leara / Nightshade)
2. **Follow the trail** (spellshard map, witness interviews, faction contacts)
3. **The interception** (another faction steals/intercepts the artifact)
4. **The chase** (skycoach battle, chimera chase, industrial pursuit)
5. **The setpiece location** (auction house, theater, Cannith facility, temple)
6. **The finale** (the syndicate's headquarters)
7. **The revelation** (attuning to the artifact reveals Duur'Sharaat's entrance)

## DM Notes
- The seasonal structure means you can run this campaign four different ways. If your group replays, choose a different season.
- The weather effects are atmospheric, not punishing. Use them for flavor, not mechanical cruelty.
- Each villain should feel *right* in their own way. The party should sometimes wonder if they're on the wrong side.
- The secondary factions' interference keeps the campaign dynamic. Even in the "final" confrontation, a third party crashing in keeps things unpredictable.
